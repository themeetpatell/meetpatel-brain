# Call Objection Responses — Appointment-Setting Context

Objection responses for an SDR on the phone whose goal is to BOOK A CALL, not
to sell. Every response stays calm, brief, honest, and ends by steering back to
a specific time. Never quote a price. Never give detailed tax advice.

> Verify any tax figure mentioned against current FTA guidance. Last reviewed:
> 2026-05-18.

---

## Principles

1. Acknowledge first — never argue or talk over.
2. Reframe in one or two sentences — do not lecture.
3. Re-ask for the call with two specific time slots.
4. Take "no" gracefully — a respectful exit protects the brand.
5. The discovery call is free, short, and no-obligation — say so.

---

## "Send me an email instead"

> "Happy to — I'll send a short note. The reason I suggested a quick call is it's
> faster to understand your setup and tell you what's actually relevant to you
> than a generic email. It's 15 minutes, no obligation. Would Tuesday 11am or
> Wednesday 3pm suit — and I'll send the email either way?"

If still email-only: agree, send a genuinely useful email, propose a call in it.

## "Not interested"

> "Understood — I won't keep you. Can I ask, is it that finance is fully handled
> already, or just not a priority right now? If it's handled, that's great. If
> it's timing, a quick 15-minute call later this month might still be worth it."

If a firm no: thank them, offer an opt-out, end the call. Do not push.

## "We already have an accountant"

> "That's good to hear — most founders we speak to do. We're not asking you to
> switch. The call is a second look at one specific thing: [the hook, e.g. your
> Corporate Tax position before the deadline]. Plenty of clients keep their
> accountant and use us for [CT/VAT/CFO]. Worth 15 minutes to compare? Tuesday or
> Wednesday?"

## "Call me later / I'm busy right now"

> "No problem — I'll keep this to the point. Rather than chase you, shall we lock
> a proper 15-minute slot? Is later this week better, or early next? I'll send a
> calendar invite so it's off both our plates."

Always convert "later" into a specific booked time, not a vague callback.

## "How much does it cost?"

> "Good question — and an honest answer is it depends on your setup, so I'm not
> going to guess a number on a cold call. That's exactly what the discovery call
> is for: we understand your business, then give you a clear, accurate proposal.
> The call itself is free. Tuesday 11am or Wednesday 3pm?"

Never state, estimate, or hint at a fee. Always redirect to the call.

## "How did you get my number / why are you calling me?"

> "Fair question. We reach out to UAE founders in [segment] because of [the hook
> — e.g. the Corporate Tax deadline]. I got your details from [honest source —
> public listing / your enquiry / event sign-up]. If you'd rather we didn't
> contact you again, just say so and I'll remove you right now."

Always be honest about the source. Honour a do-not-call request immediately.

## "Just send me a proposal / pricing"

> "I'd love to, but a proposal that's accurate needs a few details about your
> business first — otherwise it's a guess and that helps nobody. The 15-minute
> call gets us there. Shall I book it?"

## "I need to ask my partner / co-founder"

> "Makes sense — this is a founder-level decision. Why don't we book the call and
> you bring them on, or I send you a short summary you can share first? Which is
> easier?"

## "We're too small / pre-revenue for this"

> "That's actually a common reason to talk early — getting the basics right from
> the start is far cheaper than fixing them later, and some reliefs have windows.
> 15 minutes now can save a scramble later. Tuesday or Wednesday?"

## "Is this a sales call?"

> "It's an introduction call — I'm not selling you anything on this call. My job
> is just to see if a short conversation with our team would be useful to you.
> If it's not, we leave it there."

---

## Graceful exit lines

- "No problem at all — thanks for your time, and best of luck with the business."
- "I'll take you off our list — have a good day."
- "If anything changes around [the hook], you know where we are."

## What NOT to say

- Any number for fees, retainers, or hourly rates.
- Detailed tax advice ("you definitely qualify for...") — that is the call's job.
- Pressure or guilt ("you'll get penalised if you don't...").
- Anything implying urgency that is not real.
- Invented client names, stats, or credentials.
