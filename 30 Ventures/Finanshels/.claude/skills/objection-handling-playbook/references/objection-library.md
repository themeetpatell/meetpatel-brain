# Objection Library — UAE SME Prospects

> Reference for the `objection-handling-playbook` skill. The most common
> objections Finanshels hears from UAE SMEs, each with the underlying concern,
> a diagnostic question, and a model response built on Acknowledge -> Reframe ->
> Evidence -> Advance. Always tailor to the specific prospect. Verify all tax
> figures against current FTA guidance before using them as evidence.

---

## 1. "You're too expensive" / "I can get it cheaper"

**Underlying concern:** Doesn't yet see the value, or budget isn't approved, or
is comparing a full finance function to a part-time bookkeeper.

**Diagnostic question:** "When you say expensive — is it more than you budgeted,
or more than another quote you've had?"

**Model response:**
- *Acknowledge:* "Totally fair to weigh the cost — every dirham should earn its place."
- *Reframe:* "The real question isn't our fee versus a cheaper bookkeeper — it's the
  cost of getting it wrong. A late CT return, a VAT error, or messy books at audit
  cost far more than the difference, in penalties and in your time."
- *Evidence:* "We support 7,000+ UAE businesses with 150+ qualified accountants and
  an AI-native model — that's how we keep it reliable and fairly priced, not cheap-and-risky."
- *Advance:* "Let's do a 20-minute compliance health check so we're comparing the same
  scope — then the number will make sense in context."

## 2. "My cousin / our PRO does the books"

**Underlying concern:** Loyalty, low perceived stakes, or doesn't know that PRO
admin is not accounting or tax filing.

**Diagnostic question:** "Does your PRO also handle your Corporate Tax return and
your IFRS financials, or mainly the licence and visa side?"

**Model response:**
- *Acknowledge:* "Good — having someone you trust on admin really helps."
- *Reframe:* "PRO and family help is great for licensing and paperwork. Corporate Tax
  filing, VAT returns, and IFRS-compliant books are a different discipline — a mistake
  there sits with you and the FTA, not the PRO."
- *Evidence:* "We're built specifically for UAE tax and accounting — registration,
  advisory, and filing — for 7,000+ businesses. We can sit alongside your PRO, not replace them."
- *Advance:* "Want me to do a quick gap check on what's actually covered today versus
  what the FTA expects?"

## 3. "We'll deal with Corporate Tax later"

**Underlying concern:** No felt urgency; deadline feels distant; hopes it will simplify.

**Diagnostic question:** "When does your financial year end? Let's work out your
actual filing deadline together."

**Model response:**
- *Acknowledge:* "Plenty of founders feel CT can wait — you're not alone."
- *Reframe:* "The catch is the work happens *before* the deadline. The return is due
  nine months after your year-end, but it needs a full year of clean, IFRS-ready books
  behind it. Leaving it late means a rushed reconstruction and penalty risk."
- *Evidence:* "We've taken 7,000+ businesses through UAE compliance — the ones who start
  early file calmly; the ones who wait pay for the scramble."
- *Advance:* "Let's just map your deadline now and see how much runway you really have —
  no commitment."

## 4. "We already have an accountant"

**Underlying concern:** Switching feels like hassle; may be quietly unhappy but
inertia wins; unsure if a switch is worth it.

**Diagnostic question:** "If everything were perfect with your current setup, what's
the one thing you'd still want to improve?"

**Model response:**
- *Acknowledge:* "Makes sense — you've already solved this once."
- *Reframe:* "The question isn't whether you have an accountant — it's whether you're
  getting proactive advisory, clean monthly numbers, and CT/VAT handled without you
  chasing. Many founders have an accountant and still fly blind."
- *Evidence:* "Our AI-native delivery model means faster closes and real management
  reporting, not just year-end compliance — that's why 7,000+ businesses work with us."
- *Advance:* "I'm not asking you to switch today — let's do a second-opinion review of
  your last set of accounts and you decide."

## 5. "We're too small to need this"

**Underlying concern:** Believes compliance scales with size; doesn't know small
entities still must register and file.

**Diagnostic question:** "Is your revenue under AED 3M? There's actually a specific
relief that applies to you — worth getting right."

**Model response:**
- *Acknowledge:* "It's reasonable to think compliance is a big-company problem."
- *Reframe:* "In the UAE it isn't size-based. Even a small company must register for
  Corporate Tax and file a return. Small Business Relief can mean no tax — but only if
  you register and claim it correctly. Getting 'small' wrong is still a penalty."
- *Evidence:* "A large share of the 7,000+ businesses we support are exactly your size —
  the model is built for SMEs from AED 0 revenue upward."
- *Advance:* "Let me show you in five minutes what 'small' actually has to do — then you'll know."

## 6. "I don't trust outsourcing my finances"

**Underlying concern:** Fear of losing control, data security, or being out of the loop.

**Diagnostic question:** "Is it the control you'd miss, or the visibility — knowing
what's going on day to day?"

**Model response:**
- *Acknowledge:* "Your finances are sensitive — being careful is the right instinct."
- *Reframe:* "Outsourcing done well gives you *more* control, not less — clean monthly
  reporting, a clear view of cash and tax position, and a team accountable to you.
  The risk is the opposite: a shoebox of receipts no one really owns."
- *Evidence:* "7,000+ UAE businesses trust us with exactly this, and we're UAE-based and
  GCC-focused — your data and your regulator are local."
- *Advance:* "Let's start small — one month of books done our way, full visibility,
  and you judge it on the result."

## 7. "We're in a free zone so we pay 0% tax anyway"

**Underlying concern:** Believes free zone = automatic 0% CT; unaware of QFZP conditions.

**Diagnostic question:** "Have you confirmed you actually qualify as a QFZP — adequate
substance, qualifying activities, the de minimis test, and audited financials?"

**Model response:**
- *Acknowledge:* "Free zones do have a real advantage — you're right to value it."
- *Reframe:* "But 0% isn't automatic. It applies only to *qualifying income* for a
  *Qualifying Free Zone Person* — and only if you meet the substance, activity, de
  minimis, and audited-accounts conditions. Non-qualifying income is taxed at 9%, and
  failing the test can lose the 0% entirely. Free zone entities still register and file."
- *Evidence:* "We run QFZP assessments as part of our CT advisory across many of the
  7,000+ businesses we support — including free zone clients."
- *Advance:* "Let's do a QFZP qualification check so you know your 0% is actually safe."

## Lost-cause signals — when to route to a graceful exit

Not every objection should be rebutted. Recommend a graceful exit / referral when:

- The prospect has no UAE compliance obligation at all
- The structure needs Big-4-level work beyond Finanshels' scope
- Budget is genuinely zero and there is no near-term revenue
- The prospect is hostile or repeatedly disrespectful of the team's time

A clean, kind exit protects the brand and frees the rep's time.
