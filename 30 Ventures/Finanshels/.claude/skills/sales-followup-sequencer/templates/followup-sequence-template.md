# Follow-up Sequence Template — UAE SME Prospects

> Reusable template for the `sales-followup-sequencer` skill. Pick a cadence,
> assign a distinct angle and value-add per touch, vary the channel, write the
> copy in Finanshels voice. Replace every [bracket]. Verify all tax facts
> against current FTA guidance before sending.

---

## Cadence presets

| Preset | Touches | Window | Use when |
|--------|---------|--------|----------|
| Deadline-driven | 5-6 | 10-14 days | A real FTA deadline is weeks away |
| Standard | 5-6 | 18-24 days | Proposal sent, no specific deadline |
| Extended nurture | 4-5 | 5-8 weeks | Prospect signalled a long timeline |

**Pacing rule:** front-load value, never bunch touches, leave 2-4 days between
touches (tighter for deadline-driven). After the final touch, move to long-term
nurture — do not keep chasing.

---

## Angle rotation — every touch needs its own reason

| # | Angle | Purpose |
|---|-------|---------|
| 1 | Recap & make it easy | Restate the value, lower friction to act |
| 2 | Value-add resource | Give something useful, no ask of substance |
| 3 | Deadline / compliance reminder | Factual urgency tied to their FY |
| 4 | Address the likely objection | Pre-empt the silent blocker |
| 5 | Social proof / peer outcome | Approved proof point, peer context |
| 6 | Graceful breakup | Close the loop, leave the door open |

**Banned:** "just checking in", "circling back", "following up" with no new value.

---

## Channel guide

| Channel | Best for | Tone |
|---------|----------|------|
| Email | Substance, resources, recap, breakup | Clear, structured, short |
| WhatsApp | Warm short nudges (common and accepted in the UAE) | Friendly, 2-3 lines, one ask |
| LinkedIn | Soft professional touch, content share | Light, no hard sell |
| Call | The mid-sequence human moment | Warm, curious, not pushy |

Vary channels across the sequence. Respect a stated preference. Do not contact
on every channel at once.

```

```

## Value-add bank (UAE-relevant)

- Their CT return deadline, calculated from their financial-year-end (9 months after FY-end)
- VAT return due date — 28th of the month after the tax period
- Small Business Relief eligibility note (revenue <= AED 3M, periods ending on/before 31 Dec 2026)
- QFZP qualification reminder for free zone prospects
- 7-year record-keeping requirement
- A short, plain-English explainer relevant to their pain point
- A second-opinion offer on their last set of accounts

## Sample touch copy (adapt — do not send verbatim)

**Touch 1 — Email — Recap & make it easy**
"Hi [Name] — quick recap of what we proposed: [scope in one line], so you have
[outcome] handled without it eating your week. Happy to walk through it in 15
minutes — does [day] work? If a detail needs adjusting, just tell me and I'll
sort it."

**Touch 3 — WhatsApp — Deadline reminder**
"Hi [Name] — one for the diary: your Corporate Tax return is due [date], nine
months after your year-end. The books need to be ready well before then. Want me
to map the timeline so there's no scramble?"

**Touch 5 — Email — Social proof**
"Hi [Name] — we support 7,000+ UAE businesses with 150+ qualified accountants, a
lot of them at exactly your stage. The pattern is simple: the founders who get
finance off their plate early move faster. Still happy to help you do the same —
shall we lock a time?"

**Touch 6 — Email — Graceful breakup**
"Hi [Name] — I don't want to crowd your inbox, so this is my last note for now.
If getting finance handled moves up your list, I'm one message away — and I'll
send the occasional UAE compliance update so you're never caught out. All the best
with [their business]."

## Exit & breakup rules

- **Re-engages:** stop the sequence, move the deal to the next stage, log it.
- **Declines:** stop, mark closed-lost with the reason, route to nurture if a
  future fit is plausible.
- **No response after the final touch:** send the breakup touch, then add to
  long-term nurture (newsletter / quarterly check-in). Do not restart chasing.

---

## Reminders for the rep

- Never restate, discount, or invent a fee — route pricing to the account manager.
- Keep every touch short; one CTA only.
- Verify every deadline and rate against current FTA guidance.
- If an objection is the real blocker, use the `objection-handling-playbook` skill.
