# Competitor Profiles — UAE Accounting Market

> Reference for the `competitive-battlecard` skill. Honest profiles of the five
> categories of competitor a Finanshels prospect typically also considers. Be
> fair about strengths — a credible battlecard acknowledges them. Never name or
> disparage a specific competitor in prospect-facing copy.

---

## 1. Traditional accounting firm

**Who they are:** Established UAE accounting/audit practices, often partner-led,
charging on a billable-hour or fixed-retainer basis.

**Typical pitch:** "We're established, we have a name, we've been doing UAE
accounting for years, our partners sign off your work."

**Genuine strengths:**
- Brand familiarity and a long local track record
- Partner-level sign-off and audit relationships
- Comfortable with complex or larger entities

**Genuine weaknesses:**
- Often year-end-compliance focused, not proactive monthly advisory
- Manual, slower delivery — month-end close can drag
- Junior staff do the work; partner attention is thin for an SME account
- Pricing can be opaque and creep with billable hours

**Where Finanshels wins:** AI-native delivery means faster, cleaner closes and
real management reporting, not just an annual compliance file. SMEs get a
consistent service model, not whatever junior is free. 7,000+ businesses served.

**Landmine questions:** "How quickly do you get your monthly numbers today?"
"Do you get proactive advice through the year, or mainly hear from them at year-end?"
"Is your fee fixed, or does it move with hours?"

## 2. Freelance accountant / bookkeeper

**Who they are:** An individual doing books part-time, often remotely, for several
small businesses at a low monthly fee.

**Typical pitch:** "I'm cheap, I'm flexible, I'll do exactly what you ask, you can
call me directly."

**Genuine strengths:**
- Low cost and a personal, direct relationship
- Flexible and responsive for simple needs

**Genuine weaknesses:**
- Single point of failure — leave, illness, or quitting stops everything
- Rarely covers Corporate Tax filing, VAT advisory, IFRS, or audit support
- No quality review — errors go uncaught until the FTA or an auditor finds them
- No continuity, no system, no escalation path

**Where Finanshels wins:** A team of 150+ qualified accountants means continuity
and a quality-review layer — work is never one person's shoebox. Full stack:
bookkeeping, CT, VAT, payroll, audit support under one partner.

**Landmine questions:** "When your bookkeeper is on leave, who files your VAT return?"
"Who reviews their work before it goes to the FTA?" "Do they handle your Corporate
Tax return, or just the bookkeeping?"

---

## 3. Business-setup consultant offering accounting as an add-on

**Who they are:** Company-formation / PRO firms that bundle basic accounting into
a licence or renewal package as an upsell.

**Typical pitch:** "We already handle your licence and visas — let us do the
accounting too, it's all in one place and convenient."

**Genuine strengths:**
- Convenient single point of contact for licensing and admin
- Already has the relationship and the documents

**Genuine weaknesses:**
- Accounting is a side-line, not the core competence
- Often outsourced or done by non-specialist staff
- Weak on Corporate Tax advisory, QFZP assessment, IFRS, and audit readiness
- Convenience masks real compliance risk

**Where Finanshels wins:** Accounting and UAE tax are the core discipline, not an
add-on — CT registration, advisory and filing, VAT, audit-ready IFRS books.
Finanshels can sit alongside the setup consultant, handling what they cannot.

**Landmine questions:** "Is accounting their main business, or an add-on to the
licence package?" "Who handles your Corporate Tax return and QFZP assessment?"
"If the FTA queries your filing, who actually answers it?"

---

## 4. In-house hire

**Who they are:** The prospect is considering hiring a full-time accountant or
finance manager onto their own payroll.

**Typical pitch (to themselves):** "If we hire someone, we have full control and
they're always available."

**Genuine strengths:**
- Dedicated, on-site, fully aligned to the business
- Deep knowledge of the company over time

**Genuine weaknesses:**
- Expensive — salary, gratuity, visa, software, plus management overhead
- Single person — no cover for leave, no second opinion, no specialist depth
- One hire rarely spans bookkeeping, CT, VAT, payroll, and audit
- Recruiting and retaining good finance talent in the UAE is hard

**Where Finanshels wins:** A whole finance function — 150+ qualified accountants,
specialist CT and VAT depth, AI-native tooling — for less than the loaded cost of
one mid-level hire, with no recruitment risk and no single point of failure.

**Landmine questions:** "What's the fully loaded cost of that hire — salary,
gratuity, visa, software, your time managing them?" "Does one person cover
bookkeeping, Corporate Tax, VAT, payroll and audit?" "What happens when they leave?"

## 5. DIY accounting software

**Who they are:** The prospect plans to run their own books on accounting software
(e.g. a cloud bookkeeping tool) with no professional support.

**Typical pitch (to themselves):** "The software is cheap and modern, I'll just do
it myself."

**Genuine strengths:**
- Low software cost; modern, accessible interface
- Fine for very simple invoicing and basic record-keeping

**Genuine weaknesses:**
- Software records transactions — it does not advise, file, or catch errors
- No one assesses CT position, QFZP qualification, or VAT treatment
- Founder time is consumed; mistakes surface at filing or audit
- No accountability — the founder personally owns every error with the FTA

**Where Finanshels wins:** Finanshels is an infrastructure partner, not a tool —
the software plus the qualified team plus UAE tax expertise. The founder gets
their time back and a partner accountable for the result.

**Landmine questions:** "Who tells you whether a transaction is zero-rated, exempt,
or standard-rated for VAT?" "How many hours a month does doing it yourself cost
you?" "Who's accountable if the Corporate Tax return is wrong — the software, or you?"

---

## Universal do / don't

**DO:** Acknowledge the competitor's strengths; lead with the prospect's risk and
lost time; anchor every claim to an approved proof point or a verifiable FTA fact;
let landmine questions do the work; always advance to a concrete next step.

**DON'T:** Name or disparage a specific competitor to the prospect; overclaim or
invent statistics; promise a tax outcome or guaranteed saving; quote or undercut
a fee to win — route pricing to the account manager.
