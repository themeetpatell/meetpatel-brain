# January

### **ENGINE 1: FOUNDER-LED NARRATIVE**

**1. Founder Truths**

Raw, first-principle insights about:

- Why cofounder matching fails
- Why “chemistry” is overrated
- Why skill mismatch kills startups quietly
- Why most founders pick partners emotionally, not strategically

Tone: calm, confident, precise

Format: short paragraphs, no fluff, no preaching

**2. Silent Failures**

Stories without naming people:

- “I’ve seen startups die even after funding because…”
- “The first mistake founders make before building anything…”

These posts build authority without bragging.

**3. Builder POV**

Behind-the-scenes thinking:

- Why BiggMate exists
- How you think about trust, alignment, execution
- What founders should optimize for early

**4. Founder Questions**

End posts with sharp questions:

- “Who do you trust to build with?”
- “What happens if your cofounder leaves in 6 months?”

This drives comments → reach → belief.

📌 **Cadence**

- Founder profile: **1 post/day**
- BiggMate page: **3–4 posts/week**
- Every comment gets a thoughtful reply

## **ENGINE 2: WAITLIST THAT FEELS LIKE A PRIVILEGE**

No “Join our waitlist” energy.

### **Positioning**

**“Founders building seriously. Entry is limited.”**

### **Waitlist Page Must Do 3 Things**

1. Clearly state the problem (cofounder mismatch)
2. Make founders feel seen
3. Make them feel early, not late

### **Waitlist Hooks (Rotate weekly)**

- “Early founders only”
- “We’re onboarding 500 builders first”
- “Not for idea collectors”

### **Conversion Boosters**

- After signup → show:
    - “You’re #___ on the list”
    - “Top 10% get early access”
- Referral unlock:
    - Refer 3 founders → move up
    - Refer 5 → early access badge

## **ENGINE 3: CORE BELIEVERS (Your First 500)**

These people matter more than 5,000 random signups.

### **Who are Core Believers?**

- First-time founders
- Repeat founders
- Operators planning to start
- People actively thinking about cofounders

### **How to Build Them**

**Private Founder WhatsApp / Slack Circle**

- Invite only
- No promotion inside
- 1 question/day discussion

Examples:

- “What do you look for in a cofounder but never say out loud?”
- “What’s the biggest risk in choosing the wrong partner?”

Your presence here builds *founder trust*.

Goal:

500 people who feel BiggMate is **their product**.

## **ENGINE 4: PARTNERSHIP SIGNALS (Borrowed Trust)**

January is for soft alliances.

### **Targets**

- Accelerators
- Founder communities
- College entrepreneurship cells
- Operator networks

### **Playbook**

- No sales pitch
- Offer:
    - “Founder-only early access”
    - “Cofounder alignment session”
    - “Private founder AMA”

These partnerships don’t need scale.

They need **signal**.
