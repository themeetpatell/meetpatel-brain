## **D1 · Emotional & Attachment Intelligence**

How you attach, how you regulate, how you need to be held emotionally. The single most predictive dimension for long-term relationship survival.

**Weight: 20% — Highest, Research: Bowlby, Ainsworth, Gottman 
India relevance: Very High**

**Why it's weighted highest**

Attachment anxiety predicted an average of 22% of variance in destructive conflict behaviors (criticism, contempt, defensiveness, stonewalling) — Gottman's "four horsemen" that predict divorce with 87.5% accuracy. Neuroticism and Conscientiousness predicted relationship satisfaction over 9 years. Emotional dimension compatibility is the foundation everything else rests on.

Research basis: Ainsworth's attachment theory; Gottman's Four Horsemen; Mikulincer & Shaver (2007); Longitudinal study — attachment orientations predicted conflict behaviors beyond relational satisfaction alone.

**5 Measurement Questions (asked conversationally, never as a list)**

**1** "Tell me about a time someone you cared about went quiet on you — what happened inside you?" *→ Extracts: anxiety level, abandonment sensitivity, how they signal distress*

**2** "When you're really overwhelmed — not just stressed, but truly underwater — what do you need from the person closest to you?" *→ Extracts: support style preference, secure/anxious/avoidant patterns*

**3** "Has anyone ever told you that you're 'too much' or 'too closed off' in relationships? What do you think they were seeing?" *→ Extracts: self-awareness, pattern recognition, fearful-avoidant signals*

**4** "What does it feel like when an argument ends but nothing is actually resolved — just dropped?" *→ Extracts: stonewalling tolerance, conflict completion need, emotional processing style*

**5** "When you love someone, how do they know? What does it look like from the outside?" *→ Extracts: love expression style, emotional vocabulary depth, intimacy comfort level*

**AI Inference Signals (extracted from conversation, not stated)**

Response latency and message length when asked vulnerable questions — avoidant users give brief, deflecting answers; anxious users give long, detail-heavy ones

Use of "they" vs "I" when describing past relationship dynamics — externalized attribution vs internalized

Whether they spontaneously mention how they hurt others, or only how they were hurt

Emotional vocabulary richness — secure attachment correlates with precise, nuanced emotional language

How they describe needing space — as a healthy preference or as an escape mechanism

**Weighting Formula**

Attachment type match : 50%

Conflict style complement: 30%

Emotional vocabulary depth: 20%

Note: Secure + Anxious is viable if the secure partner has high emotional intelligence. Avoidant + Avoidant is low-risk for conflict but high-risk for emotional distance. Anxious + Anxious is the highest-risk pairing.

**Display Language (what users see — never clinical terms)**

**For a Secure profile:** "You bring emotional steadiness to relationships. You can stay present in hard conversations without shutting down or escalating. That's rare and genuinely valuable."

*Match narrative: "Both of you described needing to process feelings before discussing them. You'll rarely pressure each other for an immediate response — and that's a gift."*
