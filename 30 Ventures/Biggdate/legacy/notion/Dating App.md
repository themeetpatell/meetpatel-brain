3-year city plans, career ambition level, marriage and kids timeline, life pace preference, what a good life actually looks like in 10 years.

Not predictive — but culturally resonant for 30% of users. Presented as a "personality lens" — fun, insightful, never a dealmaker. Vedic chart overview as one optional dimension, not a gate.

**The product journey — 7 magic moments**

1. **The world you enter**

You don't sign up to a dating app. You apply to a community. The landing page asks one question: "What kind of person do you want to become in a relationship?" Not who do you want. Who do YOU want to become. This frame changes everything — it attracts growth-oriented, self-aware people and filters out everyone else.

*"Most apps ask who you want. We ask who you're becoming. The difference is everything."*

1. **The 21-day self-discovery arc**

Not a one-time questionnaire. A 21-day daily 3-minute conversation with your AI companion. Day 1–7: your relationship history and patterns. Day 8–14: your values, needs, and non-negotiables. Day 15–21: your life architecture and where you're going. The AI builds your 10-dimension compatibility profile silently. At day 21 you receive your "Relationship Identity" — a beautiful, shareable card showing who you are as a partner. This is the viral growth mechanism.

*"Your Relationship Identity: Secure-leaning. Values-driven. Family-forward. Builds trust slowly, loves deeply."*

1. **You enter a circle, not a feed**

After discovery, you're placed in a "Circle" — a curated group of 12–20 people at similar life stages, with compatible values, in your city. No profiles visible yet. No photos. For 2 weeks, the circle interacts through: weekly discussion prompts ("What's something you've changed your mind about in the last year?"), shared interest threads, anonymous voice notes. The AI watches who gravitates toward whom. Connections emerge naturally — like they do at the best parties.

*"You've been talking to Arjun in the circle for 3 days. You both went deep on the same question. Want us to make an introduction?"*

1. **The introduction — not a match**

When the AI detects genuine mutual pull (conversation depth, response time, topic overlap, explicit signals), it creates a private introduction between two people. Not a match notification. An introduction — like a mutual friend would do: "We think you two should talk. Here's why specifically." Both parties see each other's compatibility landscape across all 10 dimensions. No photo until both accept the introduction. Then photo unlocks. Then a shared first question both answer before any free messaging.

*"Arjun and you align on 8 of 10 dimensions. The strongest: how you process conflict. The one to watch: your financial styles differ — worth exploring early."*

1. **The AI companion — your relationship guide**

Throughout the journey, your AI companion is always present. Not a chatbot. A relationship intelligence layer that knows you deeply — your patterns, your needs, your growth edges. It helps you: understand conversations in progress ("Why does he keep going quiet after vulnerable exchanges?"), prepare for difficult conversations, debrief after dates, understand your own reactions. It never tells you what to do. It helps you understand yourself well enough to make your own great decisions.

*"You've gone quiet in this conversation twice when he mentioned family. That's worth noticing."*

1. **IRL experiences — the move to real life**

BiggDate runs curated experiences in every city — not speed dating. Not cocktail mixers. Designed experiences where connection happens naturally: cooking classes, hiking groups, book clubs, creative workshops, stargazing evenings, startup founder dinners. Circle members are invited to experiences together. They meet in person without the pressure of "this is a date." The app then enables follow-up connections from who they met. IRL is not an add-on. It's the core product promise: we make your social life romantic and fun.

*"Your circle is doing a sunset pottery session this Saturday in Bengaluru. 8 people confirmed. Arjun is going."*

1. **The relationship arc — we don't disappear after the match**

After two people start meeting regularly, BiggDate transitions to "Relationship Mode" — with their consent. Monthly compatibility check-ins. A private "relationship health" view showing which dimensions are thriving and which need attention. AI-guided conversation starters for difficult topics. Pre-meet prep prompts before important conversations. The product continues generating value through the relationship — creating the recurring revenue layer no competitor has.

*"You've been seeing each other for 6 weeks. Your Financial dimension shows the most divergence. Here's how to open that conversation gently."*

## What's Missing in the First Release

Here's an honest audit of gaps, ranked by severity:

### Blockers — Ship before scaling

**1. No native mobile app**
Everything breaks without this. Voice notes, real push notifications, camera access, offline state, background sync. iOS + Android is not optional.

**2. Real-time messaging**
The current polling-based chat kills the "alive" feeling that makes dating conversations feel electric. Need WebSocket or Supabase Realtime for instant message delivery.

**3. Voice notes in chat**
WhatsApp trained 535M Indians to send voice notes. It's more personal than text, lower friction for longer thoughts, and a strong safety signal (voice is harder to fake than text). This alone would differentiate BiggDate from every other chat in the market.

**4. Gender balance mechanics**
No feature exists today to address the 3:1 men-to-women ratio. Need: referral bonuses for women inviting women, women-only match preferences, stricter quality gates for male profiles, explicit "women's safety" marketing.

**5. Push notifications working properly**
Web push is unreliable. Soul Knock answers, new matches, mutual connections — these need to arrive instantly on a native push channel or users disengage within days.

### Growth Limiters — Ship in first 90 days

**6. Match quality feedback loop**
Users have no way to tell BiggDate "this match was terrible" or "we went on three dates." Without this signal, the matching AI can't improve. Need: post-Soul Knock feedback, post-date rating, match quality signals flowing back into the model.

**7. Referral + vouching system**
India runs on trust networks. "My friend vouches for this person" is worth more than any algorithm. A Friends Vouch feature — where a trusted person (friend, sibling) can co-sign a profile — would be transformative for both conversion and safety.

**8. Vernacular language support**
Only 7-8% of Indian youth use English-first apps. The rest of the market is Hindi, Tamil, Telugu, Marathi, Bengali. Maahi should speak Hindi. The app should auto-detect language. This alone 10x's the addressable market within India.

**9. "Coming soon" / waitlist flow**
There's no viral loop baked into the product. Every new user should generate an invitation opportunity. The Soul Knock system is inherently viral (you invite someone to answer a question) — but there's no formal referral infrastructure.

**10. Loading states and skeleton screens**
The current FE has null renders while loading (AuthProvider pattern). On mobile networks in India (even with 4G), this creates perceived slowness that kills D1 retention.

### Strategic Moat Builders — Year 1

**11. Commitment tracking / relationship status**
No mechanism to know when BiggDate succeeds. Users who start dating someone should have a "we're seeing each other" status that keeps them in the ecosystem (Couple mode) instead of just deleting the app. This data is the foundation of everything.

**12. Family approval flow**
India-specific. Allow users to invite one trusted person (parent, elder sibling, best friend) to review their profile and their top matches. That person sees a limited profile view and can give a thumbs up/down. Not arranged marriage — just structured social proof. This is the product no Western company can clone.

**13. In-app video for the "vibes check"**
Before meeting in person, users want to see if someone is real and matches their energy. A 5-minute in-app video call, available only after Soul Knock completes, would dramatically increase meeting conversion and reduce safety concerns.

**14. Date outcomes tracking**
When two people meet IRL, BiggDate should know. A simple "did you meet?" prompt + "how did it go?" (1-5 stars, optional note) creates the training data to make AI matching dramatically better over 12 months.

**15. The Pulse community needs depth**
Currently it's a basic feed. It should be the relationship intelligence community — daily questions that get answered by thousands of users, creating a public signals layer that also serves as top-of-funnel content for SEO and social.
