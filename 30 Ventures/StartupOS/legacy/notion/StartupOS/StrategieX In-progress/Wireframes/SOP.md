# SOP

### **1. Purpose**

The purpose of this SOP is to standardize the process of creating wireframes for product development. This ensures that all wireframes are clear, consistent, and align with the product’s goals and user experience principles. Wireframes help communicate the layout, structure, and navigation of the product to stakeholders, designers, and developers.

### **2. Scope**

This SOP applies to all product team members involved in the design and development process, including UX designers, product managers, developers, and any other stakeholders who contribute to wireframe creation, review, and approval.

### **3. Roles and Responsibilities**

- **UX Designer**: Responsible for creating wireframes based on user needs, product requirements, and feedback. Ensures wireframes are user-centered and follow best practices.
- **Product Manager (PM)**: Provides input on product requirements, user stories, and business goals. Reviews wireframes for alignment with product strategy.
- **UI Designer**: Collaborates with UX designers to ensure that the wireframes align with visual design elements and UI guidelines.
- **Developer**: Provides technical input on the feasibility of the wireframes and suggests changes if necessary.
- **Stakeholders**: Participate in wireframe reviews, provide feedback, and approve the wireframes.

### **4. Wireframing Process**

1. **Gather Requirements and Understand User Needs**
    - **Step 1**: Review product requirements, user stories, personas, and customer journey maps to understand the user needs and business goals.
    - **Step 2**: Conduct user research or refer to existing research data to gather insights into user behavior, pain points, and preferences.
    - **Output**: A comprehensive understanding of the requirements, goals, and user expectations.
2. **Define the Scope and Purpose of the Wireframe**
    - **Step 1**: Determine the specific screens, features, or flows that need to be wireframed (e.g., onboarding screens, dashboard, checkout process).
    - **Step 2**: Identify the level of fidelity required for the wireframe (low, mid, or high fidelity) based on the stage of the design process.
    - **Output**: A clear definition of the scope and purpose of the wireframe.
3. **Create Initial Wireframes**
    - **Step 1**: Choose the appropriate wireframing tool (e.g., Figma, Sketch, Balsamiq, Adobe XD) based on team preferences and project needs.
    - **Step 2**: Start with low-fidelity wireframes to outline the basic structure, layout, and navigation flow without focusing on visual details.
    - **Step 3**: Use consistent elements (e.g., buttons, input fields, navigation bars) from the design system to maintain uniformity.
    - **Output**: Low-fidelity wireframes that provide a basic layout and structure for review.
4. **Iterate on Wireframes and Add Detail**
    - **Step 1**: Gather initial feedback from the product manager, UI designer, and stakeholders on the low-fidelity wireframes.
    - **Step 2**: Iterate on the wireframes based on feedback, refining the layout, adding more detail, and moving to mid-fidelity wireframes if needed.
    - **Step 3**: Incorporate content placeholders, annotations, and descriptions to clarify functionality and interactions.
    - **Output**: Mid-fidelity wireframes with more detailed layouts and content placement.
5. **Review and Validate Wireframes**
    - **Step 1**: Present the wireframes to the product team and relevant stakeholders for a review session. Use collaboration tools (e.g., Figma, Miro) for live feedback and discussions.
    - **Step 2**: Gather feedback on usability, alignment with user needs, and product goals. Ensure any technical constraints are considered.
    - **Step 3**: Document all feedback and decisions made during the review session for transparency and future reference.
    - **Output**: Consolidated feedback and action items for further refinement.
6. **Finalize High-Fidelity Wireframes**
    - **Step 1**: Based on feedback, create high-fidelity wireframes that include detailed UI elements, precise spacing, and interactive elements (if needed).
    - **Step 2**: Ensure that the wireframes are aligned with the design system, brand guidelines, and accessibility standards.
    - **Step 3**: Add annotations and specifications (e.g., font sizes, padding, interaction states) to guide developers in the implementation phase.
    - **Output**: High-fidelity wireframes ready for final approval and handoff.
7. **Handoff to Development Team**
    - **Step 1**: Export the wireframes and specifications in a format suitable for the development team (e.g., Figma links, PDFs, or Zeplin).
    - **Step 2**: Conduct a handoff meeting with developers to walk through the wireframes, clarify any questions, and ensure a smooth transition to development.
    - **Step 3**: Address any follow-up questions or concerns from the development team promptly.
    - **Output**: Wireframes and specifications successfully handed off to the development team.
8. **Post-Implementation Review**
    - **Step 1**: After the development phase, conduct a review to ensure that the implemented design aligns with the approved wireframes.
    - **Step 2**: Identify any discrepancies and work with the development team to make necessary adjustments.
    - **Step 3**: Document learnings and update wireframing best practices to avoid similar issues in the future.
    - **Output**: A review report highlighting discrepancies, resolutions, and lessons learned.

### **5. Tools and Resources**

- **Wireframing Tools**: Figma, Sketch, Adobe XD, Balsamiq.
- **Collaboration Tools**: Miro, InVision, Zeplin.
- **Documentation and Feedback Tools**: Confluence, Notion, Google Drive.
- **Design System**: Access to the organization’s design system for consistent UI elements.

### **6. Best Practices**

- **Consistency**: Use a design system to maintain consistency in wireframes (e.g., buttons, icons, colors).
- **Clarity**: Include annotations and labels to explain interactions, navigation flows, and content placement.
- **Feedback-Driven**: Prioritize iterative feedback loops to refine wireframes based on team and stakeholder input.
- **User-Centered**: Focus on user needs and pain points to ensure wireframes lead to intuitive and efficient user experiences.
- **Accessibility**: Ensure wireframes adhere to accessibility guidelines (e.g., WCAG) to provide inclusive experiences.

### **7. Review and Continuous Improvement**

- **Frequency of Review**: Conduct quarterly reviews of the wireframing process to identify areas of improvement.
- **Feedback Loop**: Regularly collect feedback from team members involved in the wireframing process to enhance the SOP.
- **Iterate SOP**: Update the SOP based on new tools, techniques, and evolving product needs.

### **8. Compliance and Version Control**

- **Version Control**: Use proper version control for wireframes (e.g., version naming conventions) to keep track of changes and iterations.
- **Storage and Access**: Store wireframes in a central repository with controlled access to maintain security and version integrity.

### **9. Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)**

- **Wireframe Approval Time**: Average time taken to move from initial wireframe to final approval.
- **Feedback Incorporation Rate**: Percentage of feedback incorporated into subsequent wireframe iterations.
- **Alignment Rate with Final Design**: Percentage of final designs that align with the wireframes provided to developers.
- **Stakeholder Satisfaction**: Survey score measuring stakeholder satisfaction with the wireframing process and outcomes.
