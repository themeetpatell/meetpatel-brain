# SOP

### **1. Purpose**

The purpose of this SOP is to establish a consistent process for creating, testing, and iterating on prototypes. This helps ensure that prototypes are effective in communicating design intent, validating user interactions, and gathering actionable feedback before moving into development.

### **2. Scope**

This SOP applies to all team members involved in product design and development, including UX designers, UI designers, product managers, developers, and other stakeholders responsible for prototyping, reviewing, and approving prototypes.

### **3. Roles and Responsibilities**

- **UX Designer**: Leads the creation of interactive prototypes based on wireframes, user stories, and feedback. Focuses on usability, interaction design, and user flows.
- **UI Designer**: Collaborates with UX designers to add visual elements, branding, and style to the prototype. Ensures the prototype aligns with UI guidelines and design systems.
- **Product Manager (PM)**: Provides input on user stories, business goals, and requirements. Reviews prototypes to ensure they align with the product vision and strategy.
- **Developers**: Provide technical input to ensure the feasibility of the prototype and suggest improvements from a development perspective.
- **Stakeholders**: Participate in prototype reviews, provide feedback, and approve the final prototype for development.

### **4. Prototyping Process**

1. **Define Prototyping Goals and Scope**
    - **Step 1**: Clarify the purpose of the prototype (e.g., concept validation, user testing, stakeholder approval) and the specific aspects to be prototyped (e.g., user flow, interaction design, visual style).
    - **Step 2**: Determine the fidelity level required (low, mid, or high fidelity) based on the stage of the design process and the audience for the prototype.
    - **Output**: Documented goals and scope of the prototype.
2. **Select the Appropriate Prototyping Tool**
    - **Step 1**: Choose the right prototyping tool based on the project needs and team familiarity (e.g., Figma, Adobe XD, InVision, Axure).
    - **Step 2**: Set up the tool with the necessary design assets, components, and libraries from the design system.
    - **Output**: Prototyping tool set up and ready for use.
3. **Create the Initial Prototype**
    - **Step 1**: Start with a low-fidelity or mid-fidelity prototype to outline the basic user flows and interactions. Focus on functionality and layout rather than visual details.
    - **Step 2**: Use consistent components from the design system to ensure uniformity and coherence.
    - **Step 3**: Incorporate interactive elements (e.g., buttons, menus, hover effects) and define the user journey across screens.
    - **Output**: Initial prototype with basic interactions and user flows.
4. **Iterate and Add Visual Details**
    - **Step 1**: Gather initial feedback from the product manager, UI designer, developers, and stakeholders to identify areas for improvement.
    - **Step 2**: Refine the prototype based on feedback, adding visual elements, branding, and high-fidelity design elements to create a more realistic experience.
    - **Step 3**: Include micro-interactions, animations, and transitions to enhance the user experience and simulate real-world scenarios.
    - **Output**: High-fidelity prototype with detailed visual design and interactions.
5. **Conduct Usability Testing**
    - **Step 1**: Plan and conduct usability testing sessions with target users to validate the prototype's effectiveness, usability, and overall experience.
    - **Step 2**: Use remote or in-person testing tools (e.g., UserTesting, Lookback) to capture user feedback, screen recordings, and interaction data.
    - **Step 3**: Analyze the results to identify usability issues, friction points, and areas for improvement.
    - **Output**: Usability Testing Report with key findings, insights, and actionable recommendations.
6. **Review and Iterate on the Prototype**
    - **Step 1**: Present the refined prototype to the product team and stakeholders for a comprehensive review session using collaboration tools (e.g., Figma, Miro).
    - **Step 2**: Document feedback from stakeholders and prioritize changes based on impact, feasibility, and alignment with product goals.
    - **Step 3**: Iterate on the prototype based on feedback, making necessary adjustments to improve usability, functionality, or visual appeal.
    - **Output**: Updated prototype with incorporated feedback ready for final approval.
7. **Finalize and Approve the Prototype**
    - **Step 1**: Conduct a final review session with all stakeholders to ensure the prototype aligns with the product vision, requirements, and user needs.
    - **Step 2**: Obtain formal approval from key stakeholders (e.g., product manager, design lead, development lead) to move forward with development.
    - **Output**: Approved prototype ready for handoff to the development team.
8. **Handoff to Development Team**
    - **Step 1**: Export the prototype and design specifications using prototyping tools (e.g., Figma, Zeplin) in formats suitable for developers.
    - **Step 2**: Conduct a handoff meeting with developers to walk through the prototype, clarify interactions, and address any technical questions.
    - **Step 3**: Provide access to design assets, style guides, and any additional documentation needed for implementation.
    - **Output**: Successful handoff of the prototype and design specifications to the development team.
9. **Monitor and Validate During Development**
    - **Step 1**: Collaborate with the development team throughout the implementation phase to ensure the final product aligns with the approved prototype.
    - **Step 2**: Conduct regular check-ins and reviews to catch discrepancies early and make adjustments as needed.
    - **Step 3**: Perform a final validation after development to ensure fidelity between the prototype and the developed product.
    - **Output**: A validated product that matches the design and interactions of the prototype.

### **5. Tools and Resources**

- **Prototyping Tools**: Figma, Adobe XD, InVision, Axure, Sketch.
- **Usability Testing Tools**: UserTesting, Lookback, Maze, Hotjar.
- **Collaboration Tools**: Miro, Zeplin, InVision, Confluence.
- **Design System**: Access to the organization’s design system and component library.

### **6. Best Practices**

- **Iterative Design**: Continuously iterate on prototypes based on user feedback and stakeholder input to refine and enhance the user experience.
- **Consistency**: Use components from the design system to ensure consistency across screens and interactions.
- **Feedback-Driven**: Prioritize user feedback and usability test results to guide prototype iterations.
- **Accessibility**: Ensure that prototypes adhere to accessibility guidelines (e.g., WCAG) to create inclusive experiences.
- **Collaboration**: Encourage cross-functional collaboration throughout the prototyping process to align on goals and address technical constraints early.

### **7. Review and Continuous Improvement**

- **Frequency of Review**: Conduct quarterly reviews of the prototyping process to identify areas for improvement.
- **Feedback Loop**: Regularly collect feedback from team members involved in prototyping to enhance the SOP.
- **Iterate SOP**: Update the SOP based on new tools, techniques, and evolving product needs.

### **8. Compliance and Version Control**

- **Version Control**: Maintain version control for prototypes (e.g., version naming conventions) to keep track of changes and iterations.
- **Storage and Access**: Store prototypes in a centralized repository with controlled access to maintain security and version integrity.

### **9. Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)**

- **Prototype Approval Time**: Average time taken to move from initial prototype to final approval.
- **Usability Test Success Rate**: Percentage of usability tests that meet defined success criteria.
- **Feedback Incorporation Rate**: Percentage of feedback incorporated into subsequent prototype iterations.
- **Alignment Rate with Final Product**: Percentage of final products that align with the approved prototypes.
- **Stakeholder Satisfaction**: Survey score measuring stakeholder satisfaction with the prototyping process and outcomes.
