# Overview SOP

### 1. **Purpose**

- **1.1 Objective**: Clearly define the purpose of the SOP.
- **1.2 Scope**: Explain what areas and activities the SOP covers.

### 2. **Roles and Responsibilities**

- **2.1 Stakeholder Roles**: List all key roles (e.g., Product Manager, Developer, UX Designer, Customer Support) and their responsibilities in the product lifecycle.
- **2.2 Responsibility Matrix**: A RACI (Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed) chart or similar to clarify who is responsible for each task.

### 3. **Standard Procedures**

- **3.1 Product Development Process**
    - **3.1.1 Requirements Gathering**: Steps for collecting and documenting product requirements.
    - **3.1.2 Design and Prototyping**: Procedures for designing and prototyping features.
    - **3.1.3 Development**: Coding standards, development environments, and testing protocols.
    - **3.1.4 Testing and Quality Assurance**: Steps for unit testing, integration testing, and user acceptance testing (UAT).
- **3.2 Product Release**
    - **3.2.1 Pre-Release Checks**: Checklist of tasks to be completed before a release.
    - **3.2.2 Deployment**: Procedures for deploying the product to production.
    - **3.2.3 Post-Release Monitoring**: Steps for monitoring the product after release and resolving any issues.
- **3.3 User Support and Issue Resolution**
    - **3.3.1 Support Request Handling**: Process for handling customer support tickets.
    - **3.3.2 Bug Tracking and Resolution**: Steps for logging, prioritizing, and resolving bugs.
- **3.4 Product Maintenance**
    - **3.4.1 Regular Maintenance**: Schedule and procedures for routine maintenance.
    - **3.4.2 Updates and Patches**: Process for applying updates and patches to the product.
- **3.5 Feedback and Continuous Improvement**
    - **3.5.1 User Feedback Collection**: Methods for gathering feedback from users.
    - **3.5.2 Feedback Implementation**: Process for evaluating and implementing feedback into the product.
