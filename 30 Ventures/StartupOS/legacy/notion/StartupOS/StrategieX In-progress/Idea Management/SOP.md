# SOP

**Purpose**

To establish a consistent and transparent process for prioritizing features, enhancements, and bug fixes based on their importance and urgency.

**Responsibilities**

- **Product Manager:** Leads the prioritization process, facilitates discussions, and ensures alignment with the product strategy and roadmap.
- **Development Team:** Provides input on technical feasibility, effort estimates, and potential risks associated with each item.
- **Stakeholders:** Contribute insights and perspectives on business goals, user needs, and market trends.

### Guiding Principles:

- **Client-Centricity:** Prioritize features that directly address client pain points, enhance their financial management capabilities, and simplify tax compliance.
- **Team Empowerment:** Focus on features that streamline workflows, improve collaboration, and empower the FinOps team to provide exceptional service to clients.
- **Data Security & Compliance:** Maintain a high priority on features that ensure data security, privacy, and adherence to relevant financial and tax regulations.
- **Scalability & Performance:** Consider the long-term scalability and performance of the platform, prioritizing features that support growth and handle increasing user loads.
- **User Experience:** Prioritize features that enhance the overall user experience, making both the client and team apps intuitive, user-friendly, and enjoyable to use.

### **MoSCoW Categories**

- **Must-Have:**
    - **Critical Features:** Essential functionalities that are core to the product's value proposition and must be included in the next release.
    - **Compliance Requirements:** Features or fixes necessary to meet regulatory or legal obligations.
    - **High-Impact Bug Fixes:** Critical bugs that severely impact user experience or product stability.
- **Should-Have:**
    - **High-Value Features:** Features that deliver significant value to clients and contribute to business goals, but are not absolutely essential for the next release.
    - **Usability Enhancements:** Improvements that significantly enhance the user experience and ease of use.
    - **Moderate-Impact Bug Fixes:** Bugs that impact user experience but do not pose a critical risk.
- **Could-Have:**
    - **Nice-to-Have Features:** Desirable features or enhancements that would improve the product but are not urgent or critical.
    - **Performance Optimizations:** Improvements that enhance product performance or efficiency.
    - **Low-Impact Bug Fixes:** Minor bugs that have a minimal impact on user experience.
- **Won't-Have (this time):**
    - **Out-of-Scope Features:** Features or ideas that are not aligned with the current product strategy or roadmap.
    - **Low-Priority Enhancements:** Improvements that offer minimal value or can be deferred to a later release.
    - May be revisited in the future based on evolving priorities or feedback.

---

### **Prioritization Process**

- **Apply MoSCoW Labels:**
    - Conduct a collaborative prioritization session and Discuss each item in the backlog and assign a MoSCoW label based on its importance and urgency.
    - Consider factors such as:
        - **Client Feedback & Requests:** Weigh the frequency and urgency of client requests and feedback, particularly for features that address significant pain points, specifically **No of occurrences for the issue / feedbacks**
        - **Business Goals & Revenue Potential:** Prioritize features that align with business objectives and have the potential to drive revenue growth.
        - **Technical Feasibility & Dependencies:** Evaluate the technical complexity, resource requirements, and dependencies of each item.
        - **Regulatory & Compliance Considerations:** Ensure that new features or enhancements comply with relevant financial and tax regulations.
        - **Competitive Landscape:** Consider the features and capabilities of competing products in the market.
- **Prioritize Backlog:**
    - Organize the backlog based on MoSCoW labels:
        - With Must-Haves → Should-Haves → Could-Haves → Won't-Haves.
