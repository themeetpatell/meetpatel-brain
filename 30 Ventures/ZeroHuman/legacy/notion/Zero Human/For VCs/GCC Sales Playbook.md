# GCC Sales Playbook

---

## Sales Philosophy

**Mission**: Help GCC businesses create Arabic video ads in 60 seconds, saving 90% on production costs while building a marketplace that empowers creators.

**Approach**: Consultative selling focused on understanding Arabic content needs, cultural sensitivity, and business outcomes.

---

## Sales Process (5-Step Framework)

### Step 1: Discovery (15-20 minutes)

**Goal**: Understand pain points, Arabic content needs, current solutions

**Key Questions**:

- "How do you currently create Arabic video ads?"
- "What's your biggest challenge with video production?"
- "How much do you spend on video ads per quarter?"
- "How long does it take to create one Arabic video ad?"
- "Do you have in-house Arabic content creators?"
- "What's your target audience? (GCC, MENA, Global)"

**Pain Points to Identify**:

- ✅ High production costs ($5K-50K per ad)
- ✅ Slow turnaround (weeks to months)
- ✅ Limited Arabic content creation tools
- ✅ Cultural sensitivity concerns
- ✅ Need for rapid iteration
- ✅ Budget constraints

**Qualification Criteria**:

- **BANT** (Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline)
- **Arabic Content Need**: Must create Arabic video ads
- **Budget**: $500+/month for SMB, $2K+/month for mid-market
- **Timeline**: Need solution within 30-90 days

### Step 2: Demo (20-30 minutes)

**Goal**: Show product value with live Arabic voice-to-video demo

**Demo Structure**:

1. **Opening** (2 min): Set context, understand their use case
2. **Voice Input Demo** (5 min):
    - "Create a luxury watch ad in Arabic"
    - Show Arabic voice recognition
    - Highlight cultural intelligence
3. **AI Co-Director** (5 min):
    - Show talent selection (GCC-appropriate)
    - Script generation (Arabic)
    - Music selection
    - Shot sequences
4. **Generation** (3 min):
    - 60-second generation
    - 4K quality output
    - Brand customization
5. **Marketplace** (5 min):
    - Show buying/selling ads
    - Performance data
    - Creator monetization
6. **Q&A** (5 min): Address concerns, next steps

**Demo Best Practices**:

- ✅ Use their actual product/service in demo
- ✅ Speak in Arabic if customer prefers
- ✅ Show GCC cultural intelligence
- ✅ Highlight cost savings (90% reduction)
- ✅ Show marketplace value (buy pre-made ads)
- ✅ Address compliance concerns (Vision 2030)

### Step 3: Trial (14 days)

**Goal**: Get customer to experience product value

**Trial Setup**:

- **Starter Plan**: Free 14-day trial
- **Onboarding**: 30-minute setup call
- **Support**: Daily check-ins (first 3 days)
- **Resources**: Video tutorials (Arabic + English)

**Trial Success Criteria**:

- ✅ Create 3+ video ads
- ✅ Use Arabic voice input
- ✅ Explore marketplace
- ✅ Share feedback

**Trial Conversion Tactics**:

- **Day 3**: Check-in call, address questions
- **Day 7**: Share case study, success metrics
- **Day 10**: Discuss pricing, next steps
- **Day 14**: Close call, offer discount (20% first 3 months)

### Step 4: Objection Handling

**Common Objections & Responses**:

### "It's too expensive"

**Response**: "I understand budget concerns. Let's calculate ROI:

- Current cost: $20K per ad × 4 ads/quarter = $80K/quarter
- Zero Human: $299/month = $900/quarter
- Savings: $79K/quarter = $316K/year
- ROI: 35,000% return on investment"

### "We need human creativity"

**Response**: "Absolutely! Zero Human enhances human creativity:

- AI handles production (talent, shots, music)
- You focus on strategy and messaging
- Rapid iteration lets you test 10x more creative variations
- Marketplace lets you buy proven high-performing ads"

### "Arabic quality concerns"

**Response**: "We understand this is critical:

- Native Arabic voice recognition (GCC dialects)
- Cultural intelligence (modesty, preferences)
- GCC talent library (culturally appropriate)
- Beta tested with 50+ GCC businesses
- Can I show you Arabic demos from similar companies?"

### "We already have an agency"

**Response**: "Great! Zero Human complements agencies:

- White-label solution for your agency
- Scale production 10x without hiring
- Agency keeps 70-80% of revenue
- Your agency becomes more competitive
- Would you like to introduce us to your agency?"

### "We need to think about it"

**Response**: "Of course! What specific concerns do you have?

- Technical: We can do a deeper technical demo
- Budget: We can customize pricing
- Timeline: We can start with a pilot
- Compliance: We're Vision 2030 aligned
- What would help you make a decision?"

### Step 5: Close (15-20 minutes)

**Goal**: Get signed contract and payment

**Closing Techniques**:

- **Assumptive Close**: "Great! Let's get you set up on the Professional plan..."
- **Urgency Close**: "We're offering 20% discount for early adopters (expires this week)..."
- **Value Close**: "Based on your $80K/quarter spend, you'll save $79K/quarter..."
- **Trial Close**: "You've seen the value in trial, let's make it permanent..."

**Closing Checklist**:

- ✅ Pricing confirmed
- ✅ Plan selected (Starter/Professional/Enterprise)
- ✅ Payment method (credit card/PO)
- ✅ Start date confirmed
- ✅ Onboarding scheduled
- ✅ Contract signed

## Common Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Price Objection

**Customer**: "It's too expensive"
**Response**:

1. Acknowledge: "I understand budget is a concern"
2. Reframe: "Let's calculate ROI"
3. Show value: "Current cost vs. Zero Human cost"
4. Offer: "We can start with Starter plan ($99/month) and upgrade later"

### Scenario 2: Need Approval

**Customer**: "I need to get approval from my manager"
**Response**:

1. Understand: "Who needs to approve this?"
2. Help: "I can join the meeting with your manager"
3. Provide: "Here's a one-pager for your manager"
4. Follow-up: "When can we schedule that meeting?"

### Scenario 3: Competitor Comparison

**Customer**: "We're also looking at [Competitor]"
**Response**:

1. Acknowledge: "That's a great product"
2. Differentiate: "Here's how we're different:
    - Voice-directed (they use text)
    - Arabic-first (they have limited Arabic support)
    - Marketplace (they don't have this)
    - GCC cultural intelligence (they're global, not GCC-focused)"
3. Offer: "Would you like to compare side-by-side?"

### Scenario 4: Technical Concerns

**Customer**: "Will it work with our systems?"
**Response**:

1. Understand: "What systems do you use?"
2. Address: "We integrate with [list integrations]"
3. Offer: "We can do a technical integration call"
4. Reassure: "We have API access for custom integrations"
