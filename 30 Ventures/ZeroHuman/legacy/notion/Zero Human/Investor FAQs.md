> - **What does churn look like in the industry?**
>     - High customer churn rates due to the **constant evolution** and emergence of new AI technologies and platforms.
>     - Churn in the AI industry can be attributed to the **lack of effective customer support** and training, leading to frustration and disengagement.
>     - Churn may occur when **customers find alternative AI solutions** that better meet their specific needs and requirements.
>     - Some customers may churn due to the **high costs associated with AI platforms** and the availability of more affordable options in the market.
>     - Churn can be influenced by the complexity and **steep learning curve** of AI technologies, leading to user abandonment.
>     - **Inadequate integration** capabilities and compatibility issues with existing systems can contribute to customer churn.
>     - Churn may result from a lack of transparency and trust in AI-generated content, especially in terms of **ownership and copyright concerns.**
>     - Customer churn can be driven by **limited customization options** and the inability to adapt AI solutions to unique business requirements.
>     - Churn may occur when customers perceive a **lack of innovation and updates** in AI platforms, leading to a loss of interest and motivation.
>     - In some cases, customers may churn due to **poor user experience** and interface design, making it difficult to navigate and utilize AI tools effectively.

# 🤑 GTM & Sales Motion

> Attract - Serve - Engage
> 
> - **How do you acquire customers?**
>     - Offer a **free trial** of the web product
>     - Implement a **referral program** where existing customers can refer new customers and receive incentives.
>     - Collaborate with **influencers + industry experts** + featuring in popular **newsletters** and startups with **community userbase**
>     - Use targeted online **advertising campaigns** on platforms such as Google AdWords and social media platforms to reach potential customers.
>     - Participate in relevant **industry events and conferences** to showcase the web product and network with potential customers.
>     - Develop **strategic partnerships with other AI companies** or technology providers to cross-promote each other's products and services.
>     - Offer educational resources such as **webinars or tutorials** to educate potential customers about the benefits and features of the web product.
>     - Utilize content marketing strategies such as **blogging** and guest posting on industry websites to establish thought leadership and attract organic traffic.
>     - Engage in **social media marketing** by creating compelling and shareable content related to the web product.
>     - Collaborate with **universities and educational institutions** to offer the web product as a learning tool for students and researchers.
> - What is the CAC by channel?
>     - **Organic Search:** Measure the cost of acquiring customers through search engine optimization (SEO) efforts, such as optimizing website content and keywords to improve organic search rankings.
>     - **Paid Search:** Calculate the cost of acquiring customers through paid search advertising campaigns on search engines like Google, Bing, or Yahoo.
>     - **Social Media Advertising:** Determine the cost of acquiring customers through paid advertising campaigns on social media platforms like Facebook, Instagram, Twitter, or LinkedIn.
>     - **Referral Programs:** Assess the cost of acquiring customers through referral programs, where existing customers refer new customers and receive incentives.
>     - **Influencer Marketing:** Evaluate the cost of acquiring customers through collaborations with influencers or industry experts who promote the product or service to their followers.
>     - **Content Marketing:** Measure the cost of acquiring customers through content marketing efforts, such as creating valuable and informative blog posts, videos, or podcasts.
>     - **Email Marketing:** Calculate the cost of acquiring customers through email marketing campaigns, including the cost of email software, design, and sending.
>     - **Affiliate Marketing:** Evaluate the cost of acquiring customers through affiliate marketing programs, where affiliates promote the product or service and earn a commission for each customer they refer.
>     - **Events and Conferences:** Determine the cost of acquiring customers through participation in industry events and conferences, including booth fees, travel expenses, and promotional materials.
>     - **Partnerships and Joint Ventures:** Assess the cost of acquiring customers through strategic partnerships or joint ventures with other companies, sharing marketing efforts and costs.
