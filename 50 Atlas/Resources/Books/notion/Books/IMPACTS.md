## **🧭 1. Core Philosophy**

Traditional OKRs focus on **goals**.

IMPACTS focuses on **forces** — the energy and structure that make a company evolve.

Each of the 7 dimensions represents one *core force of startup growth*:

| **Force** | **Definition** | **Company Analogy** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **I — Intent** | Clarity of purpose and direction | The company’s North Star |
| **M — Milestones** | Concrete checkpoints toward intent | The company’s map |
| **P — Progress** | Rate of forward movement and completion | The company’s engine |
| **A — Alignment** | How synchronized the team and decisions are | The company’s nervous system |
| **C — Compounding** | How much work creates future leverage | The company’s flywheel |
| **T — Traction** | Market validation and external momentum | The company’s reputation and pull |
| **S — Signal** | Quality of insights and feedback loops | The company’s sensory system |

---

## **🧩 2. The Framework — How It Works**

IMPACTS turns the company into a **dynamic feedback loop**:

1. **Intent** defines *why* you exist.
2. **Milestones** define *what* you’re doing next.
3. **Progress** measures *how far* you’ve moved.
4. **Alignment** ensures *everyone’s moving in sync.*
5. **Compounding** ensures *today’s work makes tomorrow easier.*
6. **Traction** shows *the market is responding.*
7. **Signal** feeds *what to learn and adapt next.*

## **⚙️ 3. How Each Force Works (With Examples)**

Let’s go deep into each one.

---

### **🧠**

### **I — Intent (The North Star)**

**Purpose:** Define what you are trying to create, why, and how it evolves.

**Key Inputs**

- Vision statement (1–2 sentences)
- Quarterly intent: “This cycle, our company’s intent is to…”
- Founder narrative clarity score (AI checks coherence across content, actions, and focus)

**Example**

> Intent: “Empower 10,000 early-stage founders to launch faster with AI copilots.”
> 

> 
> 

> Supporting signals:
> 
- Internal comms consistency (team knows direction)
- No conflicting initiatives
- AI Coach says: 87/100 clarity

**Output:** *Intent Clarity Score (ICS)*

→ used to anchor all other forces.

---

### **🧭**

### **M — Milestones (The Map)**

**Purpose:** Translate Intent into measurable checkpoints that create structure.

**Key Inputs**

- 3–5 major milestones per quarter
- Each milestone has timeline, owner, and dependencies
- AI tags milestones to IMPACTS forces (e.g., “Launch Beta” = Progress + Traction)

**Example**

| **Milestone** | **Owner** | **Category** | **Deadline** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Beta launch ready | Product | Progress, Traction | Dec 15 |
| Hire 2 senior engineers | HR | Alignment, Compounding | Nov 30 |
| Secure 3 pilot clients | Sales | Traction | Jan 5 |

**Output:** *Milestone Momentum Score (MMS)*

→ measures completion velocity and on-time delivery.

---

### **⚙️**

### **P — Progress (The Engine)**

**Purpose:** Quantify movement — how effectively the team is advancing.

**Key Inputs**

- Task velocity (e.g., tasks closed / week)
- Feature or deliverable completion %
- Revenue or user delta (% change per week)
- AI anomaly detection (e.g., “velocity drop after sprint 3”)

**Example**

- Week 1 → 20% product readiness
- Week 4 → 65%
- Week 8 → 95%
    
    → Progress Score: 88/100
    

**Output:** *Progress Efficiency Score (PES)*

= Completed Deliverables / Planned Deliverables × (Quality Factor)

---

### **💫**

### **A — Alignment (The Nervous System)**

**Purpose:** Keep every person, project, and decision aligned with Intent.

**Key Inputs**

- Weekly pulse check (1–10 alignment rating by team)
- Cross-department OKR overlap %
- Communication latency (avg response time on key threads)
- Meeting focus (AI analysis of transcripts → % on-topic)

**Example**

> Pulse survey shows 9/10 average alignment.
> 

> AI flags that product and marketing goals diverged on GTM timeline.
> 

> After sync, realigned, raising score from 75 → 88.
> 

**Output:** *Alignment Coherence Index (ACI)*

---

### **🔁**

### **C — Compounding (The Flywheel)**

**Purpose:** Track how much of your current work makes future work faster, cheaper, or better.

**Key Inputs**

- % of reusable assets (code, templates, automations)
- % of playbooks documented
- Time-to-repeat (how fast recurring tasks are done)
- AI “Leverage Factor” (identifies projects producing exponential payoff)

**Example**

- Built internal onboarding automation → saved 12 hours/week.
- Created GTM playbook reused in 3 launches.
- Compounding Score: 82/100.
    
    > → AI predicts future output will rise 1.6× with same team size.
    > 

**Output:** *Leverage Index (LI)*

= Compounding Value Created / Resources Used

---

### **🚀**

### **T — Traction (The Pull)**

**Purpose:** Measure the market’s response — proof that your engine works.

**Key Inputs**

- Growth metrics (revenue, users, MRR, etc.)
- Conversion rates
- NPS / CSAT scores
- Social or PR signals
- Investor interest / funding progress

**Example**

| **Metric** | **Before** | **After** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| MRR | $15K | $22K |
| Users | 1,200 | 2,100 |
| NPS | 42 | 61 |

→ Traction Index: 90/100

**Output:** *Traction Momentum Index (TMI)*

= Weighted combination of growth, sentiment, and engagement.

---

### **📡**

### **S — Signal (The Sensory System)**

**Purpose:** Close the loop — how quickly and accurately the company learns and adapts.

**Key Inputs**

- Data coverage (% of decisions informed by live data)
- Feedback frequency (customer + internal)
- Experimentation cadence (tests / month)
- Decision lag time
- Signal noise ratio (how many signals acted on vs ignored)

**Example**

> Customer feedback integration doubled (8 → 16/week).
> 

> Added analytics dashboard → real-time product usage insights.
> 

> Team executed 4 A/B tests per month.
> 

> → Signal Score: 92/100 (strong sensing culture).
> 

**Output:** *Signal Sensitivity Score (SSS)*
