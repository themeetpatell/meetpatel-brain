# Play Bigger — Ramadan, Peterson, Lochhead, Maney

## Why it matters to Meet

The book that codified *Category Design* as a strategic discipline. The thesis: in any category, the *category king* captures 70–80% of the value. So the right strategic move isn't to compete inside an existing category — it's to design and name a new category, then position yourself as its king. Directly relevant to StartupOS (potentially a new category) and Biggdate (potential category re-design within dating).

## One-line thesis

The companies that win don't compete in existing categories — they design new categories, name the problem in new terms, and become the category king who captures most of the resulting value.

## Core ideas

1. **Category Kings rule.** In any meaningful category, one company captures 70–80% of the total market value. Being #2 in a category is *much* worse than being #1 in a smaller, well-defined one. Strategy = pick or build the category where you can be king.

2. **Frame, name, claim.** (a) Frame the problem in a new way that highlights the cost of the status quo. (b) Name the new category with language the market adopts. (c) Claim the position with explicit category-king positioning.

3. **Point of View (POV).** The category king has a strong, articulated point of view about the world. Not "we're a better X" but "X itself is the wrong frame; here's the new frame".

4. **Lightning Strike.** A coordinated, intense launch moment that establishes category leadership in the public imagination. Not a slow build — a sudden, dominant claim.

5. **Conditioning the Market.** Category kings consistently educate the market about the new category — through content, events, references, executive briefings. This is *ongoing*, not a one-time launch.

6. **Mission Amplifiers.** Customers, partners, analysts, press who repeat the category narrative because they believe it. Build these deliberately.

## Killer quotes

> *"In tech and in many other industries, the company that designs a category and develops it almost always becomes its category king."*

> *"Differentiation thinking misses the point. You're not trying to be a different X — you're trying to be the only one of a new thing."*

> *"The category gets stuck in people's minds first. The product gets bought second."*

> *"Conditioning is the long, slow work of getting the world to see the problem through your lens."*

## How to apply

### StartupOS — potential category design

- **Existing category:** "AI tools for founders" / "productivity for startups" / "founder tools"
- **Potential new category:** *Founder OS* / *AI Cofounder* / *Operating Intelligence for Founders*
- **Frame:** the problem isn't "founders need more tools". The problem is founders make better decisions when they have a thinking partner — and the cost of operating *alone* is the silent killer of pre-PMF companies. Frame the cost of "alone".
- **Name:** "Founder OS" might be too generic. "AI Cofounder" might be more potent (and more controversial = more memorable).
- **Claim:** Meet's public writing positions him AS the person defining this category. Don't compete with Notion. Define a new category.

### Biggdate — category re-design

- **Existing category:** "dating apps"
- **Potential new category:** *Relationship Intelligence Platforms* / *Intentional Matchmaking* / *Long-form Compatibility*
- **Frame:** the problem isn't "more matches". The problem is *time-to-real-connection*. Dating apps measure swipes; the right metric is months-to-meaningful-relationship.
- **Caution:** category design in consumer requires bigger lightning strike and longer conditioning than B2B. Biggdate may not be ready for full category design until post-launch traction.

### Finanshels — category challenge

- Finanshels is currently positioned inside the "accounting platforms" category. Hard to be king of accounting (incumbents own it).
- Potential category re-design: *AI-Native Operating Stack for UAE SMBs* — bundles accounting + tax + AML + audit + advisory as one operating function, not separate services.
- The category king of "AI-native SMB ops" in UAE/GCC could be Finanshels if claimed deliberately.

### Public content angle

- Meet's LinkedIn posting is part of category conditioning. Use it deliberately:
  - Write about the *category* not the *product* most of the time
  - Define the problem in language that's slightly new
  - Repeat the frame consistently over months — conditioning compounds

## Where the book is wrong (or limited)

- The "category king captures 70-80%" claim is true for many categories but not all. Some categories are healthier with 2–3 strong players (consumer goods, regional markets). Don't over-apply.
- Lightning Strike works in tech-PR-heavy markets. Less applicable in regulated industries (Finanshels) where slow trust-building beats sudden launches.
- The book occasionally crosses into MBA-jargon territory ("legendary marketing", "the legendary company"). Cut through the language; the underlying principles are real.
- Category design without product-market fit is just naming exercises. PMF first, category design second (or in parallel).
