# Working Backwards — Colin Bryar & Bill Carr

## Why it matters to Meet

The operational playbook for how Amazon actually works — written by two long-term insiders. Not "Bezos shareholder letter" but the unglamorous internal mechanisms: PR/FAQs, narratives instead of slides, the bar raiser hiring process, the single-threaded leader. These mechanisms are why Amazon shipped consistently for 20 years. They are imitable. Meet's portfolio benefits hugely from adopting two or three of them.

## One-line thesis

Amazon's success is not a CEO; it's a set of mechanisms — and the mechanisms are documented, transferable, and underused in most companies.

## Core ideas

1. **Working Backwards (PR/FAQ).** Before building, write the launch press release. If you can't make a compelling PR, the idea isn't ready. See [[Strategy/Working Backwards|Working Backwards framework]] for the full method.

2. **No slides — narratives.** Amazon banned PowerPoint at senior meetings. Replaced with 6-page narrative memos. First 20 minutes of every meeting = silent reading. Then discussion. The narrative format forces clarity slides hide.

3. **Single-Threaded Leader (STL).** Each significant initiative gets ONE leader whose only job is that initiative. Not "in addition to their other work" — only that work. Solves the problem of important things never shipping because everyone's also doing day-job work.

4. **Bar Raiser hiring.** Every hire includes a "bar raiser" — a trained interviewer from outside the hiring team — who has veto power. Their job: ensure this hire raises the bar of the team. Prevents teams from hiring "good enough" candidates because they need the headcount.

5. **Input metrics vs Output metrics.** Output metrics (revenue, profit) are the result of input metrics (price, selection, availability, customer experience). Teams should obsess over input metrics they control, not output metrics they don't.

6. **Two-pizza teams.** No team larger than what two pizzas can feed (~8 people). Larger teams = coordination tax exceeds output gain.

## How to apply

### Finanshels — adopt these now

- **PR/FAQ before any new product / initiative.** Before the AI demand engine launches, write the PR/FAQ. See draft in [[Strategy/Working Backwards|the framework note]].
- **Single-Threaded Leader for the AI demand engine.** Don't let someone "also own" it on top of other work. One leader. Only that.
- **Narrative memos for L10 IDS items.** Instead of "let's discuss" with slides, write a 2-page memo on the issue. Distribute 24 hours in advance. Silent reading at the meeting. Decision in 30 minutes.
- **Bar Raiser for CoE senior hires.** Designate one trusted external operator as the Bar Raiser. Their veto holds.

### StartupOS — adopt these for the wedge phase

- **PR/FAQ for each wedge candidate.** Writing the PR forces the wedge to be sharp. The wedge that produces the best PR wins.
- **Input metrics framing.** Don't fixate on "ARR" — fixate on "founders who logged a strategic decision in week 1 of using StartupOS". Input metric. Move it, ARR follows.

### Cross-portfolio

- **Two-pizza teams as a hiring constraint.** As any venture grows past 8, split rather than scale.
- **Single-Threaded Leader** as the standard structure for major initiatives, not the exception.

## Where the book is wrong (or limited)

- Heavily focused on operational scale. Less useful pre-PMF — the mechanisms assume you have something working that needs to be scaled.
- Bar Raiser process assumes you have enough hires to develop the role. For a 5-person venture, this is overkill.
- Some Amazon mechanisms (Andon Cord, Two-Way Door) are mentioned but under-treated — Meet should reach for original Amazon shareholder letters too.
