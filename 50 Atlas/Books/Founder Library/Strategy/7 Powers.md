# 7 Powers — Hamilton Helmer

## Why it matters to Meet

The single most rigorous book on competitive strategy written this century. Helmer is a working strategy investor (Capital Group, Strategy Capital) — not an academic. The book defines, with mathematical precision, *exactly* what creates persistent differential returns. Most pitches that claim to have a moat have no power. Meet's portfolio — especially StartupOS and Biggdate — needs to be evaluated against this. The Acquired podcast made the book canonical in the founder community for good reason.

## One-line thesis

A "Power" is a condition that creates persistent, differential returns. There are exactly seven, no more. Without a power, your business is a feature waiting to be commoditized.

## Core ideas

(Each Power covered in detail in the [[Strategy/7 Powers|7 Powers framework note]] — this note is the *book* level: how Helmer thinks about the topic.)

1. **Power = Benefit + Barrier.** A real Power must create real value (Benefit) AND prevent competitors from arbitraging it away (Barrier). Both required. Most "moats" claim Benefit without Barrier.

2. **The 7 Powers are exhaustive.** Helmer's claim: every durable competitive advantage maps to one (or more) of: Scale Economies, Network Economies, Counter-Positioning, Switching Costs, Branding, Cornered Resource, Process Power. If you claim something else, it's actually one of these in disguise, or it's not durable.

3. **Strategy = the study of Powers.** Strategy isn't planning. Strategy isn't vision. Strategy is the discipline of identifying which Power you can build and constructing the business around it. Everything else is operations.

4. **Power develops at different points in the company lifecycle.** Counter-Positioning is usually born at company founding (incumbents can't adopt your model without cannibalizing their existing business). Scale Economies, Network Economies, Switching Costs typically develop in the takeoff stage. Branding, Process Power develop slowly in the stability stage.

5. **You can't reverse-engineer Power.** Trying to add a Power after the business is built rarely works. The Power has to be designed into the business at the architectural level.

## Killer quotes

> *"Strategy is the study of fundamental conditions that lead to persistent and meaningful differentials in business returns."*

> *"A barrier is a condition that prevents arbitrage of the benefit by competitors. Without a barrier, the benefit will be competed away."*

> *"In a world without barriers, returns equal the cost of capital."*

> *"The strategist must always ask: 'In what conceivable way can what we are doing produce persistent differential returns?'"*

## How to apply

### Finanshels — Powers audit

Already covered in [[Strategy/7 Powers|the framework note]]. Net read:
- **Strong:** Counter-Positioning (traditional firms can't go down-market without cannibalizing enterprise margin), Switching Costs (books + history + AML records lock in)
- **Emerging:** Scale Economies, Process Power (CoE methodology)
- **Strategic move:** deepen Counter-Positioning by going further down-market than incumbents can rationally follow

### StartupOS — Powers audit

- Most likely Powers: Process Power (the operating methodology IS the product), Switching Costs (data + context lock-in)
- **Critical:** if the answer is "we'll have network effects eventually", that's not a Power yet. Network economies in B2B SaaS are slow and uncertain. Don't bet the wedge on it.

### Biggdate — Powers audit

- Network Economies are the *category default* but saturated
- **Branding + Counter-Positioning** is the asymmetric play (paid-only = counter-position; dignity-led brand = branding power)

### Cross-portfolio rule

- Every quarterly portfolio review: explicitly score each venture on which Powers are present, emerging, or absent
- A venture with no clear path to any Power is a candidate for "kill or pivot to find one"
- Use this as the strategic question, not "is the team executing"

## Where the book is wrong (or limited)

- Helmer treats Power as relatively binary (present/absent). Real-world Powers come in degrees. Use his framework as a *direction*, not as a checklist.
- Light on *how to build* a Power — strong on identifying it. Pair with [[Strategy/Working Backwards|Working Backwards]] and [[Strategy/Crossing the Chasm|Crossing the Chasm]] for the building-side.
- Counter-Positioning chapter is brilliant but underdeveloped — for founders, it's the most actionable Power and deserves its own book.
