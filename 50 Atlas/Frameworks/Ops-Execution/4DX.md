# 4DX — 4 Disciplines of Execution

> Whirlwind kills strategy. Most organizations don't fail at strategy — they fail at executing strategy *while running the day-to-day*. 4DX is a discipline for getting one or two genuinely important things done *despite* the whirlwind.

## When to use it
- A specific goal keeps slipping ("we never get to that initiative")
- A team that's busy but not advancing strategic objectives
- Personal projects that compete with operating work (Meet's vault upgrade is a 4DX-able goal)
- When OKRs feel too abstract and you need a tighter execution layer underneath

## The 4 disciplines

### Discipline 1 — Focus on the Wildly Important
- Pick **one** WIG (Wildly Important Goal) per team
- Format: *From X to Y by When* — must be measurable
- Examples: *"From $X to $Y MRR by Sept 30"* / *"From 0 to 50 weekly active founders by Dec 31"*
- One WIG. Not 3. Not "and also". The whole point is sacrificing optionality for execution.

### Discipline 2 — Act on Lead Measures
- **Lag measures** = the WIG itself (revenue, users)
- **Lead measures** = behaviors that *predict* the lag
- Lead measures must be: (a) predictive, (b) influenceable by the team
- Examples:
  - Lag: MRR. Lead: number of qualified sales conversations per week
  - Lag: weekly active founders. Lead: founders onboarded who log ≥1 decision in week 1
  - Lag: published posts. Lead: drafts started per week

The team's daily attention goes to **lead measures**, not lag. The lag follows.

### Discipline 3 — Keep a Compelling Scoreboard
- Simple, visible, owned by the team
- Both lead and lag measures on it
- Updated weekly minimum
- The test: can someone glance at it and immediately know "are we winning?"
- Format: not a spreadsheet — a poster, a wall, a dashboard. *Visible*.

### Discipline 4 — Create a Cadence of Accountability
- Weekly WIG session (~20 min)
- Each person/team: (a) reports last week's commitments, (b) reviews scoreboard, (c) makes one specific commitment for next week to move lead measures
- Same time, same format, every week
- Misses are surfaced and addressed, not papered over

## How to apply

### Finanshels (alongside EOS)

EOS handles the operating layer. 4DX layers on top of the highest-priority Rock.

Example WIG: *"From 18-hour average lead response time to 30 minutes by Sept 30."*

Lead measures the CoE team commits to weekly:
- Number of WhatsApp leads triaged within 4 minutes
- Number of AI-bot-handled leads with sales-accept rating ≥4/5
- Number of manual quality reviews completed on AI-handled leads

Scoreboard: visible in CoE workspace, updated weekly Monday. WIG session: 20 min after the L10.

### Meet personally — vault godmode rollout as WIG

**WIG:** *"From Phase 1 complete to Phase 2 Founder Library + Framework Atlas + UAE-GCC Playbook + Competitive Atlas all shipped by [date]."*

**Lead measures:**
- Frameworks distilled per week (target: 6)
- Books distilled per week (target: 5)
- Competitor dossiers shipped per week (target: 3)
- UAE plays shipped per week (target: 3)

**Scoreboard:** counter at top of `_AI/Contexts/current-state.md`

**Cadence:** check-in every weekly review

### StartupOS — pre-launch WIG

**WIG:** *"From wedge undecided to wedge chosen + first 25 beta founders onboarded by Sept 15."*

Lead measures:
- Customer-development interviews completed per week (target: 5)
- Drafted wedge decision-frame iterations (target: 1/week)
- Beta founders onboarded (target: 4/week starting week 6)

## Anti-patterns
- **More than one WIG per team.** Defeats the entire framework.
- **Lag measures as lead measures.** "Revenue this week" is not a lead measure — it's just the WIG decomposed. Lead measures are *behaviors* you can control today.
- **Invisible scoreboards.** Spreadsheets people don't open daily ≠ scoreboards.
- **WIG sessions that drift.** 20 min, structured, every week, same time. Treat like a non-negotiable.
- **Confusing 4DX with "general productivity".** 4DX is specifically for strategic execution *despite* operating whirlwind. Don't apply it to day-to-day work.

## 4DX + other frameworks

| Together | Combination |
|---|---|
| With OKRs | Each Objective gets a 4DX WIG layer underneath it |
| With EOS | The top-priority Rock becomes the 4DX WIG |

| With Bullseye | When testing channels, lead measures = channel-specific behaviors |
