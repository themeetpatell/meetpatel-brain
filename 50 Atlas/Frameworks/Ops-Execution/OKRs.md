# OKRs — Objectives and Key Results

> Objectives = what we're going to achieve (aspirational, qualitative). Key Results = how we'll know we did it (measurable, quantitative). Used right, OKRs align an org without micromanagement. Used wrong, they become annual review theater.

## When to use it
- Setting quarterly goals for a venture
- Aligning a team of 5–500 around shared priorities
- Personal goal-setting (yes, individual OKRs work)
- Founder-level focus discipline

## The framework

### Objective
- 3–5 words, verb-led, aspirational, qualitative
- Inspires the team without being measurable on its own
- *"Own the UAE SMB accounting category"* — Objective
- *"Become the trusted founder OS"* — Objective

### Key Results (3–5 per Objective)
- Measurable, time-bound, outcome-based (not activity-based)
- *"Hit $X ARR from SMB tier by Sept 30"* — KR
- *"Reach 1,000 weekly active founders by Dec 31"* — KR
- *"NOT: 'Launch new feature'"* — that's an activity, not an outcome

### Stretch principle
- KRs should sit at 60–70% confidence
- If you'd hit them easily, the bar is too low
- If you'd never hit them, they demoralize
- Hitting 70% of stretch KRs ≈ excellent quarter; 100% = bar was too low

### Cadence
- **Quarterly OKR setting** — leadership level
- **Weekly check-in** — pull KR progress; surface at-risk early
- **Quarterly review** — score 0.0–1.0 per KR, average per Objective
- **Annual** — strategic refresh

## How to apply

### Finanshels — example quarterly OKRs

**Objective 1: Lock the AI demand engine as the default acquisition channel**

- KR1: Move 60% of inbound leads through the AI engine (currently 0%) by Sept 30
- KR2: Maintain sales-acceptance rate of AI-qualified leads at ≥85%
- KR3: Reduce average lead response time from 18h to 30min

**Objective 2: Scale the CoE without scaling proportional headcount**

- KR1: Hire 2 pre-sales analysts; 90-day ramp completion by EOQ
- KR2: Reduce average cost-per-qualified-lead by 25%
- KR3: Increase customer count per CoE FTE by 30%

**Objective 3: Lock channel partner motion**

- KR1: Sign 15 active channel partners (≥1 referral/month)
- KR2: 25% of new customers via channel partner by Sept 30
- KR3: Partner-sourced LTV ≥120% of direct LTV (validates the model)

### StartupOS — example founder-only OKRs (pre-team)

**Objective: Lock the StartupOS wedge and ship minimum proof**

- KR1: Decision frame complete + signed off by July 15 — wedge chosen
- KR2: Minimum lovable demo shipped to 25 beta founders by Sept 15
- KR3: 12 founders log ≥3 strategic decisions in the OS within 30 days of access

### Personal — example founder OKRs (Meet)

**Objective: Activate public founder brand**

- KR1: 36 LinkedIn posts shipped (3/week × 12 weeks)
- KR2: Newsletter at 1,000 subscribers (currently [X])
- KR3: 4 strategic conversations / month from inbound (investors, founders, ops talent)

## Anti-patterns
- **KRs as task lists.** "Launch the feature" is not a KR. "1,000 paying users from the feature in 90 days" is.
- **Too many Objectives.** 3 max per venture per quarter. 5+ = no priority.
- **OKRs tied to performance reviews.** Kills the stretch principle — people sandbag. Keep OKRs separate from comp.
- **Setting and forgetting.** Weekly check-ins are the discipline. Without them, OKRs decay by week 4.
- **Cascading OKRs top-down without team input.** OKRs work when teams *own* their KRs. Imposed OKRs feel like budget targets.
- **Mistaking activities for results.** "Conduct 20 customer interviews" = activity. "Validate 3 specific pain hypotheses from interviews" = result.

## OKRs vs EOS Rocks

| | OKRs | EOS Rocks |
|---|---|---|
| **Best for** | Product/PM teams, AI-native ventures, growth orgs | Operationally heavy small businesses |
| **Granularity** | More structured (Obj + 3-5 KRs) | Simpler (Rock = priority + completion criteria) |
| **Cadence** | Quarterly + weekly | Quarterly + weekly L10s |
| **Strength** | Outcome-focus, stretch | Simplicity, team-wide adoption |

For Finanshels: EOS Rocks at the CoE team level, OKRs at the strategic initiative level (e.g., AI demand engine has OKRs).
