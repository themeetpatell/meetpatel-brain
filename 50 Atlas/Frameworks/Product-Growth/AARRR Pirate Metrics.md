# AARRR — Pirate Metrics

> Five letters that describe every customer's journey through your product. Old framework, still works, because the five stages map cleanly to where most growth problems hide.

## When to use it
- Diagnosing where in the funnel growth is breaking
- Setting up first measurement system for a new venture
- Quarterly review: which stage is the bottleneck?
- Onboarding a growth team — common vocabulary

## The framework — 5 stages

1. **Acquisition** — how do users find you? Channel × cost × volume.
2. **Activation** — first valuable experience. Did they reach "aha"?
3. **Retention** — do they come back? Day 1, 7, 30, 90 cohorts.
4. **Revenue** — do they pay? How much? When?
5. **Referral** — do they bring others? Viral coefficient, NPS, word-of-mouth.

Diagnostic rule: **fix retention before chasing acquisition.** Leaky bucket math. Pouring water into a bucket with holes is malpractice.

## How to apply

### Finanshels — AARRR snapshot
- **Acquisition** — WhatsApp inbound (best), paid social (4× weaker), referrals (strong but under-systematized), partners (early)
- **Activation** — defined as: first month of clean books delivered + first VAT/AML filing submitted on time. Currently fuzzy.
- **Retention** — sticky once activated (switching costs). Real risk is month 0–3 churn before activation.
- **Revenue** — monthly recurring, with upsell paths (advisory, AML setup, audit prep)
- **Referral** — informal, channel-partner motion emerging. Not yet a system.

**Biggest leak right now:** likely activation. Customers sign up but the first 30 days are make-or-break. Fix activation = fix everything downstream.

### Biggdate (pre-launch projection)
- **Acquisition** — founder-led brand + creator partnerships + diaspora communities
- **Activation** — defined as: verified profile + first 3 quality matches in week 1
- **Retention** — measured weekly active members
- **Revenue** — paid-only model; no free tier blurs revenue with activation
- **Referral** — branded artifacts + storytelling around outcomes

### StartupOS (pre-launch projection)
- **Acquisition** — Meet's public writing + founder communities
- **Activation** — defined as: first strategic decision logged within 14 days
- **Retention** — weekly active founders using the OS for ≥1 decision
- **Revenue** — paid subscription, opinionated pricing band
- **Referral** — founder-to-founder, especially via Meet's network

## Anti-patterns
- **Chasing acquisition while retention is broken.** Cardinal sin. The leaky bucket.
- **Defining "activation" as account creation.** Activation = realized value, not signup.
- **Ignoring referral because it's "soft".** Referral is the cheapest acquisition channel — measure it.
- **Treating AARRR as 5 disconnected dashboards.** They feed each other. The diagnosis is in the *ratios* between stages.

## Diagnostic ratios worth tracking
- Acquisition → Activation: what % of signups reach aha?
- Activation → Retention: what % of activated users come back at day 7? Day 30?
- Retention → Revenue: what's the time from activation to first paid?
- Revenue → Referral: what % of paying customers refer at least one other?
