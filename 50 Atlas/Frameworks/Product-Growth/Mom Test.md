# The Mom Test (Customer Development)

> Customer interviews fail because people lie politely. Your mom will tell you your business idea is great. The Mom Test is a set of interview rules that surface truth — by asking about the user's *past behavior*, not their *future opinions*.

## When to use it
- Customer discovery for a new venture
- Validating a pivot
- Diagnosing why customers say one thing and do another
- Pre-launch market validation
- Post-launch — talking to churned users

## The three rules

1. **Talk about their life, not your idea.** Don't pitch. Ask what they already do.
2. **Ask about specifics in the past, not generic opinions about the future.** "When was the last time...?" not "Would you use...?"
3. **Talk less, listen more.** If you're talking, you're not learning. 80% of the conversation is them.

## Bad questions vs Good questions

| Bad (gets compliments) | Good (gets truth) |
|---|---|
| "Do you think this is a good idea?" | "Walk me through the last time you tried to do X." |
| "Would you use a product that does Y?" | "What's the most frustrating part of how you do Y today?" |
| "Would you pay $50/month for this?" | "How do you currently spend money trying to solve this?" |
| "What features would you want?" | "Tell me what happened the last time this problem hit you." |
| "Is X a problem for you?" | "What did you do about X last time it came up?" |

## How to apply

### Finanshels — customer interview script
Don't ask: *"Would you use an AI-powered accounting platform for UAE SMBs?"*

Do ask:
- "Walk me through what happened around your last VAT filing."
- "Who do you currently work with for your books?"
- "What was the last time you missed a filing deadline? What happened?"
- "How much do you currently spend on accounting, all-in?"
- "What's the most painful part of running compliance in the UAE?"

The answers tell you the real ICP, real pain, real willingness to switch, real budget.

### Biggdate — pre-launch interview script
Don't ask: *"Would you use a high-intent dating app?"*

Do ask:
- "Tell me about your last 3 months on dating apps."
- "When was the last time you went on a date from an app? How did it go?"
- "How long did you stay on each app before deleting it?"
- "How much have you spent on premium dating subscriptions in the last year?"
- "Have you ever paid for anything *adjacent* — matchmaking, coaching, events?"

### StartupOS — interview founder users
Don't ask: *"Would you want an AI Chief of Staff?"*

Do ask:
- "Tell me about the last major decision you made as a founder. How did you make it?"
- "Who did you talk to before deciding?"
- "What tools or notes did you use?"
- "What part of running the company do you wish you had a co-founder for?"
- "When was the last time you felt 'I need someone to think this through with me'? What did you actually do?"

## Anti-patterns
- **Pitching during discovery.** The moment you describe your product, the interview is dead.
- **Asking hypothetical "would you" questions.** People lie. Past behavior is the only honest signal.
- **Confirmation bias listening.** You hear what supports your idea. Have someone else read the transcript.
- **Asking opinions of strangers.** Opinions are cheap. Behavior is expensive.
- **Stopping at 5 interviews.** 15+ before you trust the pattern.

## What to capture after each interview

1. **Pain language** — exact words they used to describe the problem
2. **Workarounds** — what they currently do to solve it (a real workaround proves the pain is real)
3. **Money already being spent** — even adjacent spend signals willingness to pay
4. **Trigger events** — what causes them to actively search for a solution
5. **Quotes** — verbatim, for later use in copy + positioning
