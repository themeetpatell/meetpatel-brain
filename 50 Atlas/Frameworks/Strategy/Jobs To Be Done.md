# Jobs To Be Done (JTBD)

> Customers don't buy products. They *hire* products to make progress in a specific situation. Find the job, you find the buyer.

## When to use it
- Designing a new product
- Re-positioning an existing one ("nobody's buying" or "wrong people are buying")
- Customer interviews — to get past stated preferences and find the real trigger
- Pricing — what is the job worth to the customer?

## The framework (4 steps)

1. **Find the job.** What progress is the customer trying to make in their life? Not the feature they want — the *outcome they're seeking* and the *struggle they're escaping*.
2. **Map the four forces:** (a) push of the current situation, (b) pull of the new solution, (c) anxiety about switching, (d) habit/inertia of the status quo. The customer switches only when push + pull > anxiety + habit.
3. **Identify the "hiring moment."** The specific situation that triggered the search. ("I was preparing my Q3 books and realized I had no way to file VAT cleanly.")
4. **Define the success criteria** from the customer's perspective — not from yours. What does "the job got done well" look like for them?

## How to apply

### Finanshels
- **Job:** "Help me stop worrying about VAT/AML/audit so I can focus on running my business."
- **Hiring moment:** First-time owner-founder who just got a tax notice OR scaling SMB past first finance hire OR foreign founder confused by UAE compliance.
- **Push:** "I keep missing filings" / "My current accountant is unresponsive" / "I don't trust the numbers"
- **Pull:** "Calm, competent, on top of it"
- **Anxiety about switching:** "Will the new firm handle the handover cleanly? Will they understand my business?"
- **Implication for positioning:** Sell *calm + competence*, not "AI accounting platform". The AI is invisible. The job is peace of mind.

### Biggdate
- **Job:** "Help me find a long-term partner without the exhaustion of swiping or being judged on photos alone."
- **Hiring moment:** After a bad breakup, or after 6 months on Hinge/Bumble with nothing real, or after a 30th birthday clarifying intent.
- **Implication:** The brand sells *seriousness* and *dignity*, not "more matches".

### StartupOS
- **Job:** "Help me operate like a team of 50 when I'm a team of 5."
- **Hiring moment:** Founder hits the wall — too many decisions, too many tools, no thinking partner.
- **Implication:** Position as a *cofounder substitute*, not "AI tools for startups".

## Anti-patterns
- Confusing features with jobs ("our customers love the dashboard" — the dashboard isn't the job)
- Asking customers "what do you want" instead of "tell me about the last time you tried to do X"
- Defining the job in *your* category language instead of the customer's life language
