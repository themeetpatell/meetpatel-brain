# Working Backwards (Amazon PR/FAQ)

> Write the press release before you write the code. If you can't make a compelling launch announcement, the thing isn't worth building. Most strategy fails not because of execution, but because the underlying idea wouldn't make a real customer care.

## When to use it
- New product or feature proposal
- New venture brief (paired with [[_AI/Workflows/new-venture-brief|new-venture-brief workflow]])
- Pivot consideration — write the PR for the pivot, see if it lands
- Investor pitch sharpening — if your pitch can't fit the PR/FAQ structure, the pitch is mush

## The framework (4 documents, in this order)

1. **The Press Release** — written as if launching today. Headline + sub-headline + customer quote + problem + solution + how-it-works + endorsement quote + call-to-action. **Max one page.**

2. **The FAQ** — internal (engineering, ops, finance) + external (customer, press) questions. Forces honesty about hard parts. 5–15 questions.

3. **Visuals / mocks (optional)** — what the experience looks like. Low-fi sketches are fine.

4. **Customer experience walkthrough** — narrate what the customer actually does, end to end, in present tense.

The discipline: if you can't write a compelling PR, the idea isn't ready. *Don't build.*

## How to apply

### Example PR — Finanshels AI Demand Engine (hypothetical launch)

**Headline:** *Finanshels Launches AI Pre-Sales Engine — Cuts SMB Lead Response Time From 18 Hours to 4 Minutes*

**Sub-headline:** New WhatsApp-native engine qualifies, schedules, and onboards UAE SMB accounting leads automatically — freeing 60% of pre-sales team hours.

**Customer quote:** *"We responded to a Finanshels ad on Saturday at 2am from a Ramadan night-shift. By 2:04am, our books were scoped and a CoE analyst was assigned. We signed Monday."* — Owner, UAE F&B SMB, [size].

**Problem:** UAE SMBs need accounting answers fast, often outside business hours. Traditional firms respond in days. Even high-velocity SMB firms respond in hours. Most leads die in the gap.

**Solution:** Finanshels AI Demand Engine triages every WhatsApp + form lead in under 4 minutes, qualifies the fit, scopes the engagement, and schedules a human onboarding call — at any hour.

**How it works:** [3 sentences on the actual mechanism]

**Endorsement:** [Real-named partner or customer]

**CTA:** WhatsApp +971 [number] to test it now.

The act of writing this forces clarity on: what does "win" actually look like, what's the customer benefit in plain words, what's the proof.

### Pre-build test (any venture)
Before committing to a major build, write the PR. If the PR is boring, vague, or has no quotable customer benefit — the idea isn't ready. Re-scope.

## Anti-patterns
- **Writing the PR with internal language.** "Leveraging our proprietary AI platform" is a tell. Real PRs talk customer benefit in customer words.
- **Skipping the FAQ.** The FAQ is where the strategy gets stress-tested. Skipping it = skipping strategy.
- **Writing the PR after building.** Reverses the discipline. The whole point is to write it first.
- **Making the PR aspirational instead of present-tense.** "Will eventually" / "in the future" weaknesses the forcing function.
