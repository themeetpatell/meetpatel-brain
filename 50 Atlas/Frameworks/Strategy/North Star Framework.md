# North Star Framework

> One metric, chosen carefully, that captures the value customers get *and* aligns every team's work. Pick wrong and you'll optimize the company toward the wrong outcome for years.

## When to use it
- Setting up a new venture's measurement system
- Resolving disagreement on "what are we actually optimizing for"
- Quarterly review when you notice teams are pulling in different directions
- Pre-fundraise — what's the one number you'll tell investors about every month

## The framework

A North Star Metric (NSM) must satisfy three tests:

1. **Captures the value customers receive** — not just usage or revenue, but actual realized value
2. **Reflects long-term value** — moves up when the business is healthier 12 months out
3. **Is leading, not lagging** — predicts revenue rather than describing past revenue

Plus, the NSM is supported by **3–5 input metrics** ("inputs") that, when moved, mathematically move the NSM. Teams own inputs. The org owns the NSM.

## How to apply

### Finanshels — candidate NSM
- **Option A:** *Monthly Active Compliant Accounts* (accounts with current filings, AML in good standing, active monthly bookkeeping)
- **Option B:** *Total Filings Submitted On Time per Month*
- **Option C:** *Net Revenue Retention*

NSM should capture *value to customer*, not just revenue. Option A fits best — it measures customers who are getting the actual outcome they hired Finanshels for (peace of mind / compliance).

Input metrics:
- New paid accounts / month
- Filings completed on time / total filings due
- Account churn rate
- Active product usage (dashboard logins, WhatsApp touchpoints)
- Average response time to customer queries

### Biggdate — candidate NSM
- **Option A:** *Number of mutual high-intent connections per month*
- **Option B:** *Number of off-app first dates reported per month*
- **Option C:** *Long-term relationship outcomes self-reported at 12 months*

Option B is closer to value, Option C is the truest but lags too long. **Pick B as NSM, use C as a leading-indicator validation cohort.**

Inputs:
- Verified active members
- Profile completion rate
- Mutual intent matches per active user / week
- Conversation → first date conversion %

### StartupOS — candidate NSM
- **Option:** *Weekly Active Founders Using The OS for At Least One Strategic Decision*

This forces the product to be *consequential*, not just opened.

Inputs: WAU, retention curves, strategic decisions logged per user, time saved per decision.

## Anti-patterns
- **Picking revenue as NSM.** Revenue lags. It rewards extraction (price hikes, upsells) over value creation.
- **Picking a vanity metric.** "Total registered users" — meaningless if 95% never come back.
- **Picking too many NSMs.** "Top 5 metrics we care about" = no metric we care about. The whole point is one.
- **Changing NSM every quarter.** The North Star should outlast a quarter. Pick well, then commit.
- **Optimizing the NSM at the expense of the customer.** If you can move the NSM via dark patterns, the NSM was wrong.
