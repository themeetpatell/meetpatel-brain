# Wedge and Beachhead

> Don't go after the market. Go after the smallest, sharpest beachhead inside it — win the beachhead so completely that the rest of the market notices.

## When to use it
- Launching a new venture or product
- Re-positioning a stalled venture
- Choosing geography, segment, or use-case to lead with
- Anytime the answer to "who's it for" is "everyone"

## The framework (5 tests for a real wedge)

1. **Narrow enough to be obvious.** "VAT filing for UAE e-commerce sellers with $50k–500k revenue" is a wedge. "SMB financial operations" is not.
2. **Big enough to matter.** If you win 100% of the wedge, is the resulting business interesting? (Not "category-defining" — just *interesting*.)
3. **Reachable through one channel.** You can find every wedge customer through a single repeatable channel (one event, one WhatsApp community, one keyword, one partner).
4. **Painful enough that they'll switch.** The wedge segment has acute pain *now*, not theoretical pain.
5. **Adjacent expansion path exists.** Winning the wedge opens an obvious next segment, not a dead end.

## How to apply

### Finanshels — wedge candidates worth testing
- **Wedge A:** UAE NRI-owned e-commerce SMBs, $100k–$1M GMV, post-VAT-implementation. Channel: WhatsApp + Indian-diaspora business networks. Pain: tax/AML overwhelm + language comfort. Expansion: full UAE SMB book once playbook locks.
- **Wedge B:** Free-zone professional services firms (consulting, agencies), 1–10 employees. Channel: free-zone events + partner referrals.
- **Wedge C:** Restaurants & F&B (vertical-specific accounting needs). Channel: F&B distributor partners + industry WhatsApp groups.

The job is to **pick one**, run it for 90 days, and measure conversion + CAC. Then expand or kill.

### StartupOS — wedge sharpening
Current candidates (from `30 Ventures/StartupOS/CLAUDE.md`):
- AI Chief of Staff for solo founders (narrowest, fastest)
- AI-native execution OS for 5–20 person teams
- Founder ecosystem layer
- AI-native fundraise OS

Run all four against the 5 tests. The one that passes all 5 (especially "reachable through one channel") wins. Use the [[_AI/Prompts/strategy/wedge-selection|wedge-selection prompt]] to force the call.

### Biggdate — wedge already implied
"High-intent dating for 25–40-year-olds in UAE + Indian diaspora who are tired of Hinge" is closer to a wedge than "premium dating app". The reachability test asks: can you find every wedge customer through ONE channel? Probably yes — diaspora communities, specific creator partnerships, founder-led brand.

## Anti-patterns
- **"We'll do SMB to start, then enterprise."** No. Pick SMB or enterprise. The motion is different.
- **Wedge defined by your product, not their pain.** "Customers using ZeroHuman for paid social" is not a wedge — that's a use case.
- **Multiple wedges in parallel.** You don't have the team. Pick one.
- **Wedge so small the business is uninteresting.** "Vegan accountants in Sharjah" — too small. The wedge has to be small relative to the market, not small absolute.
