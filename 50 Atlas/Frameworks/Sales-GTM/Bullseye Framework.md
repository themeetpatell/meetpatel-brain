# Bullseye Framework (19 Traction Channels)

> Most founders pick the 1–2 channels they're personally familiar with. That's the wrong answer 80% of the time. Bullseye forces you to consider all 19 channels, test 3 cheaply, and double down on the 1 that works.

## When to use it
- Launching a new venture (or new product line)
- Stuck in a single-channel trap and growth has plateaued
- Choosing where ZeroHuman should focus client acquisition
- Quarterly channel review — is the current dominant channel still best?

## The 19 channels

1. **Viral Marketing** — referrals built into the product
2. **PR** — earned media, press coverage
3. **Unconventional PR** — stunts, publicity hacks
4. **Search Engine Marketing (SEM)** — paid search ads
5. **Social and Display Ads** — paid social, banner ads
6. **Offline Ads** — TV, radio, billboards, print
7. **Search Engine Optimization (SEO)** — organic search
8. **Content Marketing** — blog, newsletter, podcast, long-form
9. **Email Marketing** — direct-to-inbox, lifecycle, drip
10. **Engineering as Marketing** — free tools, calculators, microsites
11. **Targeting Blogs** — guest posts, sponsorships on niche blogs
12. **Business Development (BD)** — partnerships, integrations, affiliate
13. **Sales** — outbound sales, founder-led sales, SDR teams
14. **Affiliate Programs** — paid partners selling on your behalf
15. **Existing Platforms** — App Store, Chrome Store, Stripe Apps, etc.
16. **Trade Shows** — in-person events, conferences
17. **Offline Events** — meetups, dinners, owned events
18. **Speaking Engagements** — conferences, podcasts as guest
19. **Community Building** — own community, Slack, Discord, WhatsApp groups

## The Bullseye process (3 rings)

**Outer ring — Brainstorm:** for each of the 19, write one specific tactic you could try. Not "yes/no" — actual tactic.

**Middle ring — Promising:** pick 5–6 channels that seem most likely to work given your business. Justify each.

**Inner ring — Test:** pick the top 3. Run a small, time-boxed, low-cost test (~2 weeks, ~$1k–5k). Define what "working" looks like in advance.

**The bullseye:** one channel will produce dramatically better results. Go all-in on that one. Push to scale until it caps out. Then move to the next.

## Anti-patterns
- **Skipping the outer ring brainstorm.** "We obviously won't do TV ads" — but the brainstorm forces you to consider *what TV-like thing might work* (sponsored podcast slot, etc.). Don't skip.
- **Picking the inner ring based on what you know.** That's selection bias. Pick based on what's promising *for this venture*.
- **Testing too many channels at once.** 3 is the max. Channel tests need real focus.
- **Killing a channel test too early.** 2 weeks minimum. Some channels (SEO, content) need months.
- **Confusing initial traction with the right channel.** Sometimes the easy first channel is a local maximum. Re-Bullseye every 6–12 months.

## What "winning" looks like
- One channel produces ≥50% of qualified leads at acceptable CAC for 90 days running.
- That's the channel to scale. Pour resources in until it saturates (CAC starts climbing materially).
- *Then* move to the next channel from the Promising ring.
