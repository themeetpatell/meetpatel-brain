# Cold Outreach Writer

Writes short, trigger-led cold outreach sequences across email, LinkedIn, and WhatsApp whose single goal is to book a short discovery call with a UAE founder or finance lead.

## When to use

- You need a fresh cold email sequence for a defined prospect segment
- You are prospecting on LinkedIn and need a connection note plus follow-up DMs
- A WhatsApp nudge is appropriate (warm intro, event lead, prior reply)
- A real-world trigger has appeared (CT deadline, VAT penalty wave, new licence, fundraise) and you want outreach mapped to it
- You are refreshing a tired sequence that stopped getting replies
- You want subject-line variants to A/B test against a segment

## Inputs needed

**Required**
- Target segment (e.g. "Dubai mainland trading LLCs, AED 2–10M revenue, founder-led")
- The trigger / hook (e.g. CT first-return deadline, VAT penalty exposure, new free zone licence issued, recent fundraise, financial year-end approaching)
- Channel(s): email / LinkedIn / WhatsApp (one or several)
- Known personalisation facts (only genuinely known — company name, licence type, public funding news, mutual connection)

**Optional**
- Sender name, title, and Finanshels role
- Prior interaction history (downloaded a guide, met at an event, replied once)
- Number of touches wanted (default 4)
- Segment-specific pain points beyond the headline trigger
- Preferred call length to offer (default 15 minutes)

## Workflow

### Step 1 — Pick the sharpest hook
Choose the single most relevant, time-sensitive trigger from `references/outreach-hooks.md`. One hook per sequence — do not stack three reasons. The hook must be true for this segment now (e.g. CT return due within 9 months of year-end). If no real trigger exists, the outreach is not ready — go back and find one.

### Step 2 — Confirm the segment pain and the one ask
State the specific pain the hook creates for this founder (penalty, lost time, deadline risk, no clean numbers for investors). The ask is always the same: a short, low-pressure call. Never a quote, never a fee, never "reply with your questions" — one clear ask only.

### Step 3 — Design the multi-touch sequence
Default 4 touches, each a distinct angle so the prospect sees a new reason every time:
- **Touch 1 — Trigger:** lead with the hook and the deadline/risk; one-line ask.
- **Touch 2 — Value / insight:** give one genuinely useful UAE-tax insight (a thing they may not know), no pitch.
- **Touch 3 — Social proof:** anchor to an approved proof point (7,000+ businesses, 150+ accountants, named clients only from the approved list).
- **Touch 4 — Soft breakup:** polite, no guilt, leave the door open and offer an easy opt-out.
Space touches 3–4 working days apart. Skip weekends and UAE public holidays.

### Step 4 — Write the copy per channel
Keep every message short and scannable. Email body ≤ 120 words. LinkedIn connection note ≤ 300 characters. LinkedIn DM ≤ 600 characters. WhatsApp ≤ 60 words and only where there is prior contact or consent. Founder-first voice ("you/your"), plain English, no jargon without translation, no corporate filler. Lead WHY → WHAT. End every touch with one clear ask.

### Step 5 — Write subject-line options
Provide 3 subject lines per email touch: one trigger-specific, one curiosity, one plain/direct. No clickbait, no fake "Re:", no false urgency. Subject lines must honestly reflect the body.

### Step 6 — Add personalisation slots and the opt-out
Mark personalisation as explicit `[bracketed]` slots so the sender fills real facts — never invent a detail to fake rapport. Every email includes a genuine, easy opt-out line. WhatsApp includes a "happy to stop here" line.

### Step 7 — Date the sequence and hand off for review
Lay the sequence on a working-day calendar from a chosen start date. Flag that a Finanshels team member must review and personalise before sending.

## Quality checklist

- [ ] Exactly one hook drives the whole sequence — reasons are not stacked
- [ ] The hook is a real, current UAE trigger verified against FTA guidance
- [ ] Each touch is a distinct angle (trigger / value / social proof / breakup)
- [ ] Every message has one clear ask — a short call, never a quote
- [ ] No fee, price, or pricing hint appears anywhere
- [ ] Email bodies ≤ 120 words; LinkedIn note ≤ 300 chars; WhatsApp ≤ 60 words
- [ ] Subject lines are honest — no fake "Re:", no false urgency, no clickbait
- [ ] Only approved proof points and client names used (7,000+, 150+, approved list)
- [ ] Personalisation is bracketed for the sender — no invented prospect facts
- [ ] Every email has a genuine, easy opt-out line
- [ ] Sequence is dated on working days, skipping weekends and UAE holidays
- [ ] Voice is founder-first, plain English, WHY → WHAT, no corporate filler

**Example 2** "Draft LinkedIn connection note + 2 DMs for founders of newly licensed DMCC free zone tech companies — hook is QFZP eligibility and the choice they need to make early."

## Guardrails

- UAE jurisdiction only — never reference IRS, US states, UK/EU tax, or CPE.
- Outputs are professional work product — a Finanshels team member must review and personalise before anything is sent to a prospect.
- AI does not set or confirm pricing — never put a fee or pricing hint in cold outreach; the ask is a call, not a quote.
- Never invent Finanshels credentials, client names, case studies, testimonials, or numbers beyond the approved proof points.
- Never fabricate a personal detail about a prospect to fake personalisation — use only genuinely known facts and leave the rest as bracketed slots.
- Respect anti-spam and consent norms — outreach must be relevant, honest, and offer an easy opt-out; no deceptive subject lines.
- Treat all prospect data as confidential; never use real client identifiers in examples.
- Verify every tax rate, threshold, and deadline against current FTA guidance before relying on it.
