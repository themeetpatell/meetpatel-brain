# Partner Pitch Builder

Turns a partner profile into a polished, partner-first one-page partnership pitch that leads with what the partner and their clients gain.

## When to use
- A partner has been scored (ideally via `partner-prospecting`) and is ready to be approached
- An inbound partnership enquiry needs a structured proposal in response
- Re-pitching a dormant partner with a refreshed model or incentive
- Adapting a generic pitch to a specific partner category (free zone, bank, VC, fintech)
- Preparing leave-behind material for a partnership meeting or event

## Inputs needed
**Required**
- Partner profile — name/ref code, category, client base (from `partner-prospecting` if available)
- Proposed partnership model — referral, co-marketing, white-label / embedded, or reseller
- The partner's incentive — the benefit type that motivates them (rate left blank for management)

**Optional**
- Specific pain point the partner's clients face (e.g. "their startups get hit with CT penalties")
- Named decision-maker and their role
- Any prior conversation context or agreed terms
- Co-branding constraints or partner brand requirements
- Target send date or meeting date

## Workflow
### Step 1 — Confirm the partner frame
Restate who the partner is and the single most important thing they want — better client outcomes, a new revenue stream, a capability gap filled, or stickier clients. Every section of the pitch serves that frame. If the input lacks it, ask before drafting.

### Step 2 — Write the opportunity (WHY)
Open with the partner's reality, not Finanshels. Name the gap or moment in their client journey where finance/tax becomes a problem the partner cannot fully solve. Lead WHY → WHAT. One short paragraph.

### Step 3 — Define the model and referral flow
Explain the chosen model in plain English and show how a referral actually moves: who introduces, how the handoff happens, what the partner does and does not do. Keep it concrete — a partner must picture it working on Monday.

### Step 4 — State the partner incentive
Describe the incentive type clearly (revenue share, preferred pricing for their clients, co-marketing reach, capability they can now offer). Leave any percentage, fee, or commission as `[rate — to be confirmed by Finanshels management]`.

### Step 5 — Lay out the support Finanshels provides
List what the partner gets so referring feels safe: a dedicated partnerships contact, fast response on referrals, co-branded enablement material (link to `partner-enablement-kit`), transparent updates on referred clients, and the credibility of an established partner. Anchor one approved proof point here.

### Step 6 — Close with a simple next step
End with one low-friction action — a 20-minute call, a pilot with a handful of referrals, or signing a short partnership outline. Include the partner-facing tagline "7,000+ businesses. One finance infrastructure."

### Step 7 — Quality pass and hand-off note
Run the checklist, then add a short internal note flagging which fields management must complete (rates, named contact, dates) before the pitch is sent.

## Output format
```
PARTNERSHIP PITCH — [partner name or ref code]
Prepared by: [team member]   Date: [date]   Status: DRAFT — management review required

[Partner-first headline — the opportunity in one line]

THE OPPORTUNITY (why this matters to you)
[One paragraph — the gap in the partner's client journey]

HOW IT WORKS — [model name]
[Plain-English description of the model and how a referral flows]
- You: [what the partner does]
- Finanshels: [what Finanshels does]
- Your client: [what the client experiences]

WHAT YOU GET
- [Incentive — rate: to be confirmed by Finanshels management]
- [Additional partner benefit]
- [Additional partner benefit]

THE SUPPORT BEHIND YOU
- Dedicated partnerships contact: [name — TBC]
- Co-branded enablement kit, fast referral response, transparent updates
- [One approved proof point — e.g. 7,000+ businesses served]

NEXT STEP
[One concrete, low-friction action]

7,000+ businesses. One finance infrastructure.

---
INTERNAL NOTE — fields for management to complete before sending:
- [ ] Incentive rate / commission structure
- [ ] Named partnerships contact
- [ ] Call or meeting date
```

## Quality checklist
- [ ] Pitch opens with the partner's benefit, not Finanshels' services
- [ ] WHY → WHAT structure followed throughout
- [ ] Partnership model is named and the referral flow is concrete
- [ ] Partner incentive is described; the actual rate is left for management
- [ ] Exactly one approved proof point is anchored (7,000+ / 150+ / named clients)
- [ ] Partner-facing tagline "7,000+ businesses. One finance infrastructure." is included
- [ ] Plain English — no unexplained jargon, no corporate filler
- [ ] Fits on one page; confident and helpful, never alarmist
- [ ] Next step is single and low-friction
- [ ] Internal hand-off note lists every management-completed field
- [ ] No invented partner names, client names, testimonials, or numbers
- [ ] UAE jurisdiction only — no foreign-tax references; marked DRAFT for review

## Examples
**Example 1** "Build a referral-program pitch for a Dubai business-setup consultant — their newly licensed clients have no finance function."
**Example 2** "Draft a co-marketing partnership pitch for a UAE VC accelerator that wants its portfolio companies CT-ready."
**Example 3** "Write a white-label / embedded accounting pitch for an audit firm that needs bookkeeping overflow for its SME audit clients."

## Guardrails
- UAE jurisdiction only — never reference IRS, US states, UK/EU tax regimes, or CPE.
- Output is professional work product — a Finanshels partnerships team member must review it before it reaches any partner.
- AI does not set or confirm commission, referral, or revenue-share rates — leave them marked for management completion.
- Never invent Finanshels credentials, partner names, client names, case studies, testimonials, or numbers beyond the approved proof points.
- Treat all partner and client data as confidential — never use real identifiers in examples; use reference codes.
- Verify any tax rate, threshold, or deadline referenced against current FTA guidance before relying on it.
