# Finanshels Voice & Tone Guide

## The Finanshels Voice in One Sentence

**Clear, confident expertise — delivered with the empathy of a trusted advisor, never the anxiety of a compliance officer.**

---

## Six Voice Characteristics

### 1. Plain English First
Translate regulation into language a smart non-accountant can act on. Lead with the practical implication, then support it with the rule.

> **Not this:** "Pursuant to Article 17 of Federal Decree-Law No. 47 of 2022, taxable persons are required to submit their Corporate Tax return within nine calendar months following the end of the relevant tax period."
>
> **This:** "Your Corporate Tax return is due within 9 months of your financial year-end. So if your year ends 31 December, the deadline is 30 September the following year."

---

### 2. Confidence Without Arrogance
We know UAE tax. We state facts directly. We don't hedge unnecessarily — but we are honest when something needs FTA verification.

> **Not this:** "You might possibly need to register for Corporate Tax, although this could depend on various factors."
>
> **This:** "If your business earns more than AED 375,000 in taxable income, Corporate Tax at 9% applies. Let's confirm your structure and calculate your exposure."

---

### 3. Calm in the Face of Complexity
We do not create panic around deadlines or penalties. We state the facts, and immediately pivot to the solution.

> **Not this:** "Warning: If you miss the Corporate Tax registration deadline, you could face serious FTA penalties. This is an urgent matter."
>
> **This:** "The CT registration deadline depends on your licence-issuance month. We'll confirm your exact date and handle the EmaraTax registration before it's due."

---

### 4. Solution-Oriented
Every problem statement comes with a next step. We don't leave clients sitting with a problem — we move to action.

> **Not this:** "There are several complex considerations involved in your free zone tax status."
>
> **This:** "Your free zone status can mean 0% CT on qualifying income — but the conditions are specific. We'll review your activities and structure and tell you exactly where you stand."

---

### 5. Empathetic Efficiency
We acknowledge that compliance is not what founders get out of bed for. We validate that briefly, then get to the point.

> **Not this:** "As a business owner, you have a legal obligation to comply with all applicable UAE tax laws and regulations."
>
> **This:** "We know tax compliance isn't why you started your business. That's exactly why we handle it — so you don't have to."

---

### 6. Specific, Not Vague
We use actual numbers, actual deadlines, actual regulation names. Vagueness erodes trust.

> **Not this:** "There are various thresholds and deadlines you need to be aware of."
>
> **This:** "VAT registration is mandatory once your taxable supplies exceed AED 375,000 over the past 12 months. The return is filed quarterly, due by the 28th of the following month."

---

## Tone by Context

### Delivering good news
Warm, direct, brief. Let the news land first.
> "Your VAT registration is live. Your TRN is [number]. You can start charging VAT on your invoices immediately — we'll send you an updated invoice template."

### Delivering a deadline reminder
Calm, specific, action-ready.
> "Quick heads-up: your Q1 VAT return covers January–March and is due by 28 April. We'll have the draft ready for your review by 22 April. Just send over the bank statements by 15 April and we're on track."

### Explaining a regulation for the first time
Educational, structured, patient. Define terms, give an example.
> "UAE Corporate Tax launched for financial years starting on or after 1 June 2023. At a high level: income up to AED 375,000 is taxed at 0%. Anything above that is taxed at 9%. There are reliefs for small businesses and specific rules for free zone companies — we'll walk you through how these apply to your business."

### Responding to a client concern or mistake
Reassuring, honest, forward-focused.
> "We've reviewed the filing and spotted an input tax credit that was omitted. This is correctable — we'll prepare a voluntary disclosure and submit it to the FTA. These happen; what matters is that we catch and fix them promptly."

### Marketing / social content
Punchy, benefit-forward, confident. Hook in the first line.
> "Most UAE founders don't realise their Corporate Tax deadline is tied to their licence-issuance month — not 31 December. Here's how to check yours."

## Words We Use / Avoid

### Use
- "we'll handle", "we manage", "your team will" → ownership language
- "here's what this means for you" → translating regulation to impact
- "reach out to us", "let us know" → low-friction CTAs
- "straightforward", "clear", "simple" → reducing intimidation
- "confirm", "verify", "review" → appropriate epistemic caution without hedging
- "AED 375,000 threshold", "9% rate", "28th of the month" → specifics

### Avoid
| Avoid | Replace with |
|-------|-------------|
| "Please be advised" | Just say the thing |
| "Kindly note" | "Note:" or just say it directly |
| "It is important to mention" | Say it without the preamble |
| "Urgent compliance action required" | State the deadline and the fix |
| "You may be at risk of penalties" | "Here's how to stay compliant" |
| "Best-in-class service" | Show don't tell — describe what we do |
| "Seamless experience" | Overused; be specific |
| "Leverage" (as a verb) | "Use" |
| "Actionable insights" | Say what the insight actually is |
| "Going forward" | "From now on" or just start the sentence |
| "Touch base" | "Check in" or just say "Let's talk" |

## Sentence Rhythm

**Complex ideas → short sentences.** Break a regulatory concept across two or three sentences rather than stacking clauses.

**Good:**
> UAE Corporate Tax has two tiers. Income up to AED 375,000 is taxed at 0%. Everything above that is taxed at 9%.

**Avoid:**
> UAE Corporate Tax operates on a tiered basis whereby income up to AED 375,000 is subject to a 0% rate and income in excess of this threshold is subject to a 9% rate which is applied to the portion of taxable income above the stated threshold.

## Before / After Rewrites — Full Examples

### Example A: Client VAT registration email

**Before (cold, jargon-heavy, no empathy):**
> Dear Client,
>
> Please be advised that your VAT registration application has been approved by the FTA. Your Tax Registration Number (TRN) is [TRN]. Kindly note that you are now required to charge VAT at the applicable rate of 5% on all standard-rated supplies and issue compliant VAT invoices. Failure to comply may result in penalties.
>
> Regards,
> Finanshels

**After (Finanshels voice):**
> Hi [Name],
>
> Great news — your VAT registration is approved. Your Tax Registration Number (TRN) is [TRN].
>
> From now on, you'll charge 5% VAT on your standard-rated sales and issue VAT-compliant invoices. We've put together a quick checklist of what needs to update (invoice template, accounting software settings, contracts) — we'll send that over today.
>
> Your first VAT return will cover the period from your registration date. We'll be in touch ahead of the deadline to make sure everything is in order.
>
> Any questions, just reply here.
>
> [Name], Finanshels

---

### Example B: LinkedIn post about CT deadline

**Before (alarmist, vague):**
> URGENT: UAE Corporate Tax deadlines are approaching and many businesses are still not registered. The FTA could penalise non-compliant businesses heavily. Don't risk it — contact us today!

**After (Finanshels voice):**
> Most UAE founders assume their Corporate Tax deadline is 31 December.
>
> It isn't.
>
> Your CT registration deadline is tied to your licence-issuance month — and the FTA has set different dates for different months. Miss yours and you're looking at avoidable penalties.
>
> The fix is straightforward: check your licence date, look up your deadline on EmaraTax, and register if you haven't already.
>
> Not sure where you stand? Drop us a message and we'll check for you — no charge.

---

>
