# Onboarding Checklist — Team Member

Standardises the onboarding of every new Finanshels hire so nothing falls through the cracks and every joiner is client-ready on schedule.

---

## When to use

- A new accountant, tax associate, or client manager is joining the firm
- A returning employee or contractor is being re-activated
- A hire's onboarding was informal and needs to be formalised retrospectively
- A manager asks: "What does a new joiner need to be up and running in 90 days?"

---

## Inputs needed

**Required**
- Full name and job title of the new hire
- Start date
- Reporting manager's name

**Optional**
- Assigned client portfolio (names or client IDs)
- Office location (Dubai, Abu Dhabi, remote)
- Any pre-employment context (e.g., prior Big 4 experience, fresh graduate)
- Specific gaps identified at hiring (e.g., "no prior VAT experience")

---

## Workflow

### Step 1 — Gather inputs
Confirm the four required inputs. If the client portfolio is unknown, note "TBC — to be assigned by end of Week 1."

### Step 2 — Build the system-access checklist
List every system the hire needs access to, mapped to the responsible owner who must action each item before Day 1:

1. **Email & calendar** — Google Workspace / Microsoft 365 (IT / Office Manager)
2. **EmaraTax** — Add as authorised user on relevant client accounts (Tax Lead)
3. **Accounting software** — Xero / QuickBooks / Zoho (per client stack) (IT + Client Manager)
4. **Document management** — Shared drive folder structure, naming conventions (Office Manager)
5. **Practice management tool** — Task/project tracker used internally (Manager)
6. **WPS portal access** — If role includes payroll (Payroll Lead)
7. **goAML portal** — Read access; full access only once AML/CFT training is completed (Compliance Lead)
8. **Communication channels** — Slack/Teams, relevant client channels (Manager)
9. **Finanshels knowledge base / wiki** — Link and login (Manager)

### Step 3 — Build the UAE tax knowledge ramp-up plan
Map modules to the hire's role. An accountant needs all modules; a client manager can deprioritise technical return-preparation modules but must cover client-facing and compliance basics.

| Week | Module | Owner | Format |
|------|--------|-------|--------|
| 1 | UAE Corporate Tax fundamentals (Federal Decree-Law No. 47/2022): rates, thresholds, Small Business Relief, QFZP | Tax Lead | Self-study + 1-hour debrief |
| 1 | UAE VAT fundamentals: 5% standard rate, registration thresholds, return cadence | Tax Lead | Self-study + quiz |
| 2 | EmaraTax walkthrough: registration, return filing, payment flows | Senior Accountant (shadow) | Live demo |
| 2 | Transfer pricing basics: arm's-length principle, disclosure form obligations | Tax Lead | Document review + Q&A |
| 3 | AML/CFT obligations for DNFBPs: CDD, goAML, sanctions screening | Compliance Lead | Mandatory training module |
| 3 | IFRS / IFRS for SMEs applied to client bookkeeping | Senior Accountant | Case study |
| 4 | Payroll & WPS: MOHRE rules, end-of-service gratuity (Federal Decree-Law No. 33/2021), GPSSA | Payroll Lead | Workshop |
| 4 | Client communication standards: how Finanshels writes emails, delivers reports, escalates issues | Manager | Brand guide read + roleplay |
| 5–6 | Free Zone vs Mainland entity differences; QFZP qualifying conditions | Tax Lead | Document review + quiz |
| 6–8 | Shadowing: observe 2 live client calls, 1 return filing, 1 management report delivery | Assigned buddy | Live |

### Step 4 — Set shadowing and first-engagement assignments
- **Weeks 1–4:** Observer only. Attends client calls alongside buddy. No client-facing communication sent independently.
- **Week 4–6:** Supervised contributor. Prepares first draft of a bookkeeping reconciliation or VAT return under senior review.
- **Week 7–12:** First solo deliverable (low-risk client, fully reviewed before delivery). Client introduced to new hire by manager.

### Step 5 — Populate the 90-day plan
Open `templates/90-day-plan.md` and fill in every bracketed placeholder. The plan has three phases:
- **Days 1–30 (Learn):** Systems, people, process, knowledge modules
- **Days 31–60 (Contribute):** Supervised client work, first deliverables
- **Days 61–90 (Own):** Solo engagement management, performance check-in

### Step 6 — Deliver outputs
Produce:
1. **Onboarding playbook** — This document, fully filled in, PDF-ready
2. **System-access checklist** — Table with owner and due date (all items due ≥ Day 1)
3. **90-day plan** — Completed template, shared with hire and manager on Day 1
4. **Calendar invites list** — Week-by-week training sessions to be scheduled

## Quality checklist

- [ ] All system access items have a named owner and a due date before Day 1
- [ ] Knowledge modules are mapped correctly to the hire's role (not generic)
- [ ] AML/CFT training is listed as mandatory for all roles
- [ ] Shadowing schedule has clear phase transitions (observe → contribute → own)
- [ ] 90-day plan has a check-in at Day 30 and Day 60 with manager
- [ ] First solo deliverable is low-risk and fully reviewed before client delivery
- [ ] No real client names used if this playbook is being prepared as a template
- [ ] Plan reviewed by hiring manager before sharing with new joiner

---

## Guardrails

- UAE jurisdiction only. No reference to IRS, CPE credits, or US state requirements.
- This playbook is a draft. It must be reviewed and approved by the hiring manager before being shared with the new hire.
- All employee personal data (name, salary, residency status) is confidential and must not appear in shared or client-facing versions of this document.
- UAE labour law obligations (gratuity, leave, notice periods) are governed by Federal Decree-Law No. 33 of 2021. HR decisions must be reviewed by a qualified HR or legal professional.
- Access to client data and EmaraTax must follow Finanshels' data-access and least-privilege policies — do not grant broad access on Day 1.
