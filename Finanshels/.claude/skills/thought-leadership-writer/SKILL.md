# Thought Leadership Writer

Converts a UAE tax insight, regulatory update, or accounting topic into a ready-to-publish LinkedIn post or blog article. Output is in the Finanshels voice: clear, confident, plain English, helpful, precise — never alarmist.

## When to use

- The FTA releases a new public clarification, cabinet decision, or EmaraTax update
- A regulatory deadline is approaching and Finanshels wants to get ahead of the conversation
- A team member has a raw insight ("free zone companies often misunderstand the de minimis test") and needs it turned into polished content
- A client question keeps coming up and it deserves a public answer
- You want to build authority on a specific UAE tax or accounting topic
- You have notes or a voice memo from a partner and need it turned into a LinkedIn post or article

## Inputs needed

**Required**
- The core insight, update, or topic (raw notes, a regulation reference, a bullet list, or a detailed brief — all formats accepted)
- Output format: LinkedIn post OR blog article

**Optional**
- Target audience: founders / finance leads / both (default: both)
- Tone dial: more educational vs. more conversational (default: balanced)
- Target length: LinkedIn post (~150–300 words) / short blog (~600 words) / long blog (~1,000–1,200 words)
- Specific CTA to include (e.g., "book a call", "download our guide", "DM us")
- SEO target keyword (for blog articles)
- Any specific angle to lead with (e.g., "lead with the common mistake", "lead with the deadline")
- Related Finanshels service to tie the piece to

## Workflow

1. **Clarify the insight**
   Before writing, identify:
   - The single most important thing the reader should take away
   - Why this matters *now* (deadline, regulatory change, common mistake, new rule)
   - Who this affects (mainland / free zone / all UAE businesses / specific size or sector)
   - What the reader should *do* after reading this

2. **Select the right format**

   **LinkedIn post** — use when:
   - The insight can be stated and explained in under 300 words
   - The hook is punchy enough to stop a scroll
   - The goal is reach and brand awareness
   - The message is timely (imminent deadline, fresh regulatory news)
   Structure: Hook line → Context (2–3 lines) → The insight (3–5 lines) → Practical implication → CTA

   **Blog article** — use when:
   - The topic requires explanation, examples, or step-by-step guidance
   - The piece can rank in organic search
   - The goal is education and lead generation
   - The topic has enough depth for 600–1,200 words
   Structure: use `templates/article-template.md`

3. **Write the piece**
   Apply the Finanshels voice from `../brand-consistency-guide/voice-tone.md`:
   - Hook first — lead with the insight, the implication, or the question (not background)
   - Plain English — translate every regulatory term into what it means for a business owner
   - Short sentences for complex regulatory ideas
   - No alarmist language — state facts calmly and pivot to the solution
   - Specific over vague — use actual thresholds, rates, and deadlines (marked [VERIFY])
   - End with a clear CTA or next step

4. **For blog articles — add SEO notes**
   After the article, provide:
   - Suggested title tag (≤ 60 characters)
   - Meta description (≤ 155 characters)
   - Primary keyword and placement suggestions
   - 2–3 internal linking opportunities (existing or planned Finanshels pages)

5. **Fact-check pass**
   Cross-reference any UAE tax figures, thresholds, or deadlines against `../_shared/finanshels-context.md`. Insert `[VERIFY against current FTA guidance]` next to any figure that cannot be confirmed from the shared context.

6. **Brand pass**
   Run through the checklist below before delivering.

## Quality checklist

- [ ] Hook is in the first line — no preamble or background before the point
- [ ] Every acronym is defined on first use (FTA, CT, VAT, QFZP, EmaraTax)
- [ ] No alarmist language — facts stated calmly with a solution path
- [ ] All UAE tax figures, thresholds, and deadlines are specific and marked [VERIFY] if unconfirmed
- [ ] No reference to non-UAE tax systems (IRS, HMRC, etc.)
- [ ] Plain English throughout — a smart non-accountant can follow the content
- [ ] Ends with a single, clear CTA or next step
- [ ] LinkedIn post is under 300 words; blog article is 600–1,200 words
- [ ] Blog article has SEO notes section completed
- [ ] Tone is confident but not arrogant; helpful but not hand-holding
- [ ] British English spelling (organisation, recognised, licence as noun)
- [ ] No empty superlatives ("amazing", "world-class", "game-changing")

## Examples

**Example 1 — LinkedIn post from a regulatory update**
> "The FTA recently clarified the de minimis threshold for free zone companies under the QFZP rules. Our free zone clients keep asking about this. Can you write a LinkedIn post explaining it in plain English for founders?"
> Client context: free zone startup founder, non-technical audience.

**Example 2 — Blog article on Small Business Relief**
> "Write a blog article explaining UAE Corporate Tax Small Business Relief — who qualifies, how to apply for it, and the AED 3M revenue threshold. Target keyword: 'UAE corporate tax small business relief'. Audience is founders of Dubai mainland companies with under AED 5M revenue."

**Example 3 — LinkedIn post timed to VAT return deadline**
> "It's mid-Q2 and VAT returns are due 28 July. Write a LinkedIn post reminding UAE businesses what they need to prepare, without being preachy or scary. Keep it practical and end with an offer to help."

## Guardrails

- All content is a professional draft to be reviewed by a qualified Finanshels team member before publishing — it is not final tax or legal advice
- Any tax rate, threshold, or deadline must be verified against current FTA guidance before the piece goes live; this skill flags with [VERIFY] but does not guarantee accuracy
- Do not include real client names, TRN numbers, or financial details in any content
- UAE jurisdiction only — do not introduce non-UAE regulatory comparisons unless explicitly requested
- If the input contains what appears to be confidential client information, flag this before proceeding and remove identifying details from the output
