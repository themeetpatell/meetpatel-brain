# Lead Scoring Workflow

Detailed scoring guide for qualifying Finanshels prospects. Use alongside the `prospect-qualification` skill. This document provides extended rubrics, ICP signal library, and common objection handlers.

---

## Ideal Client Profile — Reference Summary

Finanshels' ICP is a UAE-incorporated startup or SME that:
- Holds a mainland (DED) or free zone licence in the UAE
- Has annual revenue between AED 0 and AED 50M (sweet spot: AED 500K–AED 10M)
- Has a founder or finance lead who wants compliance handled so they can focus on the business
- Has at least one active compliance need: CT, VAT, bookkeeping, payroll, or audit

The ICP is NOT:
- Large corporates (> AED 50M revenue) who need a Big 4 firm or in-house team
- Offshore/holding entities with no UAE operating activity
- Non-UAE entities
- Purely individual/consumer financial enquiries

## Discovery Call Question Bank

Use these questions to gather the inputs needed for accurate scoring. Not all questions are needed in every call — pick the most relevant 5–7.

### Entity & structure
1. "What type of UAE licence do you hold — mainland DED or free zone? Which free zone?"
2. "Is your company fully incorporated, or are you still in the formation process?"
3. "Do you have any other entities — holding company, subsidiaries, or group structure?"

### Revenue & financials
4. "What is your approximate annual revenue for the last 12 months?"
5. "Is your revenue mostly from UAE customers, exports, or a mix?"
6. "What accounting system are you currently using — Xero, QuickBooks, Wave, spreadsheets, something else?"
7. "Are your financial statements prepared to IFRS standard, and have they been audited?"

### CT position
8. "Have you registered for Corporate Tax on EmaraTax?"
9. "Do you know when your first CT return is due? It's 9 months after your financial year-end."
10. "Do you have any transactions with related companies or group entities — loans, management fees, shared costs?"

### VAT position
11. "Are you registered for VAT? Do you have a Tax Registration Number?"
12. "Are your VAT returns up to date, or are there any overdue filings?"
13. "Have you received any FTA correspondence, penalty notices, or audit requests?"

### Compliance pain
14. "What's the main reason you're looking for a new accounting firm right now?"
15. "Is there a specific deadline or event driving your timeline — a return due, a fundraise, an audit?"
16. "How is compliance currently being handled — in-house, a freelancer, another firm?"

### Decision & budget
17. "Who else is involved in this decision — just you, or is there a finance team or board?"
18. "Do you have a sense of what you're currently spending on accounting and compliance?"
19. "What does a good outcome look like for you in the first 90 days?"

## Positive ICP Signals (score up)

Use these signals to increase confidence before assigning the final score.

**Strong positive signals:**
- CT registration deadline is imminent or already past
- VAT penalty received or overdue returns exist
- Bookkeeper / accountant recently resigned or fired
- Company is fundraising or preparing for an audit — needs clean financials fast
- Free zone entity unsure about QFZP status — direct advisory need
- Founder is the direct contact and has authority to sign
- Company has multiple entities (upsell potential across the group)
- Revenue just crossed AED 375,000 VAT threshold — urgent registration need
- Revenue just crossed AED 3M — Small Business Relief eligibility ending
- Growth-stage company (hired 5+ people in the last year) — payroll and WPS needed

**Moderate positive signals:**
- Currently using a freelancer or small local firm (easier to win than a mid-tier firm)
- Asking about management accounts or CFO services (higher LTV service)
- Referred by an existing Finanshels client (trust already established)
- Recently incorporated — catching compliance from day one is easier
- International founders unfamiliar with UAE compliance (education-led sell)

---

## Negative ICP Signals (score down)

**Strong negative signals:**
- Revenue > AED 50M and company has in-house finance team
- Already has a well-resourced finance function (CFO + controller)
- Requires tax litigation or FTA dispute resolution (out of Finanshels' scope)
- Entity is offshore / BVI / Cayman with no UAE operating presence
- Enquiry is from a non-UAE country and they want overseas tax advice
- Price shopping — has already got quotes from 5 firms and wants the cheapest

**Moderate negative signals:**
- Pre-revenue with no incorporation timeline
- Extremely complex multi-jurisdiction group structure
- Looking for a Big 4 brand name for investor optics
- Has had a bad experience with multiple firms in a row (potential problem client)

## Common Objections and Responses

**"We already have an accountant."**
Ask: "What's working well, and what prompted you to explore options now?" — the reason they enquired is usually the real objection to their current provider. Focus on the gap, not the competitor.

**"We're too small to need all of this."**
Respond: Getting registered for CT and VAT correctly from the start costs far less than fixing a penalty or a voluntary disclosure later. Even a one-person business with AED 400K revenue needs to be VAT-registered.

**"How much does it cost?"**
Respond: Pricing is scope-dependent — once we understand what you need, we can provide a fixed-fee proposal. Most clients find the cost of compliance is significantly less than the cost of getting it wrong.

**"We'll sort it ourselves."**
Note: this is a common response from founders. Keep in the nurture sequence. Compliance complexity increases with growth — the company will return when it becomes painful.

**"We just need help with one thing."**
Engage with the specific need, deliver it well, and use it as the door into a broader relationship. Many full-service clients started with a one-off CT registration.

## Pipeline Tier Action Protocols

### Tier 1 — Hot (80–100% ICP fit)
- Respond within 2 hours
- Book discovery call same day or next morning
- Assign senior account manager
- Prepare draft service bundle before the call
- Offer to send a draft proposal within 24 hours of the call
- Target: close within 5 business days

### Tier 2 — Warm (60–79% ICP fit)
- Respond within 24 hours
- Book discovery call within 3 business days
- Standard account manager
- Qualify further on discovery call before committing to a proposal
- Target: close within 10 business days

### Tier 3 — Nurture (40–59% ICP fit)
- Respond within 48 hours
- Add to email nurture sequence (compliance tips, CT/VAT guides)
- Re-score in 30 days or when a new trigger event occurs
- Do not invest more than 30 minutes of sales time until re-scored as Warm or Hot

### Tier 4 — Low fit (< 40% ICP fit)
- Send polite, helpful email response
- If out of scope entirely, refer to an appropriate specialist or firm
- Do not add to active pipeline
- Re-evaluate only if significant new information changes the score
