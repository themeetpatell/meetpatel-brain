# Training Plan — UAE Tax Updates

Keeps the Finanshels team technically sharp and FTA-current by turning regulatory change into structured, repeatable internal training.

---

## When to use

- The FTA publishes new Corporate Tax guidance, a decision, or a clarification
- A VAT public clarification or Cabinet Decision is released
- EmaraTax introduces new filing flows or portal changes
- A team member flags a knowledge gap during a client engagement
- Finanshels wants to run a quarterly "tax update" session for the whole team
- A new tax associate needs a structured catch-up curriculum on a specific topic
- Management asks: "How do we make sure the team is up to date on [topic]?"

---

## Inputs needed

**Required**
- Topic area (e.g., "CT Small Business Relief", "Free Zone QFZP conditions", "VAT return filing on EmaraTax", "AML/CFT CDD requirements")
- Target audience: junior, mid-level, senior, or all-team

**Optional**
- Specific FTA publication or decision number to anchor the training
- Deadline by which training must be completed
- Preferred delivery format (self-study, live session, workshop, or mixed)
- Any known gaps from previous client work or internal QA reviews

---

## Workflow

### Step 1 — Scope the curriculum
Identify which of the six core knowledge domains the topic falls under:

| Domain | Key Acts / Sources |
|--------|-------------------|
| A. Corporate Tax | Federal Decree-Law No. 47/2022; FTA CT guides; MoF decisions |
| B. VAT | Federal Decree-Law No. 8/2017; FTA VAT guides; public clarifications |
| C. EmaraTax & Portal Operations | FTA EmaraTax user manuals; registration/filing workflows |
| D. AML/CFT & Compliance | goAML; CBUAE/MOEC guidance; DNFBP obligations |
| E. IFRS / Accounting Standards | IFRS Foundation; IFRS for SMEs |
| F. UAE Labour & Payroll | Federal Decree-Law No. 33/2021; MOHRE; WPS; GPSSA |

### Step 2 — Define modules
Break the topic into 3–5 modules of 30–60 minutes each. Each module follows this structure:

```
Module N — [Title]
Objective: By the end of this module, the learner will be able to [verb] [outcome].
Content outline: 3–5 bullet points
Delivery: Self-study / live session / workshop
Duration: [X] minutes
Pre-read: [Document or URL if available]
Assessment: [Quiz questions — see Step 4]
```

### Step 3 — Write module content outlines
For each module, produce a detailed content outline covering:
- What changed (the regulatory update or clarification)
- Why it matters to Finanshels clients (practical impact)
- What the team must now do differently (action items)
- Common mistakes or traps to watch for
- How to communicate the change to affected clients

**Standard modules by domain (select and adapt):**

**Domain A — Corporate Tax examples:**
- CT registration deadlines: which clients are overdue, how to check on EmaraTax
- Small Business Relief: eligibility (revenue ≤ AED 3M), election process, caveats, sunset date (31 Dec 2026)
- QFZP conditions: qualifying activities, adequate substance, de minimis rule, audited financials requirement
- Transfer pricing: arm's-length principle, disclosure form with CT return, documentation thresholds
- DMTT (Pillar Two): applicable only to groups with consolidated revenue ≥ EUR 750M; financial years from 1 Jan 2025

**Domain B — VAT examples:**
- Input tax recovery: when to apportion, partial exemption methods
- Zero-rated vs exempt supplies: how to classify and report correctly
- VAT return review: common errors flagged in FTA assessments
- Late filing / payment: voluntary disclosure process on EmaraTax

**Domain C — EmaraTax:**
- New portal walkthrough: screen-by-screen for filing a CT or VAT return
- Amending a filed return: when allowed, steps, supporting documentation

**Domain D — AML/CFT:**
- CDD triggers: when enhanced due diligence is required
- SAR/STR filing: what qualifies, timeline, how to file on goAML
- Sanctions screening: daily obligation, which lists to check

### Step 4 — Write knowledge quiz
Each module gets 5 questions. Format: multiple choice (4 options) with the correct answer indicated. Questions test application, not memorisation.

Example structure:
```
Q1. [Scenario-based question]
A. [Option]
B. [Option]
C. [Option — correct]
D. [Option]
Answer: C
Explanation: [One sentence why]
```

Passing score: 80%. Team members who score below 80% must revisit the module and re-take.

### Step 5 — Set delivery schedule
Map modules to a timeline. For a quarterly all-team update session:
- Week 1: Modules 1–2 (self-study with pre-read)
- Week 2: Live session — Q&A on Modules 1–2, walkthrough of Module 3
- Week 3: Modules 4–5 (self-study)
- Week 4: Quiz, discussion, and sign-off

For an urgent update (e.g., a new FTA decision with a short effective date):
- Day 1: Briefing document circulated
- Day 3: 60-minute live session
- Day 5: Quiz completed, results logged

### Step 6 — Populate the training curriculum template
Open `templates/training-curriculum.md` and fill in every bracketed placeholder. Include all modules, quizzes, and the delivery schedule.

### Step 7 — Deliver outputs
1. **Training curriculum document** — Completed template, ready to share with team leads
2. **Module content outlines** — One page per module
3. **Quiz bank** — All questions with answers and explanations
4. **Delivery schedule** — Calendar of sessions
5. **Completion tracking table** — Names × Modules, to be ticked off as each person completes

## Quality checklist

- [ ] Every module has a clear, measurable learning objective
- [ ] Content outline covers: what changed, practical impact, action required, traps
- [ ] All regulatory references cite the correct UAE act or FTA document (not IRS/UK)
- [ ] Each module has exactly 5 quiz questions with correct answers marked
- [ ] Passing score of 80% is specified and a re-take path is described
- [ ] Delivery schedule is realistic for the team's client workload
- [ ] Completion tracking table is included so managers can monitor progress
- [ ] Document notes that rates and deadlines must be verified against current FTA guidance

## Examples

- "Build a training curriculum on UAE Corporate Tax Small Business Relief for our junior and mid-level accountants. They need to be able to advise clients by end of the month."
- "The FTA just published a new VAT public clarification on financial services. Create a 2-module internal training with a quiz so the team knows how to apply it."
- "We want a quarterly tax-update session covering CT, VAT, and AML changes from the last 3 months. Audience is the whole team."

---

## Guardrails

- UAE jurisdiction only. Never reference IRS, CPE, HMRC, or US/UK equivalents.
- All training content is internal professional development material; it is not client advice and must not be forwarded to clients without a qualified team member's review.
- Regulatory references must cite the relevant UAE act, Cabinet Decision, or FTA publication number. Do not paraphrase rates or deadlines from memory — always instruct users to verify against current FTA guidance.
- Quiz answers are indicative. They must be reviewed by a senior tax professional before being used for assessment.
