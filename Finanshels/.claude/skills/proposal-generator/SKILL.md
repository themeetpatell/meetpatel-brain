# Proposal Generator

Creates a professional, tailored Finanshels service proposal for a UAE startup or SME prospect, covering recommended services, scope, deliverables, and next steps.

## When to use

- A prospect from a discovery call says "send me a proposal"
- You have a completed lead score card (from `prospect-qualification`) and are ready to move forward
- An existing client wants to add services and needs a formal scope document
- Preparing for a follow-up meeting and want a leave-behind document
- A referral partner needs a summary of what Finanshels offers for a specific client type

## Inputs needed

**Required**
- Prospect company name and contact name / title
- Entity type (mainland / free zone — which authority)
- Estimated annual revenue (AED)
- Principal business activity
- Services to propose (minimum 1, ideally 2–4)
- Any specific compliance urgency or deadline (e.g. CT return due in 60 days)

**Optional**
- Lead score card (from `prospect-qualification` skill) — if available, pull recommended bundle directly
- Current pain point in their own words (use this language in the proposal)
- Number of employees (for payroll scoping)
- Whether they need back-period catch-up work
- Any pricing guidance from the account manager
- Preferred financial year-end (for CT filing timeline)

## Workflow

### Step 1 — Confirm the service bundle
Review the inputs and select the right bundle. If a lead score card exists, use the recommended bundle from it. Otherwise apply this logic:

| Revenue band | Default starting bundle |
|---|---|
| < AED 2M | Starter: bookkeeping + CT registration + CT return + VAT |
| AED 2M – AED 10M | Growth: Starter + management accounts + payroll + audit-ready financials |
| > AED 10M or free zone complex | Advisory: Growth + CT advisory + QFZP review + outsourced CFO |
| Compliance urgency (any revenue) | Compliance catch-up first, then ongoing bundle |

Always tailor — do not include services that are clearly not relevant (e.g. payroll if solo founder with no employees).

### Step 2 — Set the compliance context
Include the 2–3 most relevant UAE compliance facts that create urgency and demonstrate expertise. Choose from:
- CT: 9% on taxable income above AED 375,000; return due 9 months after financial year-end
- CT: Small Business Relief available for revenue ≤ AED 3M, for tax periods ending on or before 31 Dec 2026
- CT: QFZP status requires four conditions — one gap risks losing the 0% rate
- VAT: mandatory registration at AED 375,000; returns due by 28th of the following month
- VAT: overdue registrations carry backdated liability
- TP: related-party transactions require disclosure form; documentation required above FTA thresholds
- Record retention: 7 years required under UAE CT law

Only include facts directly relevant to this prospect — do not list all of them.

### Step 3 — Define scope and deliverables
For each proposed service, write one clear sentence describing what Finanshels delivers and how often. Be specific: not "we do your accounts" but "monthly bookkeeping reconciled to your bank statements and categorised to IFRS for SMEs chart of accounts."

### Step 4 — Frame the value proposition
Lead with the client's problem, not Finanshels' credentials. The proposal should feel like it was written for this company, not copied from a brochure. Use the prospect's own language from discovery notes where available.

Three-part framing:
1. **Where you are now:** the compliance gap or pain point
2. **What happens if left unaddressed:** risk (penalty, lost relief, audit exposure) — state calmly, not alarmingly
3. **What Finanshels delivers:** the concrete outcome

### Step 5 — Populate the proposal template
Use `templates/proposal-template.md`. Keep the total proposal to one page (when formatted for print) or approximately 600–800 words. Every section must be filled — no placeholder text left in the final document.

### Step 6 — Internal review before sending
Before the proposal leaves Finanshels:
- Check all UAE figures (rates, thresholds, deadlines) are correct and flagged for FTA verification
- Confirm pricing / fee ranges have been approved by the account manager (not generated by AI)
- Remove any internal-only notes
- Ensure the prospect's name, entity type, and activity are accurate

## Quality checklist

- [ ] Proposal addresses this specific client — no generic filler text remains
- [ ] Service bundle matches the client's revenue band and compliance gaps
- [ ] Compliance context section contains only facts relevant to this prospect
- [ ] All UAE figures (rates, thresholds, deadlines) are correct per the shared context
- [ ] "Investment" section completed or clearly marked for account manager to complete — AI does not set prices
- [ ] "Why Finanshels" bullets are specific and honest — no invented credentials
- [ ] Next steps are concrete and numbered (not vague "we'll be in touch")
- [ ] Disclaimer included at the bottom
- [ ] No IRS / US state / CPE references
- [ ] Tone: confident and helpful — not salesy or alarmist
- [ ] Total length: approximately one page / 600–800 words when formatted

## Examples

**Example 1**
"Write a proposal for a Dubai mainland trading LLC, AED 4M revenue, 8 employees. They have a VAT penalty notice and their bookkeeper just left. They need bookkeeping, VAT compliance, and CT filing. Discovery call was yesterday — founder (Ahmed, CEO) asked for a proposal by end of week."

**Example 2**
"A DMCC free zone tech startup, AED 2.8M revenue, 3 employees, all revenue from international clients. They haven't registered for CT yet and want to understand if they're a QFZP. Propose: CT registration, QFZP eligibility review, bookkeeping, and management accounts."

**Example 3**
"A pre-revenue Abu Dhabi free zone startup, just incorporated last month, 2 founders. They want to set up compliance from day one — bookkeeping, CT registration, and VAT advice. No payroll needed yet."

## Guardrails

- UAE jurisdiction only. No UK/US/EU tax references.
- Do not set or confirm pricing in the proposal — mark the Investment section for account manager completion.
- Do not invent Finanshels credentials, client numbers, or case studies.
- All proposals are professional work product to be reviewed by a Finanshels team member before sending to any prospect.
- KYC and AML checks are required before onboarding any client — include this in next steps.
- This proposal does not constitute a binding contract — reference the engagement letter process.
- Treat all prospect data as confidential.
