# Partner Enablement Kit

Produces co-branded enablement material so an active Finanshels partner's staff can explain Finanshels accurately and refer clients with confidence.

## When to use
- A new partnership has been signed and the partner needs ready-to-use material
- A partner's team is referring inconsistently or describing Finanshels inaccurately
- Refreshing enablement material after a service change or rebrand
- Onboarding a new contact or branch within an existing partner
- Equipping a partner before a co-marketing campaign or event

## Inputs needed
**Required**
- Partner name (or ref code) and partner type / category
- Partnership model — referral, co-marketing, white-label / embedded, or reseller
- The partner audience — who at the partner will use the material (front-desk staff, advisors, account managers)

**Optional**
- The partner's client profile and the moment in their journey when a referral is natural
- Specific services most relevant to that partner's clients (e.g. CT registration, bookkeeping)
- Co-branding requirements — partner logo, colours, tone constraints
- The agreed referral mechanism (intro email, form, warm handoff, portal)
- Named Finanshels partnerships contact for the kit

## Workflow
### Step 1 — Frame the partner and audience
Confirm the partner type, the model, and exactly who will use the material. Front-desk staff need a short talk-track; advisors need more depth. Tailor language accordingly. If the audience is unclear, ask before drafting.

### Step 2 — Build the partner one-pager
Write a one-page explainer for the partner's team: what Finanshels does (WHY → WHAT), who it is for (the ICP — AED 0–50M UAE SMEs, mainland and free zone), why a client should be referred, and the moment to refer. Anchor one approved proof point. Keep it scannable.

### Step 3 — Write the referral talk-track
Draft a short spoken script (30–60 seconds) the partner's staff can use naturally on a call or in person: a one-line description, the trigger to listen for, one sentence on why Finanshels, and the handoff line. Provide one or two responses to common client questions.

### Step 4 — Draft the client-facing intro email
Write a warm intro email the partner can forward or adapt to a client. It is from the partner to their client, introducing Finanshels — founder-first, plain English, low-pressure, with a clear next step (a no-obligation call). Keep it short.

### Step 5 — Build the FAQ
Write 6–10 plain-English Q&As covering what the partner's staff and clients will actually ask: what Finanshels does, cost (described qualitatively — no figures), how onboarding works, data security, UAE coverage, and what makes Finanshels different. Never invent specifics.

### Step 6 — Apply co-branding and assemble
Assemble the four assets into one kit. Note co-branding placement (partner logo alongside Finanshels), and apply brand colours — Deep Blue #0A2B3B headers, Bright Orange #F16610 CTAs, Blue-gray #334756 body, Inter font.

### Step 7 — Quality pass and hand-off note
Run the checklist and add an internal note listing what needs partner sign-off and which fields management must complete (named contact, referral mechanism, any pricing language).

## Quality checklist
- [ ] All four assets present: one-pager, talk-track, intro email, FAQ
- [ ] One-pager leads WHY → WHAT and names the AED 0–50M UAE SME ICP
- [ ] Exactly one approved proof point anchored in the one-pager
- [ ] Talk-track is genuinely speakable in 30–60 seconds
- [ ] Intro email is written from the partner's voice to their client, not Finanshels' voice
- [ ] FAQ answers are plain English with no invented figures, names, or claims
- [ ] No specific prices or commission rates stated anywhere
- [ ] Partner-facing tagline "7,000+ businesses. One finance infrastructure." included
- [ ] Co-branding placement noted; brand colours and Inter font specified
- [ ] Confident, helpful tone — never alarmist, no corporate filler
- [ ] UAE jurisdiction only — no foreign-tax references
- [ ] Internal hand-off note lists all sign-off and management-completion items

## Guardrails
- UAE jurisdiction only — never reference IRS, US states, UK/EU tax regimes, or CPE.
- Output is professional work product — a Finanshels partnerships team member must review it, and the partner must approve co-branding, before use.
- AI does not set or confirm pricing or commission/referral rates — describe cost qualitatively and leave rate fields for management.
- Never invent Finanshels credentials, partner names, client names, case studies, testimonials, or numbers beyond the approved proof points.
- Treat all partner and client data as confidential — never use real identifiers in examples; use reference codes.
- Verify any tax rate, threshold, or deadline referenced against current FTA guidance before relying on it.
