# Competitive Battlecard

Produces a focused, honest one-page battlecard so the Finanshels sales team can win a competitive UAE accounting deal without overclaiming or trash-talking.

## When to use

- A prospect reveals they are also evaluating another accounting option
- Preparing for a discovery or proposal call where competition is known
- A deal stalled and you suspect a competitor is in the mix
- Coaching a BD hire on how to position against a competitor category
- Updating the team's competitive talk track after losing or winning a deal
- A prospect directly asks "why you over [competitor]?"

## Inputs needed

**Required**
- Competitor type: traditional accounting firm / freelance accountant or bookkeeper / business-setup consultant offering accounting as an add-on / in-house hire / DIY accounting software
- Prospect profile: entity type (mainland / free zone), revenue band, industry, compliance status

**Optional**
- Specific competitor name (kept internal — never written into prospect-facing copy)
- What the prospect has said they like about the competitor
- Where in the sales process the deal sits
- The prospect's primary pain point and timeline
- Price comparison context (signal only — never a quote)

## Workflow

### Step 1 — Identify the competitor category
Match the situation to one of the five categories in `references/competitor-profiles.md`. If the prospect is comparing two categories (e.g. a freelancer and software), build the battlecard against the dominant threat.

### Step 2 — Map the competitor honestly
Pull the competitor's genuine strengths and weaknesses from the profile. Be honest — a battlecard that pretends the competitor has no strengths makes the rep sound unconvincing. Acknowledge what the competitor does well, then position around it.

### Step 3 — Identify where Finanshels genuinely wins
Anchor on real, defensible advantages: AI-native delivery model (faster closes, fewer errors), scale and reliability (7,000+ businesses, 150+ qualified accountants), proactive advisory rather than year-end-only compliance, UAE-based and GCC-focused, a full service stack under one partner versus a point solution. Pick the 2-3 wins that matter most for *this* prospect's pain.

### Step 4 — Build the proof points
For each win, attach an approved proof point or a verifiable UAE compliance fact. Never invent a client name, case study, or statistic. Trusted-by names (Abwaab, Silkhause, Dubai Future District Fund, Cotu Ventures) may be referenced as approved.

### Step 5 — Write landmine questions
Draft 3-5 questions the rep can ask the prospect that surface the competitor's weaknesses without naming or attacking them — e.g. "When your bookkeeper is on leave, who files your VAT return?" Questions should make the prospect discover the gap themselves.

### Step 6 — Define trap-avoidance (do / don't)
List what the rep should NOT say: never disparage the competitor by name, never overclaim, never promise a tax outcome, never quote a fee to undercut. List the do's: stay factual, lead with the prospect's risk, anchor to proof, advance to a next step.

### Step 7 — Assemble the one-page battlecard
Fill the output format. Keep it to one page — a battlecard the rep cannot scan in 60 seconds will not be used.

## Output format

```
COMPETITIVE BATTLECARD — vs [Competitor category]
Prospect: [Reference] | Entity: [Mainland / Free zone] | Rep: [Name] | Date: [Date]

COMPETITOR PROFILE
- Category: [Traditional firm / Freelancer / Setup consultant / In-house hire / DIY software]
- Typical pitch to the prospect: "[how they sell themselves]"
- Genuine strengths (acknowledge these): [...]
- Genuine weaknesses (position around these): [...]

WHERE FINANSHELS WINS (pick 2-3 for this prospect)
1. [Win] -> Proof: [proof point / compliance fact]
2. [Win] -> Proof: [...]
3. [Win] -> Proof: [...]

OUR COUNTER-NARRATIVE (one paragraph the rep can say)
[WHY this matters to the prospect -> WHAT Finanshels does -> proof -> next step]

LANDMINE QUESTIONS (ask these — let the prospect find the gap)
- "[question]"
- "[question]"
- "[question]"

DO / DON'T
DO:
- [stay factual / lead with prospect risk / anchor to proof / advance]
DON'T:
- [name or attack the competitor / overclaim / promise a tax outcome / quote a fee]

IF THE PROSPECT PUSHES BACK
- Likely objection: "[objection]" -> route to objection-handling-playbook

- [Concrete action] | Owner: [Name] | Date: [Date]
```

## Quality checklist

- [ ] Competitor mapped to a single category from competitor-profiles.md
- [ ] Competitor's genuine strengths acknowledged, not dismissed
- [ ] 2-3 Finanshels wins selected and tailored to this prospect's pain
- [ ] Every win backed by an approved proof point or verifiable FTA fact
- [ ] Counter-narrative follows WHY -> WHAT -> proof -> next step
- [ ] 3-5 landmine questions that surface gaps without naming the competitor
- [ ] Do/Don't section explicitly forbids disparagement and fee-quoting
- [ ] No competitor named in any prospect-facing copy
- [ ] No invented client names, case studies, testimonials, or statistics
- [ ] No tax outcome promised; all CT/VAT facts verified against FTA guidance
- [ ] Battlecard fits on one page and is scannable in 60 seconds
- [ ] A concrete next step with owner and date is included

## Examples

**Example 1** "Our prospect, a Dubai mainland trading company, says their business-setup consultant offered to do the accounting too as part of the licence renewal package. Build me a battlecard."

**Example 2** "A free zone tech company at AED 8M revenue is considering hiring an in-house accountant instead of outsourcing. How do I position against that?"

**Example 3** "The prospect told me they're happy with a freelance bookkeeper they found and just want to keep costs low. Give me a battlecard and landmine questions."

## Guardrails

- UAE jurisdiction only — never reference IRS, US states, UK/EU tax, or CPE.
- The battlecard is an internal sales document — competitor names stay internal and must never appear in prospect-facing copy or be disparaged by name.
- Outputs are professional work product — a Finanshels team member must review and own them before use in a deal.
- AI does not set or confirm pricing — never quote, undercut, or compare fees; route price questions to the account manager.
- Never invent Finanshels credentials, client names, case studies, testimonials, or numbers beyond the approved proof points (7,000+ businesses, 150+ qualified accountants; trusted by Abwaab, Silkhause, Dubai Future District Fund, Cotu Ventures).
- Never promise a tax outcome or guaranteed saving — describe the service, not a result.
- Treat all prospect data as confidential — never use real client identifiers in examples.
- Verify every CT/VAT rate, threshold, and deadline against current FTA guidance before using it as a competitive proof point.
