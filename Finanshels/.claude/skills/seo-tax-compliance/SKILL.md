# SEO — UAE Tax & Compliance Content

Researches UAE tax and accounting search terms and builds an actionable SEO content brief: a keyword map organised by intent and cluster, and a prioritised content roadmap so Finanshels earns organic visibility from the exact searches its ideal clients are running.

## When to use

- Building or refreshing the Finanshels blog/content SEO strategy
- Writing a new article and needing to know the right keywords and angle to target
- Identifying content gaps — topics clients search for that Finanshels doesn't yet cover
- Planning a new service page (e.g., CT registration, VAT advisory, bookkeeping for free zones)
- Quarterly content planning and you want keyword data to prioritise topics
- A regulatory change (new FTA decision, updated rates) opens a fresh keyword window

## Inputs needed

**Required**
- Focus area: choose one or more — Corporate Tax / VAT / Bookkeeping / Payroll / Free zone compliance / All

**Optional**
- Target audience: founders / finance leads / both
- Geographic modifier preference (e.g., "Dubai" vs "UAE" vs "Abu Dhabi")
- Competitor URLs or pages to analyse (if known)
- Stage of search funnel to prioritise: awareness / consideration / decision / all
- Current domain authority or content volume (helps calibrate difficulty targeting)
- Specific service page to optimise (e.g., "our VAT registration page")

## Workflow

1. **Pull the UAE keyword bank**
   Load `resources/tax-keywords.md` as the starting keyword universe. This is a curated, UAE-specific bank organised by cluster and intent — use it as the foundation before expanding.

2. **Expand by focus area**
   For each focus area requested, generate additional keyword variations:
   - Modifier variations: add "Dubai", "UAE", "Abu Dhabi", "free zone", "mainland", "SME", "startup", "2025", "2026"
   - Question forms: "how to…", "what is…", "do I need…", "when is…", "who has to…"
   - Long-tail variants: combine service + entity type + location (e.g., "VAT registration free zone company Dubai")
   - Comparison forms: "vs", "difference between", "which is better"
   - Urgency/problem forms: "deadline", "penalty", "missed", "late", "how to fix"

3. **Classify each keyword by search intent**
   - **Informational:** User wants to learn ("what is UAE corporate tax", "how does VAT work in UAE")
   - **Navigational:** User looking for a specific tool/page ("EmaraTax login", "FTA registration portal")
   - **Commercial investigation:** User comparing options ("best accounting firm Dubai", "UAE tax consultant vs in-house")
   - **Transactional:** User ready to act ("hire bookkeeper UAE", "VAT registration service Dubai", "corporate tax filing UAE")

4. **Score and prioritise**
   For each keyword, estimate:
   - **Relevance to Finanshels ICP** (High / Medium / Low): Does this search come from a UAE startup/SME founder or finance lead?
   - **Funnel stage** (Awareness / Consideration / Decision): Where in the buying journey is the searcher?
   - **Content type fit**: Blog article / Service page / FAQ / Landing page / LinkedIn post
   - **Competitive difficulty estimate** (Low / Medium / High): Generic UAE tax terms are competitive; long-tail, specific, free-zone or SME-focused terms tend to be lower competition
   - **Priority tier** (T1 = build now / T2 = next quarter / T3 = backlog)

5. **Build keyword clusters**
   Group related keywords into 6–10 clusters. Each cluster = one topic area that can support 1 anchor page + multiple supporting pieces. Example clusters:
   - UAE Corporate Tax basics (awareness)
   - CT registration process (consideration/transactional)
   - Free zone CT and QFZP status (consideration)
   - Small Business Relief (informational/consideration)
   - VAT registration UAE (consideration/transactional)
   - VAT return filing (informational/consideration)
   - Bookkeeping for UAE SMEs (consideration/transactional)
   - Accounting for free zones (consideration)
   - UAE year-end accounting and audit (consideration)
   - Outsourced CFO / management accounts (transactional)

6. **Build the content roadmap**
   For each T1 and T2 cluster, specify:
   - Primary keyword (the one to target as the page/article title focus)
   - Supporting keywords (2–5 secondary terms to include naturally)
   - Recommended content type and approximate length
   - Angle / hook (what makes the Finanshels take distinctive — not just a generic explainer)
   - Internal linking opportunities (existing or planned pages to link from/to)
   - CTA to include (what action should the reader take after this content)

7. **Identify quick wins**
   Flag 3–5 keywords where:
   - Search intent is high commercial/transactional
   - A service page does not yet exist (or is thin)
   - Competition appears low (long-tail, specific, geo-modified)
   These are the fastest path to organic leads.

## Quality checklist

- [ ] All keywords are UAE-specific — no generic international terms without a UAE modifier
- [ ] Every cluster has at least one T1 priority piece
- [ ] Quick wins section identifies at least 3 actionable targets
- [ ] Content roadmap includes a specific angle for each piece (not just a keyword + generic brief)
- [ ] No keyword targets a non-UAE jurisdiction (no IRS, HMRC, GST India references)
- [ ] Transactional/service keywords are mapped to service pages, not blog articles
- [ ] Each content brief includes a CTA aligned with the funnel stage
- [ ] FTA-specific terms (EmaraTax, TRN, QFZP) are included where relevant — these are real searches
- [ ] Keyword bank in `resources/tax-keywords.md` was used as starting point

## Guardrails

- Keyword research is a planning input — actual search volumes should be verified using live tools (Google Keyword Planner, Ahrefs, SEMrush, or similar) before committing to a roadmap
- Difficulty estimates in this skill are qualitative; validate with a real SEO tool
- Content produced from these briefs must be reviewed by a qualified Finanshels team member before publishing — tax facts must be accurate
- Do not include real client names or case studies in SEO briefs
- UAE jurisdiction only — do not target keywords that blend UAE and non-UAE tax systems without a clear disclaimer
