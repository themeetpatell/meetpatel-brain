# Performance Review Guide

Structures a fair, consistent, and evidence-based performance evaluation for every Finanshels team member using a competency rubric aligned to the firm's professional standards.

---

## When to use

- Quarterly or annual performance review cycle is open
- A probationary review (typically at 3 or 6 months) is due
- A manager asks for help structuring feedback for a team member
- A pip (performance improvement plan) needs a baseline evaluation
- A team member is being considered for promotion and needs a calibrated assessment

---

## Inputs needed

**Required**
- Employee name and job title
- Review period (e.g., Q1 2026, or "6-month probationary review ending 31 May 2026")
- Reviewing manager's name
- Role level: Junior / Mid-level / Senior / Lead

**Optional**
- Specific incidents or achievements to highlight (positive or constructive)
- Client feedback received during the period
- Self-assessment notes from the employee
- Goals set in the previous review cycle
- Any compliance or quality incidents during the period

---

## Workflow

### Step 1 — Confirm inputs and set context
Identify the role level. The rating scale and weight of each competency shifts by level:
- **Junior:** Technical accuracy and learning pace weighted highest
- **Mid-level:** Balanced across all five competencies
- **Senior:** Client service and compliance discipline weighted highest
- **Lead:** All five equally weighted, with additional leadership dimension

### Step 2 — Rate each competency
Use the five core Finanshels competencies. For each, assign a rating of 1–5 using the scale below, then write 2–4 sentences of evidence.

**Rating scale:**
| Score | Label | Meaning |
|-------|-------|---------|
| 5 | Exceptional | Consistently exceeds expectations; sets the standard for the team |
| 4 | Strong | Regularly meets and often exceeds expectations |
| 3 | Meets expectations | Reliably delivers to the required standard |
| 2 | Developing | Partially meets expectations; improvement needed with support |
| 1 | Below expectations | Does not yet meet minimum standard; urgent action required |

---

### Competency 1 — Technical Accuracy
*Does the team member produce work that is correct, well-supported, and compliant with UAE regulations?*

Evidence prompts:
- Bookkeeping: chart of accounts correct, reconciliations clean, IFRS/IFRS for SMEs applied correctly
- CT: taxable income computed correctly, registrations filed on time via EmaraTax, Small Business Relief eligibility assessed
- VAT: returns filed by the 28th, input tax recovery applied correctly, zero-rated/exempt correctly classified
- Transfer pricing: arm's-length principle applied, disclosure form submitted with CT return where required
- Error rate: how many corrections required post-delivery, any FTA penalty exposure created

### Competency 2 — Client Service
*Does the team member deliver a high-quality, consistent experience that reflects the Finanshels brand?*

Evidence prompts:
- Response time to client queries
- Proactivity: alerting clients to deadlines or risks before they ask
- Communication quality: clear, plain-English emails; no jargon without explanation
- Client satisfaction signals: direct feedback, renewals, referrals
- Handling difficult conversations: fee discussions, bad news, missed deadlines

### Competency 3 — Timeliness & Reliability
*Does the team member meet deadlines consistently without manager chasing?*

Evidence prompts:
- Percentage of deliverables submitted on time during the review period
- EmaraTax filing deadlines: any late submissions, and if so, were they flagged in advance?
- Internal deadlines: management reports, payroll runs, reconciliations
- Time management under high-volume periods (e.g., CT filing rush)

### Competency 4 — Compliance Discipline
*Does the team member apply compliance and quality controls correctly every time?*

Evidence prompts:
- AML/CFT: CDD completed for new clients, goAML filings made where required, sanctions screening current
- Record-keeping: 7-year retention rule observed, files organised to Finanshels standards
- Internal review process: work submitted for peer review before delivery, review comments acted on
- Escalation: compliance concerns raised promptly rather than buried
- Data confidentiality: client data handled according to firm policy, no incidents

### Competency 5 — Learning & Professional Growth
*Is the team member growing their knowledge and taking ownership of their development?*

Evidence prompts:
- Completion of internal UAE tax update training modules
- Self-initiated learning: FTA publications read, questions asked, knowledge shared with peers
- Applying new knowledge: e.g., proactively applying a new FTA clarification to client work
- Receptiveness to feedback: how quickly does the team member incorporate coaching?
- (For leads) Contribution to team knowledge: running training sessions, mentoring juniors

---

### Step 3 — Compute overall rating
Weight and average the five scores. Use the role-level weighting below:

| Competency | Junior | Mid | Senior | Lead |
|-----------|--------|-----|--------|------|
| Technical Accuracy | 35% | 25% | 25% | 20% |
| Client Service | 15% | 20% | 25% | 20% |
| Timeliness & Reliability | 25% | 20% | 15% | 20% |
| Compliance Discipline | 20% | 25% | 25% | 20% |
| Learning & Growth | 5% | 10% | 10% | 20% |

Weighted score → overall label:
- 4.5–5.0: Exceptional
- 3.5–4.4: Strong
- 2.5–3.4: Meets expectations
- 1.5–2.4: Developing
- 1.0–1.4: Below expectations

### Step 4 — Write the summary narrative
2–3 paragraphs:
1. Overall performance and standout achievement(s) during the period
2. Area(s) where the team member needs to improve and why it matters
3. Forward-looking statement: what success looks like in the next review period

### Step 5 — Set development actions
For each competency rated 1 or 2, and optionally for any rated 3, write one specific development action:
```
Competency: [Name]
Rating: [Score]
Action: [What the employee will do]
Support: [What the manager/firm will provide]
By: [Date]
```

### Step 6 — Populate the review template
Open `templates/review-template.md` and fill in every bracketed placeholder. The completed template is the document shared in the review conversation.

### Step 7 — Calibration check
Before finalising:
- Are ratings consistent with peers at the same level? (Cross-check with manager)
- Is the evidence specific (observable, dated) rather than vague ("good attitude")?
- Is any rating of 1 or 5 supported by at least three evidence points?
- Have compliance incidents been reflected accurately (no downplaying)?

---

## Quality checklist

- [ ] All five competencies rated with a score 1–5 and narrative evidence
- [ ] Evidence is specific — dates, deliverables, observable behaviours, not adjectives
- [ ] Role-level weightings applied correctly; weighted average computed
- [ ] Any rating of 1 has at least three evidence points and a development action
- [ ] No compliance incidents have been omitted or minimised
- [ ] Summary narrative is balanced: achievement + improvement + forward look
- [ ] Development actions are specific and time-bound
- [ ] Review template fully populated before the employee conversation

## Examples

- "Review Ahmed Al-Rashidi, mid-level accountant, Q1 2026. He's strong technically but clients have complained about slow responses. No compliance incidents."
- "6-month probationary review for our new tax associate Priya. She joined with no VAT background, has completed all training modules, and just filed her first return independently."
- "Annual review for our senior client manager. She's been with us 3 years, manages our top 10 clients, and we're thinking about promoting her to Lead."

## Guardrails

- UAE jurisdiction only. No reference to US performance review norms, IRS CPE, or non-UAE labour law.
- This review document is a management tool and must be reviewed by the hiring manager and, where applicable, HR before being shared with the employee.
- All performance data (ratings, incidents, compensation context) is confidential employee information and must be stored and shared according to Finanshels' data policy.
- UAE labour law obligations — including notice periods, end-of-service gratuity, and termination procedures — are governed by Federal Decree-Law No. 33 of 2021. Any employment decisions arising from this review must be reviewed by a qualified HR or legal professional before action is taken.
- A rating of 1 (Below expectations) that may lead to a performance improvement plan or termination requires HR involvement. Do not take unilateral action based on this document alone.
