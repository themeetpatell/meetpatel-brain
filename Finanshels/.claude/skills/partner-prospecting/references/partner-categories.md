# UAE Partner Categories — Overlap Map

> Reference for the `partner-prospecting` skill. Maps each UAE partner category
> to why their client base overlaps with Finanshels' ICP, the natural referral
> trigger, typical conflict risk, and fit notes. **Do not invent partner names.**

Finanshels' ICP: UAE-incorporated startups & SMEs, AED 0–50M revenue,
mainland (DED) and free zone entities, founders/finance leads.

---

## 1. Free Zone Authorities
- **Overlap:** Every entity they license needs bookkeeping, CT registration, and often VAT — directly ICP.
- **Referral trigger:** At licence issuance or renewal; QFZP clients need audited financials.
- **Model fit:** Co-marketing, embedded referral in onboarding flow, preferred-partner listing.
- **Conflict risk:** Low — authorities do not provide accounting; some have in-house "business services" desks.
- **Notes:** High strategic value; long sales cycle; relationship is institutional, not individual.

## 2. Company-Formation / Business-Setup Consultants
- **Overlap:** Very high — they incorporate exactly Finanshels' ICP and the client needs accounting immediately after.
- **Referral trigger:** Right after trade licence is issued — the client has no finance function yet.
- **Model fit:** Referral or white-label / embedded ("we set you up, our finance partner runs your books").
- **Conflict risk:** Caution — many setup firms bundle their own basic bookkeeping; check for in-house capability.
- **Notes:** Highest-volume category; vet for quality so referrals reflect well on Finanshels.

## 3. Law Firms
- **Overlap:** Medium-high — corporate, commercial, and M&A practices serve SMEs needing CT/VAT/UBO compliance.
- **Referral trigger:** Company formation, restructuring, fundraising, transactions, regulatory filings.
- **Model fit:** Reciprocal referral; co-hosted advisory content.
- **Conflict risk:** Low — law firms do not do bookkeeping; some advise on tax (coordinate, do not compete).
- **Notes:** Lower volume, higher-quality referrals; trust-based and relationship-led.

## 4. Banks & Neobanks
- **Overlap:** High — SME current-account holders are squarely ICP; neobanks skew to early-stage startups.
- **Referral trigger:** Account opening; loan/credit applications requiring financial statements.
- **Model fit:** Embedded referral in onboarding, co-marketing, marketplace listing.
- **Conflict risk:** Low — banks do not provide accounting; some have bookkeeping marketplace partners already.
- **Notes:** Strategic if integration is possible; procurement and compliance cycles are long.

## 5. VC Funds & Accelerators
- **Overlap:** High for the portfolio — funded startups need clean books, CT/VAT setup, and CFO support to scale.
- **Referral trigger:** Post-investment; due-diligence readiness; board-reporting requirements.
- **Model fit:** Portfolio-wide preferred partner, co-marketing, founder-education sessions.
- **Conflict risk:** Low — funds want portfolio companies financially clean; aligns with Finanshels.
- **Notes:** One fund relationship can unlock many ICP clients; outsourced CFO is a strong hook here.

## 6. Fintech / SaaS Platforms (POS, payroll, e-commerce, spend)
- **Overlap:** High — their SME users generate transactions that need bookkeeping, VAT, and reconciliation.
- **Referral trigger:** When the user's transaction volume outgrows DIY accounting.
- **Model fit:** Integration partnership, embedded referral, co-marketing, app-marketplace listing.
- **Conflict risk:** Caution — some platforms offer light bookkeeping features; position Finanshels as the human layer.
- **Notes:** Data integration adds real value; scope technical effort before committing.

## 7. Audit Firms Needing Accounting Overflow
- **Overlap:** High — audit clients frequently lack audit-ready books; audit firms cannot always do the bookkeeping.
- **Referral trigger:** When an audit reveals weak or incomplete records.
- **Model fit:** Referral / white-label accounting overflow.
- **Conflict risk:** Caution — independence rules: an audit firm cannot audit books Finanshels keeps for the same client; keep referral one-directional and documented.
- **Notes:** Steady, qualified referrals; respect auditor-independence boundaries strictly.

## Scoring shortcuts
| Signal | High | Medium | Low |
|--------|------|--------|-----|
| Client overlap | Most clients are AED 0–50M UAE SMEs | A meaningful segment is | Few are |
| Referral volume | >40/yr | 10–40/yr | <10/yr |
| Conflict | Clear — no competing service | Caution — partial in-house capability | Conflict — direct competitor or exclusive incumbent |

> Always confirm a partner's actual client mix before scoring — categories are a
> starting hypothesis, not a verdict.
