# Discovery Question Bank — UAE SME Prospects

> Reference for the `discovery-call-guide` skill. Six categories of discovery
> questions with UAE-tax-specific probes. Pick 2-3 per category and tailor to
> the prospect. Ask open, then go one level deeper on anything compliance-relevant.

---

## 1. Situation — understand the business

- "Tell me about the business — what do you do and who do you sell to?"
- "Is the company mainland or free zone? Which authority issued the licence?"
- "When did you incorporate, and what's your financial year-end?"
- "Roughly what revenue range are you in this year, and how is that trending?"
- "How many people are on the team, and do you run payroll in the UAE?"
- "Do you have any other entities — a holding company, a branch, an overseas parent?"

**Why it matters:** entity type and financial-year-end drive the CT return deadline
(9 months after year-end) and whether QFZP rules or Small Business Relief apply.

---

## 2. Pain — surface the real problem

- "What made you reach out to us now, specifically?"
- "Who handles the books today — in-house, a freelancer, a firm, or nobody yet?"
- "What's not working about the current setup?"
- "When was the last time you saw a clean set of financials for the business?"
- "How much of your week goes into finance admin or chasing your accountant?"
- "Has anything gone wrong — a missed filing, a penalty, an auditor query?"

**Why it matters:** the stated enquiry is rarely the full pain. Dissatisfaction with
an incumbent or a recent mistake is the strongest qualifier.

---

## 3. Compliance status — map the gaps

- "Are you registered for Corporate Tax on EmaraTax? Do you know your deadline?"
- "Have you filed, or do you still need to file, your first CT return?"
- "Are you VAT registered? Are your taxable supplies above AED 375,000?"
- "If free zone — have you assessed whether you qualify as a QFZP?"
- "Is your revenue under AED 3M? You may be eligible for Small Business Relief."
- "Are your books on an accounting system, or in spreadsheets?"
- "Do you need audited financial statements — for a bank, investor, or because revenue is above AED 50M?"
- "Are you up to date on UBO and AML obligations for your structure?"

**Why it matters:** this is the section that defines the service bundle. Probe gently —
many founders don't know their status. Confirm figures against FTA guidance later.

---

## 4. Timeline — establish urgency

- "Is there a deadline driving this — a CT filing, a VAT return, an audit?"
- "When would you ideally want this handled by?"
- "Is there an event coming up — fundraise, bank facility, board meeting — that needs clean numbers?"
- "If we don't solve this in the next quarter, what happens?"

**Why it matters:** a real deadline (CT return due, VAT penalty, investor diligence)
moves a deal from "someday" to "now".

---

## 5. Decision process — find the path to yes

- "Besides yourself, who else would be involved in choosing a finance partner?"
- "How do decisions like this usually get made here?"
- "Have you looked at other options, or talked to other firms?"
- "What would you need to see from us to feel confident moving ahead?"
- "If we're a fit, what would the timeline to get started look like on your side?"

**Why it matters:** identifies whether the founder is the decision-maker and what
proof or process is needed to close.

---

## 6. Budget signals — read intent, never quote

- "Have you set aside a budget for getting finance handled, or is this exploratory?"
- "What are you spending today on accounting or finance admin?"
- "Are you comparing us on price, or on getting it done properly?"
- "Would a monthly retainer or a one-off project suit you better?"

**Why it matters:** reads price-sensitivity and intent. NEVER quote a fee on a
discovery call — capture the signal and route scoping to `engagement-budget-calculator`.

---

## Buying-signal cues — tag what you hear

| Signal | What it means |
|--------|---------------|
| "Deadline", "penalty", "auditor asked", "EmaraTax notice" | Real urgency — prioritise |
| Negative language about current accountant | Incumbent is vulnerable |
| Founder personally on the call and engaged | Decision-maker access — strong |
| "What would this cost?" / "Send me a proposal" | Explicit buying intent |
| Multiple service lines surfacing in one call | High bundle potential |
| "We'll deal with it later" / "just getting quotes" | Low urgency — nurture |
| No UAE compliance obligation at all | Likely not a fit — qualify out |

---

## De-escalation phrases for an anxious founder

Founders often feel behind or worried. Stay calm and confident:

- "This is very common — a lot of UAE businesses are still catching up on CT. It's fixable."
- "Let's first get clear on where you actually stand, then we'll know the real size of it."
- "You don't need to have this figured out — that's literally what we do for 7,000+ businesses."

Never amplify fear. Finanshels is helpful and reassuring, never alarmist.
