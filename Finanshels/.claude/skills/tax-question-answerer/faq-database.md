## Corporate Tax (CT)

### FAQ-CT-01: Do I need to register for Corporate Tax?

**Short answer:** Yes — registration is mandatory for all taxable persons (companies and natural persons with qualifying business income) regardless of whether tax is actually owed.

**Full answer:**
Under Federal Decree-Law No. 47 of 2022, every UAE company and every natural person with business turnover exceeding AED 1 million per calendar year must register for Corporate Tax on EmaraTax. The FTA assigns registration deadlines based on the month your trade licence was issued — missing the deadline triggers penalties.

Registration does not mean you will owe tax. A company with revenue below AED 3M may elect Small Business Relief (for tax periods ending on or before 31 December 2026), which treats the entity as having no taxable income. But registration is still required.

**Free zone note:** Free zone entities must also register, even if they qualify for the 0% QFZP rate. The registration process confirms their eligibility, which must be assessed annually.

**Legal basis:** Federal Decree-Law No. 47 of 2022; FTA public circulars on registration deadlines.

---

### FAQ-CT-02: What are the Corporate Tax rates?

**Short answer:** 0% on taxable income up to AED 375,000, then 9% on the amount above that.

**Full answer:**
UAE Corporate Tax is structured as a two-tier rate:
- **0%** on the first AED 375,000 of taxable income per tax period
- **9%** on taxable income above AED 375,000

For multinationals that are part of groups with consolidated global revenue of EUR 750 million or more, a **15% Domestic Minimum Top-up Tax (DMTT)** applies for financial years starting on or after 1 January 2025, in line with the OECD Pillar Two framework.

Most Finanshels clients — startups and SMEs — fall in the 0%/9% band. A company with AED 600,000 taxable income pays 9% on AED 225,000 (the portion above the AED 375,000 threshold) = AED 20,250.

**Legal basis:** Federal Decree-Law No. 47 of 2022, Article 3; Cabinet Decision for DMTT.

---

### FAQ-CT-03: What is Small Business Relief and do I qualify?

**Short answer:** If your revenue is AED 3 million or less in a tax period, you can elect Small Business Relief — which means you're treated as having zero taxable income for that period.

**Full answer:**
Small Business Relief is a simplified option for small taxpayers. If a resident taxable person's revenue for a tax period does not exceed AED 3 million, they can elect SBR and effectively pay no Corporate Tax for that period. This election is available for tax periods ending on or before 31 December 2026.

Important: You still must register for CT and file a return — you simply elect SBR on the return to reduce your liability to zero.

Businesses that are part of a multinational group, or that choose to use a tax loss or benefit from a specific CT provision (like free zone 0% treatment), may not be able to claim SBR — check with your Finanshels manager.

**Legal basis:** Ministerial Decision No. 73 of 2023 (Small Business Relief).

---

### FAQ-CT-04: When is the Corporate Tax return due?

**Short answer:** The CT return (and any tax payment) is due within 9 months of the end of your financial year (tax period).

**Full answer:**
For a company with a 31 December financial year-end, the CT return and payment are due by 30 September of the following year. For a 31 March year-end, the due date is 31 December. The 9-month rule applies consistently regardless of the financial year-end date.

The return is filed via EmaraTax. If tax is owed, payment is also due by the same deadline. Late filing and late payment both attract FTA penalties — verify the current penalty schedule on the FTA website as amounts can change.

**Legal basis:** Federal Decree-Law No. 47 of 2022, Article 53.

---

### FAQ-CT-05: I'm a free zone company — do I pay Corporate Tax?

**Short answer:** Potentially 0% — but only if you qualify as a Qualifying Free Zone Person (QFZP) and only on qualifying income. Non-qualifying income is taxed at 9%.

**Full answer:**
A free zone entity can achieve 0% CT on its qualifying income if it meets all of the QFZP conditions:
1. Maintains adequate economic substance in the UAE
2. Derives qualifying income (defined by the CT Law and Cabinet Decisions)
3. Meets the de minimis test: non-qualifying income must not exceed 5% of total revenue or AED 5 million, whichever is lower
4. Prepares audited financial statements
5. Complies with transfer pricing rules

If the entity fails any condition, it loses the 0% treatment for that entire tax period and is taxed at the standard 9% rate on all income above AED 375,000.

QFZP status must be assessed annually — it is not a one-time determination. Finanshels can model QFZP eligibility as part of CT advisory.

**Legal basis:** Federal Decree-Law No. 47 of 2022, Article 18; Ministerial Decision No. 139 of 2023 (qualifying income); Ministerial Decision No. 265 of 2023 (de minimis).

## VAT

### FAQ-VAT-01: When do I need to register for VAT?

**Short answer:** When your taxable supplies exceed AED 375,000 in the past 12 months or are expected to exceed AED 375,000 in the next 30 days. Below AED 187,500 you cannot register voluntarily.

**Full answer:**
VAT registration thresholds in the UAE:
- **Mandatory registration:** Taxable supplies + imports exceed **AED 375,000** in the trailing 12 months, or you reasonably expect them to exceed AED 375,000 in the next 30 days
- **Voluntary registration:** Taxable supplies or taxable expenses exceed **AED 187,500** per year
- **Below AED 187,500:** Cannot register for VAT at all

"Taxable supplies" includes standard-rated (5%) and zero-rated supplies. VAT-exempt supplies do not count toward the registration threshold.

Once the threshold is crossed, you must apply for VAT registration on EmaraTax within 30 days. Retroactive registration with penalties is possible if missed.

**Legal basis:** Federal Decree-Law No. 8 of 2017, Article 17; Executive Regulation.

---

### FAQ-VAT-02: What is the difference between zero-rated and exempt VAT?

**Short answer:** Zero-rated means VAT is charged at 0% but you can still reclaim input VAT on your costs. Exempt means no VAT is charged and you cannot reclaim related input VAT.

**Full answer:**
This distinction matters significantly for cash flow and compliance:

**Zero-rated (0% VAT, input VAT recoverable):**
- Exports of goods outside the UAE
- International transport services
- Certain food items, healthcare, and educational services (conditions apply)
- Supply of certain investment metals

**Exempt (no VAT, input VAT NOT recoverable):**
- Certain financial services (interest, some fees)
- Bare land transactions
- Residential property (subsequent supply)
- Local passenger transport

A business making mostly exempt supplies that also has taxable overhead will face a partial input VAT recovery calculation — Finanshels can model this for you.

**Legal basis:** Federal Decree-Law No. 8 of 2017, Schedule 1 (exempt) and Schedule 3 (zero-rated); Executive Regulation.

---

### FAQ-VAT-03: When is the VAT return due?

**Short answer:** The 28th of the month following the end of your VAT return period. Quarterly filers due on 28 January, 28 April, 28 July, 28 October for calendar-quarter periods.

**Full answer:**
VAT returns are filed via EmaraTax by the 28th of the month following the VAT return period. Most Finanshels clients file quarterly (calendar quarters), making their due dates:
- Q1 (Jan–Mar): due 28 April
- Q2 (Apr–Jun): due 28 July
- Q3 (Jul–Sep): due 28 October
- Q4 (Oct–Dec): due 28 January

Some businesses are required to file monthly (typically higher-revenue businesses or by FTA direction). Check your EmaraTax profile to confirm your filing frequency.

Both the return and any VAT payment are due by this date. Late filing and late payment attract FTA penalties. If you have a VAT refund position, filing on time also starts the FTA's refund processing clock.

**Legal basis:** Federal Decree-Law No. 8 of 2017, Article 64.

---

### FAQ-VAT-04: Can I reclaim the VAT I paid on business expenses?

**Short answer:** Yes — VAT you pay on expenses used for taxable business activities is recoverable as input VAT, reducing what you owe the FTA.

**Full answer:**
Input VAT recovery is a core feature of the UAE VAT system. If you buy something for your taxable business and pay 5% VAT on it, you can deduct that VAT from the VAT you collected from customers. Only the net (output VAT minus input VAT) is remitted to the FTA.

Key conditions for input VAT recovery:
- The expense must be used for taxable (standard-rated or zero-rated) activities
- You must hold a valid tax invoice from the supplier
- The supplier must be VAT-registered
- VAT on entertainment for non-employees and on personal motor vehicles is generally blocked

If your business makes a mix of taxable and exempt supplies, you can only recover input VAT in proportion to taxable use — Finanshels can calculate your partial exemption recovery rate.

**Legal basis:** Federal Decree-Law No. 8 of 2017, Articles 53–57.

---

### FAQ-VAT-05: Do I charge VAT on services I export to customers outside the UAE?

**Short answer:** Generally no — exported services are zero-rated, meaning you charge 0% VAT. But the "place of supply" rules are specific and some cross-border services can still be UAE-standard-rated.

**Full answer:**
Under UAE VAT, the supply of services is VAT-able where the "place of supply" is the UAE. For B2B services where the customer is outside the UAE and uses the service outside the UAE, the supply is typically zero-rated (exported services). This means no 5% VAT is charged to the foreign customer, but you can still recover input VAT on your costs.

However, this gets complicated quickly:
- If the foreign customer is actually "in" the UAE at the time of supply (e.g. attending a UAE-based event), it may be standard-rated
- Certain services have special place-of-supply rules (telecom, electronic services, property-related)
- E-services supplied to UAE end consumers by non-UAE businesses can have different rules

Finanshels recommends documenting the customer's location, the nature of the service, and where it is consumed. Our team can confirm the correct VAT treatment for your specific service type.

**Legal basis:** Federal Decree-Law No. 8 of 2017, Articles 29–34 (place of supply); FTA VAT Public Clarifications.

## EmaraTax & Registration

### FAQ-EMARA-01: What is EmaraTax and how do I use it?

**Short answer:** EmaraTax is the FTA's online portal where all UAE tax registrations, VAT returns, CT returns, and payments happen. As your tax agent, Finanshels accesses it on your behalf — but you must first approve our access.

**Full answer:**
EmaraTax (emara.tax) replaced the old FTA e-Services portal. It is the single portal for:
- VAT registration, returns, and payments
- Corporate Tax registration, returns, and payments
- Excise tax (if applicable)
- Refund applications
- Correspondence with the FTA

As a Finanshels client, you don't need to navigate EmaraTax yourself for day-to-day filings — we do that as your registered tax agent. However, your role is to approve our agent access request in the portal, which requires you to log in to EmaraTax with your UAE Pass or OTP at least once.

You should also keep your EmaraTax profile updated with current trade licence details, contact information, and bank account (for refunds). Outdated information causes filing errors and delays.

---

### FAQ-EMARA-02: How long do I have to register for Corporate Tax after incorporating?

**Short answer:** The FTA assigns a deadline based on the month your trade licence was issued. Deadlines vary — missing them triggers penalties.

**Full answer:**
CT registration deadlines were set by the FTA via a phased schedule tied to licence issuance month. For most businesses, the deadline fell within the first few months of the CT regime starting (June 2023). If you are newly incorporated or have not yet registered, your deadline may have already passed — Finanshels will check your specific deadline and handle registration immediately to minimize penalty exposure.

Do not delay if you are unregistered: the FTA imposes administrative penalties for late registration, and these cannot be waived simply because the business had no taxable income.

**Legal basis:** FTA Decision on CT registration timelines; Federal Decree-Law No. 47 of 2022.

## Record Keeping

### FAQ-REC-01: How long must I keep accounting and tax records?

**Short answer:** 7 years for Corporate Tax records. 5 years for VAT records. Keep both for 7 years to cover all obligations.

**Full answer:**
UAE record-keeping requirements:
- **Corporate Tax:** All accounting records, financial statements, and supporting documents must be retained for **7 years** from the end of the relevant tax period
- **VAT:** VAT records, tax invoices, import/export documents must be retained for **5 years** from the end of the relevant tax period (real estate transactions: 15 years)

In practice, Finanshels recommends keeping all records for 7 years to cover both CT and VAT requirements in one policy. Records must be accessible to the FTA on request — store them securely (encrypted cloud or physical secure storage) and do not delete without confirming the retention period has passed.

Digital records are acceptable provided they are complete, unaltered, and retrievable.

**Legal basis:** Federal Decree-Law No. 47 of 2022, Article 56 (CT); Federal Decree-Law No. 8 of 2017, Article 78 (VAT).

## AML/CFT & Compliance

### FAQ-AML-01: Why does Finanshels ask for my passport and shareholder documents?

**Short answer:** We are legally required to — Finanshels is classified as a Designated Non-Financial Business and Profession (DNFBP) under UAE AML law, and Customer Due Diligence (CDD) is mandatory before we begin any work.

**Full answer:**
UAE AML/CFT law (Federal Decree-Law No. 20 of 2018 and its executive regulations) requires DNFBPs — which include accounting and bookkeeping firms — to carry out Customer Due Diligence on all clients. This means:
- Identifying and verifying the identity of the client entity
- Identifying and verifying all ultimate beneficial owners (anyone owning or controlling ≥ 25%)
- Assessing the risk of the business relationship
- Screening against UAE and international sanctions lists

This is a legal obligation on Finanshels, not a discretionary policy. We cannot begin work — even for the most straightforward engagement — until CDD is complete. The information you provide is stored securely and only used for compliance purposes.

**Legal basis:** Federal Decree-Law No. 20 of 2018 (AML/CFT); Cabinet Decision No. 10 of 2019; CBUAE AML/CFT Supervisory Guidance.

### FAQ-UBO-01: What is a UBO and do I need to file one?

**Short answer:** A UBO (Ultimate Beneficial Owner) is any individual who ultimately owns or controls 25% or more of your company. UAE companies must maintain a UBO register and file it with their registrar.

**Full answer:**
Under Cabinet Resolution No. 58 of 2020, all UAE companies (mainland and free zone) must:
1. Maintain a UBO register identifying every individual who holds ≥ 25% shares, voting rights, or control rights
2. Maintain a shareholder/partner register
3. File these registers with the relevant licensing authority / registrar

The UBO register must be updated whenever ownership changes and kept accurate at all times. Certain free zones have their own specific filing procedures — Finanshels can confirm the requirement for your specific free zone.

Failure to maintain and file accurate UBO information is a compliance violation and may attract penalties. This is separate from, but related to, the AML/CFT CDD obligation above.

**Legal basis:** Cabinet Resolution No. 58 of 2020 on Beneficial Ownership Procedures.
