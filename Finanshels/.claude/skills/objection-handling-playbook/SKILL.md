# Objection Handling Playbook

Helps the Finanshels sales team turn a UAE SME prospect's objection into a productive next step using a consistent Acknowledge -> Reframe -> Evidence -> Advance method.

## When to use

- A prospect raises a concern live on a call and you need a response in the moment
- A prospect's reply to a proposal or follow-up contains a stall or pushback
- Preparing for a call with a prospect who pushed back last time
- Coaching a BD hire on how to handle a recurring objection
- A deal has gone quiet and you suspect an unspoken objection is the cause
- Reviewing a lost-deal note to learn what objection was not handled well

## Inputs needed

**Required**
- The objection — verbatim quote if available, or the category (price, incumbent, timing, trust, size, DIY, free-zone-zero-tax)
- Where in the sales process it was raised (discovery call, post-proposal, follow-up)

**Optional**
- Prospect profile: entity type, revenue band, industry, compliance status
- What has been discussed or sent so far
- The prospect's stated timeline or deadline
- Whether the objection is from the decision-maker or a gatekeeper
- Any rapport or relationship context

## Workflow

### Step 1 — Capture the objection verbatim
Write the objection in the prospect's exact words where possible. Wording carries the real concern — "it's expensive" differs from "I can't justify that to my partner".

### Step 2 — Classify the objection
Match it to a category in `references/objection-library.md`: Price, Incumbent ("we already have an accountant"), DIY/PRO ("my cousin/PRO does my books"), Timing ("we'll deal with CT later"), Size ("we're too small"), Trust ("I don't trust outsourcing finances"), or Free-zone myth ("free zone so we pay 0% anyway"). If it spans two, pick the dominant one.

### Step 3 — Diagnose the real concern
The stated objection is rarely the true blocker. Use the library's "underlying concern" column and the diagnostic question to test it. Common hidden concerns: budget approval not secured, fear of switching pain, doesn't see urgency, doesn't trust the outcome, doesn't believe compliance applies to them.

### Step 4 — Build the response: Acknowledge -> Reframe -> Evidence -> Advance
- **Acknowledge:** validate the concern genuinely — never dismiss it.
- **Reframe:** shift from cost/effort to risk/value — what is the cost of *not* acting (FTA penalties, founder time, messy books at audit).
- **Evidence:** anchor in a real Finanshels proof point (7,000+ businesses served, 150+ qualified accountants, AI-native delivery, UAE-based) or a verifiable UAE compliance fact (CT 9% above AED 375K, return due 9 months after year-end, VAT penalties, 7-year record-keeping).
- **Advance:** propose one small, low-risk next step — not "so will you sign?" but "let's do a 20-minute compliance health check".

### Step 5 — Produce 2-3 tailored response options
Give the rep choices for different styles and prospect temperaments: a direct version, a consultative/question-led version, and (where useful) a story/peer-proof version. Keep each short enough to actually say out loud.

### Step 6 — Define the follow-up action
Every objection response ends with a concrete next action and owner: send a one-pager, book the health check, share a deadline calculator, loop in the decision-maker. If the objection reveals the prospect is genuinely not a fit, say so and route to a graceful exit.

## Quality checklist

- [ ] Objection captured verbatim, not paraphrased away
- [ ] Classified to a single dominant category
- [ ] Underlying concern named — not just the surface objection
- [ ] A diagnostic question is included to confirm the real concern
- [ ] Every response option follows Acknowledge -> Reframe -> Evidence -> Advance
- [ ] At least one approved proof point or verifiable UAE compliance fact per option
- [ ] No pricing committed — fee discussion routed to the account manager
- [ ] The "Advance" step is a small, low-risk action, not a hard close
- [ ] Follow-up action has an owner and a date
- [ ] Tone is confident and helpful, never alarmist or pushy
- [ ] No invented client names, testimonials, or numbers used
- [ ] Genuine non-fit objections are routed to a graceful exit, not forced

## Examples

**Example 1** "A prospect said 'honestly, you're more expensive than the freelancer doing our books now.' How do I respond?"

**Example 2** "A free zone SaaS founder told me 'we're in a free zone so we pay 0% corporate tax anyway — we don't really need help.' What do I say?"

**Example 3** "After we sent the proposal, the prospect replied 'we'll deal with corporate tax later, it's not a priority right now.' Build me a response and a follow-up."

## Guardrails

- UAE jurisdiction only — never reference IRS, US states, UK/EU tax, or CPE.
- Objection responses are professional work product — a Finanshels team member must review and own them before they reach a prospect.
- AI does not set or confirm pricing — never quote, discount, or commit a fee; route all price questions to the account manager.
- Never invent Finanshels credentials, client names, case studies, testimonials, or numbers beyond the approved proof points (7,000+ businesses, 150+ qualified accountants).
- Never overstate FTA penalties or use fear as the primary lever — reframe to value and verifiable facts, stay reassuring.
- Treat all prospect data as confidential — never use real client identifiers in examples.
- Verify every CT/VAT rate, threshold, and deadline against current FTA guidance before citing it as evidence.
- If a prospect is genuinely not a fit, do not manufacture an objection rebuttal — recommend a graceful exit.
