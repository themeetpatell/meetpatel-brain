# Brand & Content — Map of Content

> The personal brand engine. Where ideas become posts become reputation. This MOC is the surface for content workflow + brand strategy.

## The brand thesis

Meet's public brand exists for three reasons:
1. **Top-of-funnel for all ventures** — Finanshels, StartupOS, Biggdate prospects find Meet before finding the products
2. **Investor signal** — public thinking is the cheapest demonstration of strategic depth
3. **Recruiting signal** — operators who admire the writing want to work with the writer

The brand is **founder-operator energy with a sharp voice, regulated-industry credibility, and AI-native thesis**. Not coach. Not influencer. Not generic startup advisor.

## Cadence

- **Posting target:** 3 LinkedIn posts/week (Mon, Wed, Fri default)
- **Newsletter:** monthly long-form
- **X:** light, opportunistic
- **Threads:** when an idea genuinely needs steps
- **Essays:** quarterly minimum

## Workflow (idea → published)

1. **Capture** — observation lands in `00 Inbox/` or directly into `60 Outputs/Content/_Ideas.md`
2. **Develop** — pick the format (post / thread / essay), pick the prompt, run `_AI/Agents/content-engine.md`
3. **Polish** — Meet's own edit pass against voice.md
4. **Schedule** — into LinkedIn scheduler (Hypefury / Taplio / native)
5. **Track** — log to `60 Outputs/Content/YYYY-MM/` with engagement data
6. **Recycle** — high-performing posts → newsletter / thread / essay seed

## Brand health metrics (monthly)

- Posting frequency vs target (target: 12 LinkedIn/month)
- Engagement quality (DMs received that lead to conversations, not just likes)
- Lead attribution (how many ventures-relevant conversations started from a post)
- Voice consistency (does the post sound like you, or like a generic founder)

## Standing reviews

- **Weekly:** in the Friday review, ask "what was worth posting this week?"
- **Monthly:** review the month's posts — which performed, what's the pattern
- **Quarterly:** review pillars — are they still right? Drift detection.
